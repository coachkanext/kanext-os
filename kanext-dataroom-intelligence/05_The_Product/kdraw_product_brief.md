# KDraw - Play Diagramming and Playbook Intelligence

## What KDraw Is

KDraw is the play diagramming and playbook management tool inside KaNeXT OS. It lives in Dashboard > Scouting in Athletics mode and in KPlay as a coaching tool. Coaches draw, animate, organize, and share plays and drills from their phone, tablet, or desktop. Every play created is immediately available to Dipson, the Scouting tab, the Film Room, KPlay, and every other component of the platform.

KDraw replaces FastDraw (FastModel Sports, now owned by Hudl), The Hoops Geek Play Creator, FastBreak PlayBook, and physical coaching whiteboards. It launches with basketball and expands to football, soccer, hockey, volleyball, and lacrosse.

## The Problem KDraw Solves

Play diagramming is one of the most universal coaching activities in sports. Every basketball coach in America diagrams plays - on whiteboards, napkins, iPads, or dedicated software. FastDraw (now Hudl's FastDraw) has been the industry standard for two decades, used by nearly every NBA and NCAA team. But FastDraw and every other diagramming tool share the same fundamental limitation: plays are isolated files.

A coach draws a play in FastDraw. That play lives in FastDraw. It does not connect to the scouting report. It does not connect to the game film where that play was actually executed. It does not connect to the simulation engine that projects outcomes. It does not connect to the player development plan. It does not connect to the practice plan. It does not connect to the playbook the players study on their phones. It does not connect to the intelligence system that knows which plays work against which defensive systems.

The coach draws the play in one app, writes the scouting report in another app, watches the film in a third app, builds the practice plan in a fourth app, sends the playbook to players through a fifth app, and tracks execution data in a sixth app. Six tools, zero integration, zero intelligence.

KDraw solves this by making every play a shared data object inside the KaNeXT OS. Draw a play, and Dipson can reference it by name in a scouting report. Tag a film possession, and KDraw shows which play was called. Assign a play to a player in KPlay, and the player studies it with step-by-step animation on their phone. Run a simulation against an upcoming opponent, and KDraw's plays are the offensive options tested. Build a practice plan in Calendar, and KDraw's plays are the drills attached. The play is not a file. It is a connected intelligence object.

## Core Features

**Drawing Engine.** Full court and half court diagrams. Offensive and defensive player markers (numbered circles, customizable). Drag-and-drop player positioning. Movement lines for cuts (solid arrows), screens (X marks), dribble paths (wavy arrows), passes (dashed lines), and shooting zones. Court templates sized by competition level and court dimensions. Color customization for visual clarity.

**Animation System.** Step through play execution frame by frame. Each frame represents a moment in the play's progression. FastBuild-style auto-advance moves players to their next position. Play back the full animation at adjustable speed. The animation is what transforms a static diagram into a teaching tool - players see the play unfold rather than interpreting a cluttered diagram.

**Playbook Organization.** Plays organized by category: offense (sets, quick hitters, motion reads, continuity), defense (man principles, zone alignments, press schemes, trap triggers), special situations (BLOB/baseline out of bounds, SLOB/sideline out of bounds, ATO/after timeout, press break, last shot), and drills (individual, small group, team). Search by name, tag, category, or situation. Playbook export as PDF for printing or as shareable digital playbook.

**Sharing and Distribution.** Share individual plays or entire playbooks through Messages to coaching staff or players. Players receive plays in their KPlay view as required study material with step-by-step animation. Export plays as images (for social media, presentations, or print) or as animated GIFs. Embed plays in scouting reports generated by Dipson.

## Integration Points - The Competitive Moat

The integration between KDraw and the rest of the KaNeXT OS is the competitive advantage that no standalone diagramming tool can replicate. These are not theoretical integrations. They are architectural connections built into the platform.

**KDraw to Dipson.** When Dipson generates a scouting report for an upcoming opponent, it can reference specific plays from the coach's playbook. "Against their 2-3 zone, consider running your Flex Zone set (Play #14). In 8 possessions this season, this set has produced 1.12 PPP against zone defenses." Dipson knows the play because it is a data object in the system, not a file on a laptop.

**KDraw to Film Room.** When coaches tag film possessions in the Film Room, they can link each possession to the play that was called. "This possession was our Horns set." Over a season, this builds a database of play-call-to-outcome data: which plays produce the most points per possession, which plays break down against specific defenses, which players execute which plays most effectively. This data feeds Dipson's coaching recommendations and the Simulation Engine.

**KDraw to Scouting.** The Scouting tab can attach play suggestions to matchup analysis. "Against their Pack Line defense, your Dribble Drive series has averaged 0.95 PPP this season. Consider increasing usage from 15% to 25% of half-court possessions." The plays referenced are KDraw plays, viewable with one tap.

**KDraw to KPlay.** Plays assigned to players appear in their KPlay learning environment as interactive study material. Players step through the animation, read coach notes attached to each frame, and confirm completion. Coaches can assign specific plays as homework before games or practices. Completion tracking shows who studied and who did not.

**KDraw to Calendar (Practice Plans).** When coaches build practice plans in Calendar, they attach KDraw plays and drills directly to practice segments. "7:00-7:15 PM: Install Horns Counter (KDraw Play #22). 7:15-7:30 PM: 3-on-3 Shell Drill (KDraw Drill #8)." Players see the practice plan with playable animations for each segment.

**KDraw to Simulation.** When the Simulation Engine projects game outcomes against an upcoming opponent, it uses play-level data. If KDraw plays are tagged to film possessions, the system knows the expected points per possession for each play type against each defensive system. This makes simulations granular: not just "we project 74 points" but "if we run Horns at 20% usage against their ICE coverage, we project 0.98 PPP on those possessions."

## Multi-Sport Expansion

KDraw launches with basketball because basketball coaches are the most active play diagrammers and the market is most established. Expansion follows the same principle as KStat: each sport loads its own field/court template and position markers while sharing the same drawing engine, animation system, organization structure, and integration architecture.

**Football** (Phase 2): Formation diagrams with offensive and defensive fronts. Route trees with individual route assignments. Blocking schemes. Coverage shells. Play-action concepts. The football market for diagramming is even larger than basketball because of the complexity of 11-on-11 schemes.

**Soccer** (Phase 2): Set piece diagrams (corners, free kicks, throw-ins). Tactical shape illustrations (pressing triggers, build-up patterns, transition shapes). Small-sided drill designs.

**Hockey** (Phase 3): Breakout patterns. Power play setups. Penalty kill formations. Forecheck systems. Neutral zone structures.

**Volleyball** (Phase 3): Rotation diagrams. Serve-receive formations. Blocking schemes. Offensive play sets by rotation.

**Lacrosse** (Phase 3): Offensive formations. Ride and clear patterns. Man-up/man-down sets. Defensive slides.

Each sport expansion is a configuration deployment, not a new product build. The drawing engine, animation system, and integration points are universal.

## Competitive Landscape

**FastDraw (Hudl/FastModel Sports).** The 20-year incumbent. Used by nearly every NBA and NCAA team. Over 9,000 community-shared plays. Strengths: brand recognition, massive play library, professional quality output, now integrated with Hudl's film platform after the November 2025 acquisition. Weakness: desktop-first design (the iPad app has not been meaningfully updated since 2016), no connection to an intelligence system, no live play-to-outcome tracking, no AI integration, plays are files not data objects. The Hudl acquisition should improve film-to-diagram integration, but it cannot create the intelligence layer that connects plays to evaluations, simulations, and coaching packets. FastDraw Advanced pricing is enterprise-only (contact sales), creating a cost barrier for small programs.

**The Hoops Geek Play Creator.** Modern mobile-first alternative. Clean interface, FastDraw import capability, affordable pricing, growing community library. Strengths: mobile-native, good UX, aggressive pricing. Weakness: standalone tool with no ecosystem integration. Plays do not connect to film, scouting, intelligence, or any downstream system.

**FastBreak PlayBook.** Another modern alternative with football support. Team-based collaboration, no user caps, athletic department discounts. Strengths: multi-sport (basketball and football), team pricing model. Weakness: same standalone limitation - plays are isolated from every other coaching tool.

**Physical Whiteboards and Napkins.** Still the most common "tool" for the majority of coaches worldwide. Zero cost, zero integration, zero ability to share digitally, zero ability to animate, zero persistence (the whiteboard gets erased after practice).

KDraw's competitive position: it is the only play diagramming tool embedded inside an operating system with AI intelligence, live stat tracking, film analysis, scouting reports, simulation projections, player development, and financial operations. Every competitor draws plays. KDraw draws plays that the entire institution can consume, reference, track, and learn from.

## Why This Matters for Investors

Play diagramming is a universal coaching behavior with a captive market. Every basketball program in America diagrams plays. FastDraw has proven the market exists and is willing to pay for digital tools. Hudl's acquisition of FastModel in November 2025 validates the strategic value of coaching workflow tools.

KDraw enters this market with three structural advantages. First, it is mobile-native from day one (FastDraw's mobile app has not been updated since 2016, and the primary product is desktop software). Second, it is integrated into an operating system rather than standing alone, which means every play connects to seven other systems. Third, it is powered by an intelligence layer that no competitor has - Dipson can recommend plays based on opponent tendencies, track play execution effectiveness over time, and connect play calls to statistical outcomes.

The acquisition cost for KDraw is zero because it is included in the KaNeXT OS platform. Programs do not pay separately for it. This undercuts every competitor's pricing model while delivering more functionality. The revenue model is institutional: programs pay for the OS, and KDraw is one of the reasons they pay.

The multi-sport expansion path follows the same market logic as KStat. Basketball first (proven market, highest coaching tool adoption), then football (largest participation sport, complex scheme requirements create high demand for diagramming tools), then the remaining sports. Each expansion deepens the platform's value to athletic departments that run multiple sports programs.
