Purpose (LOCKED)
Matchup Interaction Governance defines how identity clashes create directional probability pressure inside the Simulation Engine.
 It exists to explain why likelihoods shift, not to decide outcomes.
This layer:
does not simulate possessions

does not evaluate talent

does not resolve outcomes
 It produces governed modifier intents that are consumed downstream.

Clarification (LOCKED): This layer produces directional pressure, not numerical predictions.

Canonical Role Within Simulation Engine (LOCKED)
Matchup Interaction Governance sits between identity inference and possession resolution.

Interaction Classes (LOCKED ORDER)
Matchup Interaction Governance is composed of three interaction classes applied in a non-negotiable order:
Class 1 — System vs System (Global Environment)
Offensive system × Defensive system

Applies to every possession

Establishes the macro environment:

pace tendencies (system layer only)

shot-diet baselines

pressure level

foul environment

Answers: “What kind of game does this matchup tend to produce?”
Class 2 — Archetype vs Coverage (Tactical Interaction)
Offensive archetype × Defensive coverage

Applies conditionally when a coverage appears

Coverage frequency is supplied by defensive inference outputs (DSIE)

Answers: “When the defense does this, who benefits or suffers?”
Rule: This is the primary player-level interaction layer.
Class 3 — Archetype vs Archetype (Direct Matchup)
Offensive archetype × Defensive archetype

Applies rarely and narrowly

Used only when effects are not already explained by:

system interaction, or

coverage interaction

Answers: “Even within the same coverage, does this specific matchup matter?”
Lock rule: Later classes may refine earlier pressure, but may not override or negate them.

Authority and Non-Authority (LOCKED)
Authorized to:
produce directional modifier intents (pressure direction + target variable)

condition modifier intents on identity clashes (system/archetype/coverage)

govern precedence and redundancy rules (prevent double-counting)

Not authorized to:
simulate possessions

force outcomes

change player/team ratings (KR), archetypes, badges, overrides

change usage or minutes

override possession resolution logic

create events

All final outcomes are resolved exclusively by the Possession Engine.

Allowed Modifier Targets (LOCKED)
This layer may target only:
shot-type distribution

shot efficiency (by shot type)

turnover probability

foul-draw probability

offensive rebound probability

pace (system layer only)


Governance Rules (LOCKED)
All modifier intents must be:
bounded downstream (bounds enforced in Modifier Framework)

composed (never arbitrarily stacked)

directionally consistent (no sign-flips downstream)

consistent across systems/archetypes

Conflict resolution (LOCKED):
Coverage modifier intents take precedence over archetype-to-archetype intents

Redundant intents are ignored (no double-counting)

Archetype×Archetype is last-resort only


Determinism (LOCKED)
Matchup Interaction Governance is fully deterministic.
 Given the same:
system identities

coverage frequencies

archetype labels
 …the same governed modifier-intent bundle is produced every time.

No learning, tuning, or adaptation occurs here.

Downstream Use (LOCKED)
Outputs from this section:
are consumed by the Modifier Framework (for composition + bounding)

then passed to the Possession Engine (for application during resolution)

They may never be surfaced as standalone conclusions or coaching advice.
 This layer explains pressure, not performance.
