This file contains the complete reference library for men's track and field athlete evaluation: trait definitions per event group, archetypes, OPF weights, qualifying standards, badges, wind/altitude conversion tables, and indoor-outdoor conversions.

# SECTION 1: TRAIT LIBRARY BY EVENT GROUP

---

## 1.1 SPRINTS (100m, 200m, 400m)

### Performance Traits (PKR inputs)
- **PR Quality:** Best wind-legal FAT mark mapped against event legend
- **Season Best Consistency:** SB relative to PR (SB within 1% of PR = elite consistency)
- **PR Progression:** Year-over-year PR improvement rate
- **Multi-Event Depth:** Performance across 100/200/400 (range indicators)

### Technical Traits (TKR inputs)
- **Reaction Time:** Block clearance speed (elite: <0.140s, good: 0.140-0.160s, average: 0.160-0.180s)
- **Drive Phase:** First 30-40m acceleration quality
- **Transition Phase:** Shift from drive to upright sprinting (30-60m zone)
- **Top-End Mechanics:** Upright sprint form, arm action, hip height, ground contact
- **Speed Maintenance:** Ability to hold form in final 20-30m (deceleration rate)
- **Curve Running (200m, 400m):** Lean mechanics, lane discipline on curves
- **Block Setup:** Start configuration and first-step explosiveness

### Athletic Traits (AKR inputs)
- **Acceleration:** 0-30m split time (if available) or 60m time as proxy
- **Maximum Velocity:** Top speed indicator (flying 30m or peak phase of 100m)
- **Speed Endurance (200m):** Ability to maintain speed through 200m
- **Special Endurance (400m):** Ability to maintain speed through 300-400m zone
- **Anthropometrics:** Height, weight, stride length relative to height
- **Power-to-Weight Ratio:** Inferred from event performances
- **Flexibility/Range of Motion:** Stride mechanics indicator

### Competition Traits (CKR inputs)
- **Championship Performance:** Marks at conference/regional/national meets vs season best
- **Round Improvement:** Prelim to semifinal to final progression
- **Head-to-Head Record:** Performance against top-ranked opponents
- **Consistency:** Standard deviation of marks across season (lower = better)
- **Conditions Performance:** Marks in adverse conditions (headwind, rain, cold)
- **Doubling Ability:** Performance when entered in multiple events same day

### IQ Traits (IQKR inputs)
- **Race Execution (400m):** Split management, even pace vs negative split capability
- **Lane Awareness:** Adaptation to inside/outside lane assignments
- **Tactical Awareness:** Response to competitive pressure, when to push
- **Relay Handoff IQ:** Exchange zone execution, timing, communication

## 1.2 HURDLES (110m H, 400m H)

### Performance Traits (PKR inputs)
- **PR Quality:** Best wind-legal FAT mark mapped against event legend
- **Season Best Consistency:** SB relative to PR
- **PR Progression:** Year-over-year improvement
- **Flat Sprint Correlation:** Hurdle time relative to flat sprint time (efficiency indicator)

### Technical Traits (TKR inputs)
- **Lead Leg Mechanics:** Attack angle, snap-down speed, height over hurdle
- **Trail Leg Mechanics:** Pull-through speed, hip clearance, lateral drift
- **Hurdle Clearance Height:** Minimal air time over hurdle = efficiency
- **Three-Step Rhythm (110mH):** Consistency of stride pattern between hurdles
- **Stride Pattern Flexibility (400mH):** Ability to alternate lead legs, 13/14/15 step patterns
- **First Hurdle Attack:** Distance to first hurdle, steps to first hurdle, block start
- **Run-Off:** Speed maintenance between final hurdle and finish line
- **Transition (400mH):** Rhythm changes at hurdles 5-7 as fatigue sets in

### Athletic Traits (AKR inputs)
- **Sprint Speed:** Flat 100m/200m/400m times (event-dependent)
- **Hip Flexibility:** Range of motion for hurdle clearance
- **Coordination:** Rhythm maintenance at speed
- **Stride Length:** Natural stride relative to hurdle spacing
- **Speed Endurance (400mH):** Maintaining rhythm and clearance under fatigue

### Competition Traits (CKR inputs)
- **Championship Performance:** Marks at major meets
- **Round Improvement:** Prelim to final progression
- **DQ History:** False starts, hurdle knockdowns causing DQ
- **Consistency:** Standard deviation of marks
- **Pressure Performance:** Big-meet execution

### IQ Traits (IQKR inputs)
- **Rhythm Management:** Maintaining or adjusting step pattern mid-race
- **Wind Adaptation:** Adjusting approach in headwind/tailwind conditions
- **Race Plan Execution:** Pace management in 400mH
- **Hurdle Count Awareness:** Knowing where you are in the race

## 1.3 MIDDLE DISTANCE (800m, 1500m)

### Performance Traits (PKR inputs)
- **PR Quality:** Best mark mapped against event legend
- **Season Best Consistency:** SB relative to PR
- **PR Progression:** Year-over-year improvement
- **Multi-Distance Performance:** 800m and 1500m marks showing range

### Endurance Traits (EKR inputs)
- **Aerobic Capacity Indicator:** Performance across distances (800-mile range)
- **Negative Split Ability:** Can run second half faster than first
- **Recovery Between Rounds:** Performance in prelim vs final within same meet
- **Cross Country Performance:** XC results as aerobic indicator
- **Doubling Ability:** Performance in 800 and 1500 at same meet

### Tactical Traits (TKR inputs)
- **Positioning:** Ability to maintain tactical position in pack
- **Move Timing:** When the athlete makes their move (early, middle, late)
- **Response to Surges:** Ability to cover accelerations within race
- **Kick Quality:** Final 200-400m acceleration ability
- **Box Avoidance:** Not getting trapped on rail or in traffic
- **Pace Pushing:** Ability to lead and control pace when needed

### Competition Traits (CKR inputs)
- **Championship Racing:** Marks at conference/regional/national meets
- **Tactical Racing in Championships:** Adapting to slow/fast championship races
- **Round Performance:** Prelim to semifinal to final progression
- **Head-to-Head Record:** Against top-ranked opponents
- **Consistency:** Range of performances across season

### IQ Traits (IQKR inputs)
- **Pace Judgment:** Ability to hit target splits
- **Kick Timing:** Knowing when to launch finishing kick
- **Field Reading:** Awareness of competitor positions and fatigue
- **Race Type Adaptation:** Adjusting tactics to fast (time trial) vs slow (tactical) races
- **Relay Split Execution:** Accurate pace judgment on relay legs

## 1.4 DISTANCE (3000m SC, 5000m, 10000m)

### Performance Traits (PKR inputs)
- **PR Quality:** Best mark (altitude-adjusted if necessary) mapped against legend
- **Season Best Consistency:** SB relative to PR
- **PR Progression:** Year-over-year improvement
- **Multi-Distance Range:** Performance across 5K/10K/XC showing endurance range
- **Steeplechase Water Jump (3000mSC):** Technical component affecting time

### Endurance Traits (EKR inputs)
- **Aerobic Ceiling:** Best distance performance indicator
- **Pace Sustainability:** Ability to maintain even or negative splits over race distance
- **Cross Country Performance:** XC results (regional, national)
- **Altitude Performance:** Marks at altitude venues
- **Recovery Capacity:** Performance across multiple races in a meet or across consecutive weeks
- **Mileage Durability:** Ability to handle high training volume (inferred from consistency)

### Tactical Traits (TKR inputs)
- **Front-Running Ability:** Can lead wire-to-wire
- **Pack Running:** Efficiency in group, drafting, conserving energy
- **Surge Response:** Covering mid-race accelerations
- **Kick Quality:** Final lap/final 800m acceleration
- **Water Jump Technique (SC):** Barrier clearance efficiency
- **Pace Variance:** Ability to run steady vs ability to change gears

### Competition Traits (CKR inputs)
- **Championship Performance:** Conference, regional, national marks
- **XC Nationals Performance:** Placement at cross country nationals
- **Round Performance:** For events with rounds at nationals
- **All-Conditions Performance:** Heat, cold, wind, rain, hills (XC)
- **Head-to-Head vs Ranked Opponents**
- **Season-Long Consistency**

### IQ Traits (IQKR inputs)
- **Pace Judgment:** Ability to execute race plan
- **When to Lead vs Follow:** Tactical decision-making
- **Kick Timing:** When to commit to final move
- **Surge Deployment:** Strategic use of surges to break opponents
- **Water Jump Strategy (SC):** Hurdle technique decisions
- **Race Reading:** Awareness of competitor fatigue

## 1.5 JUMPS (High Jump, Long Jump, Triple Jump, Pole Vault)

### Performance Traits (PKR inputs)
- **PR Quality:** Best mark (wind-legal for LJ/TJ) mapped against legend
- **Season Best Consistency:** SB relative to PR
- **PR Progression:** Year-over-year improvement
- **Indoor vs Outdoor Comparison:** Indoor marks vs outdoor marks

### Technical Traits (TKR inputs)

**High Jump:**
- Approach path (J-curve execution)
- Penultimate step and takeoff mechanics
- Bar clearance (Fosbury flop technique)
- Landing mechanics
- Approach consistency (step marks)

**Long Jump:**
- Approach speed and consistency (step marks)
- Board contact (distance from foul line)
- Takeoff angle and mechanics
- Flight technique (hitch-kick, hang, sail)
- Landing mechanics (reaching, falling back)

**Triple Jump:**
- Approach speed and consistency
- Phase ratios (hop-step-jump distribution)
- Board contact accuracy
- Active landings in each phase
- Arm action coordination
- Hop-to-step and step-to-jump transitions

**Pole Vault:**
- Approach speed and consistency
- Plant timing and mechanics
- Pole selection (stiffness relative to weight/speed)
- Inversion quality (getting upside down on pole)
- Bar clearance
- Takeoff point relative to back of box
- Grip height progression

### Athletic Traits (AKR inputs)
- **Approach Speed:** Sprint speed relevant to the event (60m, 100m times)
- **Explosive Power:** Standing vertical, standing long jump if available
- **Body Control:** Air awareness, spatial coordination
- **Strength-to-Weight Ratio:** Critical for PV, TJ
- **Ankle/Knee Stiffness:** Ground contact quality for jumps
- **Flexibility:** Hip mobility for HJ, active flexibility for PV

### Competition Traits (CKR inputs)
- **Championship Marks:** Performance at major meets
- **Consistency:** Standard deviation of marks across season
- **First-Attempt Success Rate:** Making opening heights/distances
- **Progression Strategy:** Bar/mark progression management
- **Pass Strategy (HJ, PV):** Strategic passing to conserve attempts
- **Foul Rate (LJ, TJ):** Percentage of fouls in competition

### IQ Traits (IQKR inputs)
- **Approach Adjustment:** Ability to adjust approach in changing conditions (wind, surface)
- **Competition Management:** When to pass, when to take safety jumps
- **Height/Distance Progression:** Strategic bar/mark requests
- **Conditions Reading:** Adapting to headwind/tailwind (LJ, TJ), crosswind (HJ, PV)
- **Attempt Allocation:** Knowing when to play safe vs when to attack

## 1.6 THROWS (Shot Put, Discus, Hammer, Javelin)

### Performance Traits (PKR inputs)
- **PR Quality:** Best mark mapped against legend
- **Season Best Consistency:** SB relative to PR
- **PR Progression:** Year-over-year improvement
- **Multi-Throw Range:** Performance across multiple implements (versatility)

### Technical Traits (TKR inputs)

**Shot Put:**
- Technique style (glide vs rotational)
- Ring entry mechanics
- Power position
- Release angle (optimal ~37-42 degrees)
- Release height
- Reverse/save mechanics
- Ring discipline (fouls)

**Discus:**
- Wind-up and entry mechanics
- Rotational speed through ring
- Power position
- Release angle and attitude (tilt)
- Release point relative to ring front
- Ring discipline (fouls)

**Hammer:**
- Wind mechanics (turns)
- Number of turns (3 or 4)
- Orbit radius
- Counter-weight balance
- Release timing
- Ring discipline (fouls, cage clearance)

**Javelin:**
- Approach run mechanics
- Crossover steps
- Blocking mechanics
- Release angle and point alignment
- Implement vibration (clean vs wobbly release)
- Runway discipline (fouls)

### Athletic Traits (AKR inputs)
- **Raw Strength:** Inferred from implement distances and body type
- **Rotational Power:** Speed through rotation (SP rotational, DT, HT)
- **Linear Power:** Drive across ring/runway (SP glide, JT)
- **Size/Frame:** Height, weight, wingspan
- **Speed Across Ring/Runway:** Implement-specific velocity
- **Flexibility:** Range of motion in rotational events

### Competition Traits (CKR inputs)
- **Championship Marks:** Performance at major meets
- **Consistency:** Standard deviation of marks
- **Foul Rate:** Percentage of fouls in competition
- **Series Progression:** Ability to improve through 6 attempts
- **First-Throw Quality:** Getting a mark on the board early
- **Pressure Performance:** Finals performance, clutch throws

### IQ Traits (IQKR inputs)
- **Attempt Management:** When to attack, when to play safe
- **Adjustment Between Throws:** Technical corrections mid-competition
- **Conditions Adaptation:** Wind adjustment for DT and JT
- **Implement Selection:** Understanding implement specifications and preferences
- **Competition Awareness:** Knowing what mark is needed to advance/medal

## 1.7 DECATHLON

### Evaluation Framework
The decathlon is evaluated as a composite of underlying event-group skills weighted by World Athletics scoring table contribution. The decathlete receives a single KR based on total points potential, with component analysis across all 10 events.

### Decathlon Events (Order of Competition)
Day 1: 100m, Long Jump, Shot Put, High Jump, 400m
Day 2: 110m Hurdles, Discus, Pole Vault, Javelin, 1500m

### Component KR Mapping for Decathlon

| Decathlon Event | Event Group | Component KRs Applied |
|-----------------|-------------|----------------------|
| 100m | Sprints | PKR, TKR, AKR |
| Long Jump | Jumps | PKR, TKR, AKR |
| Shot Put | Throws | PKR, TKR, AKR |
| High Jump | Jumps | PKR, TKR, AKR |
| 400m | Sprints | PKR, AKR, IQKR |
| 110m Hurdles | Hurdles | PKR, TKR, AKR |
| Discus | Throws | PKR, TKR, AKR |
| Pole Vault | Jumps | PKR, TKR, AKR |
| Javelin | Throws | PKR, TKR, AKR |
| 1500m | Distance | PKR, EKR, IQKR |

### Decathlon PKR Anchoring
- Anchor on TOTAL POINTS using World Athletics scoring tables
- Individual event marks converted to points
- Total points mapped against decathlon-specific legend tiers

### Decathlon Archetypes (see Section 2)
Decathletes are typed by their scoring distribution pattern (speed-power dominant, throw-dominant, endurance-strong, balanced).

# SECTION 2: ARCHETYPE LIBRARY

---

## 2.1 Sprint Archetypes

**Pure Speed Sprinter (100m-focused)**
- Elite 100m, competitive 200m, does not run 400m
- Explosive start, highest max velocity in group
- KR driven by PKR and AKR
- Examples: Short-distance specialists with elite reaction time and acceleration

**Speed-Power Sprinter (100m/200m double)**
- Elite across both short sprints
- Can sustain max velocity through 200m curve and straight
- High PKR across both events
- Versatile relay anchor

**Long Sprinter (200m/400m double)**
- Best relative marks at 200m and 400m
- Superior speed endurance over max velocity
- Higher IQKR demand for 400m pace management
- AKR weighted toward special endurance

**Quarter-Miler (400m specialist)**
- PR hierarchy: 400m > 200m > 100m (relative to legends)
- Elite speed endurance and lactate tolerance
- Highest IQKR demand in sprint group (pace execution is everything)
- May anchor 4x400m relay

---

## 2.2 Hurdle Archetypes

**Speed Hurdler (110mH)**
- Fast flat sprinter who clears hurdles efficiently
- Hurdle time close to flat sprint time ratio (elite ratio ~1.18-1.22 for 110mH vs 100m)
- TKR driven by clearance efficiency
- AKR reflects underlying sprint ability

**Technical Hurdler (110mH)**
- Excellent hurdle technique compensating for moderate flat speed
- Lower sprint speed but higher TKR
- Efficient clearance, minimal air time, rhythm consistency
- Development path: improve flat speed to unlock PR

**Intermediate Hurdler (400mH)**
- Hybrid of speed endurance and hurdle technique
- Must manage stride pattern changes through fatigue
- Highest TKR demand of any event (rhythm + fatigue + technique)
- IQKR critical for stride pattern decisions at hurdles 5-8

---

## 2.3 Middle Distance Archetypes

**Tactical Miler (1500m specialist)**
- Best marks in slow, tactical races where kick matters
- Elite IQKR and CKR
- May have moderate PKR in time trials but elevates in championship races
- Kick quality is the defining trait

**Front Runner**
- Runs best from the front
- Strong EKR, high pace sustainability
- Lower tactical flexibility but can break races open
- Effective in time-trial-paced races

**Kicker**
- Sits and waits, then produces devastating final 200-400m
- Highest IQKR and CKR combination
- PKR may look moderate because fast times require someone else to set pace
- Most dangerous in championship racing

**800m/1500m Double Threat**
- Competitive across both events
- Rare combination of speed and endurance
- EKR and AKR both strong
- High value in team scoring (can score in multiple events)

---

## 2.4 Distance Archetypes

**Grinder**
- Even-pace specialist, never fades
- Highest EKR in group
- Moderate kick, relies on wearing opponents down
- Strong XC performer

**Front-Running Pacer**
- Controls race from the front, sets honest pace
- High EKR and TKR
- Effective in championship races where no one wants to lead
- Can string field out and isolate weak kickers

**Kicker (Distance)**
- Sits in pack, produces strong final lap
- PKR may not reflect championship ceiling (needs someone else to set pace)
- Highest IQKR in distance group
- XC performance may underrepresent track ceiling

**Steeplechase Specialist**
- Barrier technique is the differentiator
- Combination of distance running and hurdle/jump ability
- TKR includes water jump technique
- Often converts from flat distance events

**Volume Racer**
- Can compete across 1500m through 10000m
- Exceptional EKR and recovery
- High team-scoring value (enters multiple events)
- May not have one elite event but contributes across distances

---

## 2.5 Jump Archetypes

**Explosive Vertical Jumper (HJ)**
- Elite approach speed and takeoff power
- May come from basketball or other jumping sports
- High AKR, developing TKR
- Ceiling may be very high with technical development

**Technical High Jumper**
- Average athleticism elevated by elite technique
- Approach curve, penultimate step, and bar clearance all refined
- High TKR compensates for moderate AKR
- Consistent competition results (high CKR)

**Speed Long Jumper**
- Sprinter who long jumps
- Elite approach speed (sub-11.0 100m typical for elite LJ)
- May have moderate technique but distance comes from speed
- AKR dominant profile

**Technical Long Jumper**
- Board accuracy, flight technique, and landing maximize moderate approach speed
- High TKR relative to AKR
- Lower foul rate than speed jumpers
- Consistent marks (high CKR)

**Power Triple Jumper**
- Emphasizes hop-step-jump power over approach speed
- Strong phase ratios, powerful ground contacts
- High AKR
- May be built heavier than typical sprinter-jumpers

**Technical Triple Jumper**
- Phase ratio optimization, active landings, rhythm
- Preserves energy through each phase transition
- High TKR, moderate AKR
- Efficient technique allows competitive marks with less raw power

**Athletic Pole Vaulter**
- Raw speed and gymnastics ability
- High grip height potential from approach velocity
- May be developing technical aspects
- AKR dominant, TKR as development area

**Technical Pole Vaulter**
- Plant timing, inversion, and clearance are elite
- May use a stiffer pole than athleticism alone would suggest (technique enables it)
- High TKR and IQKR
- Competition management (grip height progression, pole selection) is a strength

---

## 2.6 Throw Archetypes

**Power Thrower (SP, DT, HT)**
- Raw strength and size dominant
- High AKR, developing TKR
- Distance comes from force applied to implement
- May have higher foul rate from aggressive attempts
- Often excels across multiple throw events

**Technical Thrower**
- Refined mechanics compensate for moderate physical tools
- High TKR, moderate AKR
- Consistent marks, low foul rate
- Maximal efficiency from available power

**Speed Thrower (JT, DT)**
- Approach/rotational speed is the primary distance driver
- Leaner build than typical power thrower
- May have sprint or jump background
- High implement velocity at release

**Multi-Throw Athlete**
- Competitive across 2+ throw events
- Versatile physical profile
- High team-scoring value
- May be a decathlete who throws well

---

## 2.7 Decathlon Archetypes

**Speed-Power Decathlete**
- Scoring concentrated in sprints, hurdles, jumps
- 100m, LJ, HJ, 110mH are primary point sources
- Weaker in throws and 1500m
- High overall AKR

**Throw-Heavy Decathlete**
- Scoring concentrated in throws (SP, DT, JT)
- Larger athlete, may lose points in running events
- High PKR in throw events compensates for moderate PKR in running events
- Unique body type for decathlon

**Balanced Decathlete**
- Even scoring distribution across all 10 events
- No major weaknesses, no single event domination
- Highest overall KR potential (no points left on table)
- Rare archetype at elite level

**Endurance-Strong Decathlete**
- Stronger in 400m and 1500m than typical decathlete
- Gains points where others lose them
- May have XC or middle distance background
- Moderate speed-power events

# SECTION 6: INDOOR-OUTDOOR CONVERSION FACTORS

---

## 6.1 Standard Conversion Factors

Indoor times are generally slower than outdoor equivalents due to: banked/flat turns, shorter straightaways, tighter track geometry, temperature/ventilation differences.

| Event (Indoor) | Event (Outdoor) | Typical Conversion |
|-----------------|----------------|-------------------|
| 60m | 100m | Not directly convertible (different race structure) |
| 200m (indoor) | 200m (outdoor) | Indoor typically 0.3-0.5s slower on flat track, 0.1-0.3s on banked |
| 400m (indoor) | 400m (outdoor) | Indoor typically 0.5-1.0s slower on flat, 0.3-0.5s on banked |
| 800m (indoor) | 800m (outdoor) | Indoor typically 0.5-1.5s slower |
| Mile (indoor) | 1500m (outdoor) | Mile = 1609m. Convert time to 1500m pace equivalent |
| 3000m (indoor) | 3000mSC/5000m | Pace conversion, not direct comparison |
| 60mH (indoor) | 110mH (outdoor) | Not directly convertible (different hurdle count and spacing) |
| HJ, LJ, TJ, PV, SP (indoor) | Same (outdoor) | Marks are directly comparable; indoor may be slightly lower due to approach limitations |

Note: Banked tracks produce faster times than flat indoor tracks. Always note track surface type when available.

# SECTION 9: SYSTEM RISKS

---

## 9.1 Performance Risks

**Wind-Aided Only:** Best marks are all wind-aided; no wind-legal PR of comparable quality. Risk: PKR may be inflated.

**Altitude-Assisted Only:** Best distance marks set at altitude with no sea-level equivalent. Risk: PKR may overstate sea-level ability.

**Indoor-Only:** No outdoor season results available. Risk: outdoor performance uncertain.

**Single-Meet Peak:** PR comes from one exceptional meet with no supporting marks close to that level. Risk: PR may be anomaly.

**Championship Underperformer:** Consistent pattern of worse marks at championship meets vs regular season. Risk: CKR suppressed, may not perform when it matters most.

**Injury Flag:** Known injury history that may affect current or future performance. Risk: trajectory assessment unreliable.

**Age-Relative Concern:** Performance plateau or regression at young age may indicate ceiling. Risk: development upside may be limited.

## 9.2 Data Risks

**Unverified Marks:** PR from meet without standard TFRRS/World Athletics reporting. Risk: timing or measurement accuracy uncertain.

**Hand Timing:** Results from hand-timed meets. Risk: times unreliable without FAT conversion.

**Missing Wind Data:** Sprint or jump marks without recorded wind readings. Risk: cannot confirm wind-legal status.

**Limited Competition History:** Fewer than 5 meets in current season. Risk: confidence level reduced.

# SECTION 10: KLVN LAMBDA NORMALIZATION (College)

---

## 10.1 How Lambda Works in Track and Field

Lambda in track and field works differently than in team sports. In basketball, lambda adjusts production stats because the same box score numbers mean different things at different levels. In track and field, a 10.50 100m is a 10.50 100m regardless of level.

**Lambda adjusts CONTEXT, not MARKS:**
- Lambda does not change the PKR. A PR is a PR.
- Lambda adjusts CKR, TKR, and IQKR to reflect the competition quality in which those traits were demonstrated
- Running a 10.50 to win a JUCO conference championship demonstrates different CKR than running a 10.50 in a D1 prelim round

### Competition Quality Lambda

|-----------|--------|
| NCAA D1 Championships | 1.000 |
| NCAA D1 Regional/Prelims | 0.975 |
| NCAA D1 Conference Championship | 0.950 |
| NCAA D1 Major Invitational (high-level field) | 0.940 |
| NCAA D1 Dual/Regular Season | 0.920 |
| NCAA D1 Low-Level Invitational | 0.900 |
| NCAA D2 Championships | 0.900 |
| NCAA D2 Conference Championship | 0.870 |
| NCAA D2 Regular Season | 0.850 |
| NCAA D3 Championships | 0.870 |
| NCAA D3 Conference Championship | 0.850 |
| NCAA D3 Regular Season | 0.830 |
| NAIA Championships | 0.870 |
| NAIA Conference Championship | 0.840 |
| NAIA Regular Season | 0.820 |
| NJCAA D1 Championships | 0.850 |
| NJCAA D1 Conference Championship | 0.820 |
| NJCAA D1 Regular Season | 0.800 |
| NJCAA D2 Championships | 0.780 |
| NJCAA D3 Championships | 0.720 |
| Unattached/Open Meet | 0.880 |
| High School State Championship | 0.750 |
| High School Invitational | 0.700 |

### Lambda Application Rules
1. PKR is NEVER adjusted by lambda. The mark is the mark.
2. CKR uses lambda to weight the value of competition performances. Winning at NCAA D1 Championships (lambda 1.000) contributes more to CKR than winning at a dual meet (lambda 0.920).
3. TKR uses lambda only when assessing technical execution against competition quality. Running technically well against weak competition is less informative than running technically well against elite fields.
4. IQKR uses lambda when assessing tactical racing. Tactical intelligence demonstrated in championship-quality fields (deep, fast races) is weighted higher than in thin fields.
5. AKR and EKR are NOT adjusted by lambda. Physical traits are physical traits.

## 11.1 Decathlon Scoring Overview

The World Athletics (formerly IAAF) scoring tables convert individual event performances into points. The formulas are:

**Running events:** Points = A x (B - P)^C (where P = performance in seconds)
**Jumping events:** Points = A x (P - B)^C (where P = performance in centimeters)
**Throwing events:** Points = A x (P - B)^C (where P = performance in meters)

A, B, C are constants specific to each event.
