# KaNeXT Execution Timeline

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

---

## Purpose

This document presents the Year 1 execution sequence for KaNeXT, organized by trigger rather than by fixed calendar date. It identifies what happens when partnership execution occurs, what depends on what, and what triggers readiness for subsequent milestones. Year 2 is described at the milestone level, conditional on Year 1 execution.

The athletic system is the visible engine of the Year 1 plan. Championships, enrollment growth, platform adoption, and media visibility all flow from the athletic buildout at the partner institution. Sports are the front-facing story. Underneath the athletic story, groundwork for future capital events begins in Year 1 even though those events do not activate until later.

---

## Year 1 Sequence

### Trigger 1: Fund Close

Fund close is the starting trigger for the Year 1 sequence. All downstream execution is gated on capital availability through the fund and parallel SAFE.

On fund close:
- $30M fund operational. $3M SAFE capital available.
- OSK Group subsidiary structure finalized. KaNeXT IP LLC formation completed.
- Core team hires initiated: VP of Athletics, Chief of Staff, Director of Enrollment Operations, key engineering and operations additions.
- Legal counsel engaged across corporate, fund formation, securities, intellectual property, and partnership agreements.
- Corporate infrastructure activated: office lease, insurance, payroll, accounting, banking.
- Board governance structure activated (founder retains operating authority; advisory board formed).

### Trigger 2: Year 1 Partnership Execution

Partnership execution is the event that unlocks operational deployment at the institution.

On Athletics Operating Agreement execution with the partner institution:
- Year 1 capital deployment begins according to the capital deployment plan.
- The partner institution institutional coordinator designated on the university side.
- KaNeXT institutional lead designated on the operator side.
- Initial $2.1M the partner institution Institutional Support disbursed per agreement terms.
- Joint operational integration planning begins.
- Platform deployment planning begins for the partner institution activation across KTV, KPay, KStat, KVision, Dipson, VoIP, and associated infrastructure.

### Trigger 3: GAAC Mandate Execution

The GAAC governing body mandate runs parallel to Year 1 partnership execution. GAAC does not require the partner institution execution to proceed, but the two are coordinated because the partner institution is a GAAC member institution and the founder's existing conference relationships provide the relationship infrastructure for the mandate.

On GAAC Mandate Agreement execution:
- Platform activation across GAAC member institutions at zero cost to the conference and zero cost to member schools.
- Software-first deployment. Schools use existing video sources through KaNeXT's camera-agnostic architecture.
- KTV broadcast activation for GAAC competition.
- KVision and KStat activation for GAAC games.
- KPay activation for GAAC commerce and institutional transactions.
- Conference-level analytics and media infrastructure provided to the GAAC Commissioner's office.

### Trigger 4: Pre-Season Build

With partnerships in execution and capital deploying, the pre-season build prepares the athletic programs, housing infrastructure, and platform deployment for competitive season launch.

During pre-season build:
- Head coach hires across the six Varsity programs (built to NAIA competition quality for Year 2 activation) and selected Development tier teams.
- Athletic staff hires: assistant coaches, athletic trainers, strength and conditioning, medical support, athletic operations.
- Athlete recruiting through the KaNeXT intelligence system. Roster builds across Varsity and development tiers.
- Master lease execution on cluster housing in the South Florida market.
- Athletic facility partnerships executed with commercial gym and junior college partners.
- Weight room and training facility equipment procurement and installation at partner locations.
- Team dining contract execution.
- Academic integration planning with the partner institution leadership for athlete academic support.
- NAIA membership application submitted by October 15, 2026 deadline for April 2027 Council of Presidents decision. Active NAIA Varsity membership targeted for Fall 2027 (Year 2). Year 1 competitive affiliation selected from available options: USCAA, NCCAA, independent scheduling, or combination.
- NIL distribution infrastructure activated through KPay.
- Platform integration with the partner institution's existing Populi student information system.

### Trigger 5: Fall Semester Launch

Fall semester marks the public face of Year 1. Teams begin practice, enter competition, and the athletic engine begins generating the visibility that drives the Year 2+ institutional flywheel.

At fall semester launch:
- All Varsity and development tier teams activated in their respective competitions.
- The partner institution athlete cohort enrolled as full-time students. Housing, dining, and academic support operational.
- GAAC competition active for development tier teams. KTV broadcasts every GAAC contest.
- Varsity competition begins for approved programs at Year 1 competitive affiliation level (USCAA, NCCAA, independent, or combination). D1 guarantee game scheduling executed where competitive. NAIA Varsity competition activates Year 2 upon NAIA membership approval.
- Platform live for the partner institution students, faculty, staff, and administrators.
- Dipson operational across institutional functions including athletic recruiting, admissions support, and student services.
- Athletic media production active. KTV content flows.
- Recruiting engine for Year 2 athletic class activates.
- National and regional media outreach begins around the athletic programs.

### Trigger 6: Mid-Year Operations

The mid-year window covers the heart of competitive season and the first measurable outcomes of the partnership.

During mid-year operations:
- Athletic season results begin materializing. National rankings, conference standings, and individual athlete recognition.
- Enrollment inquiry volume measurement. Athletic visibility drives prospective student interest.
- Platform adoption metrics across the partner institution community.
- Commerce flow measurement through KPay.
- KTV viewership and engagement metrics.
- Intelligence system calibration against competitive outcomes.
- Year 2 athletic recruiting in active campaign.
- Year 2 institutional planning begins jointly with the partner institution leadership.
- Forward capital event groundwork progresses per below.

### Trigger 7: Year 1 Close and Year 2 Preparation

Year 1 close consolidates operational results, evaluates partnership performance against the expansion criteria in the Athletics Operating Agreement, and positions both parties for Year 2 decisions.

At Year 1 close:
- Athletic performance review across all programs and tiers.
- Enrollment impact measurement.
- Financial reconciliation for both KaNeXT and the partner institution.
- Platform performance review.
- Partnership performance review against expansion criteria.
- Year 2 planning: athletic expansion, development tier growth in additional sports, platform scope expansion, and Full IOA conversion analysis.
- Year 2 capital planning in alignment with Forward Capital Architecture.

## Forward Capital Event Groundwork

While Year 1 capital funds the Year 1 partnership launch and does not fund the forward capital events, groundwork for those events begins in Year 1. This groundwork is operational positioning rather than capital deployment. The actual acquisitions and infrastructure buildouts are future capital events activated later.

**Miami Lakes campus groundwork.** Early conversations and relationship development with Graham Companies, the generational landowner in Miami Lakes. Site evaluation. Zoning and regulatory research. No capital commitment. No LOI. Positioning for future acquisition when the partner institution reaches the fully-built state.

**Camera company groundwork.** Industry research and vendor identification for eventual camera manufacturing acquisition. Target company diligence at the preliminary level. No acquisition initiated. The Year 1 mandate and the partner institution deployments operate camera-agnostic, proving the software stack independent of owned hardware.

**Fulfillment center groundwork.** Industry research and facility evaluation for eventual merchandise production and distribution. Scale planning against projected mandate rollout. No facility acquired. Year 1 merchandise activity for the partner institution operates through standard third-party fulfillment until scale justifies a dedicated operation.

**VoIP infrastructure groundwork.** Regulatory landscape evaluation (FCC, international numbering authorities). Target telecommunications company identification. No acquisition initiated. KaNeXT OS VoIP functionality operates on existing infrastructure providers during Year 1.

**Bank charter groundwork.** Regulatory consultation with federal and state banking authorities. Target community bank identification. Deposit trajectory modeling based on the partner institution commerce and projected mandate commerce. No charter filing initiated. Year 1 financial operations flow through KaNeXT OS and partner institutions. The bank charter becomes a viable capital event when deposit volume justifies it.

**Mandate rollout groundwork.** Relationship development with NAIA, NJCAA, USCAA, and NCCAA national office leadership. Pitch materials refined based on GAAC deployment learnings. No mandate agreements executed beyond GAAC in Year 1. The learnings from running GAAC at full depth inform how KaNeXT approaches the larger bodies in Year 2 and beyond.

The Year 1 execution demonstrates capacity and credibility. The Forward Capital Architecture activates against that demonstrated capacity.

## Year 2 Milestone Conditions

Year 2 execution is conditional on Year 1 performance. Specific execution plans are developed at the time Year 2 capital is raised, based on Year 1 results. The following are the milestone conditions that position each Year 2 activity to activate.

**Expanded athletic footprint condition.** Additional development tier teams across existing Varsity sports. Possible new Varsity sport additions. Expansion gated on Year 1 athletic performance and enrollment pull.

**Full IOA expansion condition.** Conversion from Athletics Operating Agreement to Full Institutional Operating Agreement. Gated on the expansion criteria defined in the Year 1 agreement, including athletic performance, enrollment growth, operational excellence, institutional health, partnership standing, and financial performance.

**Campus acquisition condition.** Miami Lakes campus land acquisition and early development activates when the partner institution has reached the state of growth and execution that the campus build supports. The campus follows demonstrated institutional momentum, not the reverse.

**Mandate expansion condition.** Engagement with NAIA, NJCAA, USCAA, NCCAA. Gated on GAAC deployment results and platform infrastructure readiness.

**Platform infrastructure condition.** Camera company acquisition, fulfillment center buildout, VoIP acquisition. Gated on demand signals from GAAC mandate operations and projected demand from larger governing body mandates.

**Bank charter condition.** Community bank acquisition and charter transfer. Gated on deposit volume from platform commerce reaching a scale that justifies the regulatory investment.

No Year 2 milestone is committed in advance. Each activates when Year 1 results justify the forward capital event that enables it.

## Dependencies and Sequencing Principles

**Partnership execution is the gate.** Capital does not deploy before the Partner Institution Athletics Operating Agreement is executed. Operational hires beyond corporate infrastructure and initial team builds are gated on partnership execution.

**Athletics is the visible engine.** Championships, enrollment growth, media visibility, and institutional brand all flow from athletic performance. The athletic system is the operational centerpiece of Year 1 execution.

**Software deployment is lighter than infrastructure acquisition.** the partner institution platform deployment and GAAC mandate deployment proceed through the existing KaNeXT OS. These do not depend on owning the camera company, the fulfillment center, or the bank. The infrastructure acquisitions are future capital events, not Year 1 requirements.

**Groundwork is not deployment.** Year 1 groundwork for forward capital events is preparation, not execution. Relationships, research, targets, and positioning all develop. Capital commitments and binding agreements wait for the Year 2 capital events that fund them.

**Momentum compounds forward.** The Year 1 athletic engine generates the institutional brand and platform usage data that makes Year 2 capital raisable. Without the Year 1 execution, the Year 2 capital events lack the foundation to justify their scale. The sequence matters.

## Disclaimer

All execution timeline descriptions are forward-looking and based on current operational planning. Actual execution depends on partnership execution timing, operational conditions, regulatory developments, market conditions, and the discretion of operational leadership. No specific calendar dates, months, or fiscal quarters are committed by this document. The Year 2 milestone conditions are descriptions of readiness states, not commitments to specific execution timing or scope. Each Year 2 milestone requires separate capital, separate agreements, and separate evaluation at the time of activation.
