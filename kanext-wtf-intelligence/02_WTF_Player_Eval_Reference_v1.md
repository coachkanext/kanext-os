# Women's Track and Field Player Evaluation Reference v1

---

## 0. SCOPE

This is the single authoritative reference document for the women's track and field evaluation system. It contains all trait bands, event benchmarks, OPF weights, archetypes, badges, overrides, risks, and KLVN normalization data.

**ALL BENCHMARKS IN THIS FILE ARE WOMEN'S-SPECIFIC.** Do NOT reference men's benchmarks.

# SECTION 2: TRAIT CLUSTERS AND SCORING

## 2.1 Performance Cluster (PKR)
The primary production metric. In track and field, the mark IS the performance.

### Running Events
- **Primary Mark:** Wind-legal personal best in primary event (weight 1.0)
- **Season Best:** Current season best mark (weight 0.9)
- **Indoor Best:** Indoor season best for context (weight 0.7)
- **Secondary Event Marks:** PBs in secondary events (weight 0.5)
- **Championship Performance:** Best mark in championship meets (conference, regional, national) (weight 0.95)
- **Relay Splits:** Individual relay leg times (weight 0.5 for PKR)

Scoring: Map each mark against the event benchmark table. Weight and composite.

### Field Events
- **Primary Mark:** Personal best in primary event (weight 1.0)
- **Season Best:** Current season best (weight 0.9)
- **Indoor Best:** Indoor best (weight 0.7)
- **Consistency Band:** Spread between PB and season average; tighter spread = higher score (weight 0.6)
- **Championship Performance:** Best mark in championship meets (weight 0.95)

### Heptathlon
- **Total Points:** World Athletics scoring table total (weight 1.0)
- **Event Balance Score:** Standard deviation of individual event KRs; lower SD = more balanced = higher score (weight 0.4)

## 2.2 Technical Cluster (TKR)
Event-specific technique quality.

### Sprint TKR (V1 proxy)
- Start reaction time (if available from championship data)
- Speed maintenance: ratio of 100m split in 200m vs standalone 100m
- 200m curve running efficiency: 100m-to-200m ratio
- 400m pace distribution: negative split capability
- At V1: TKR is largely proxy-weighted (0.7)

### Hurdle TKR (V1 proxy)
- Flat-to-hurdle differential (smaller gap = better technique)
- DNF rate (lower = more consistent technique)
- Consistency across meets
- At V1: proxy-weighted 0.7

### Middle Distance / Distance TKR (V1 proxy)
- Pacing consistency: standard deviation of splits across races
- Negative split frequency
- Tactical versatility: can race from front, middle, or closing position
- At V1: proxy-weighted 0.7

### Jump TKR (V1 proxy)
- First-attempt clearance rate (HJ/PV)
- Foul rate (LJ/TJ)
- Consistency (spread between PB and average)
- Phase ratios (TJ only)
- At V1: proxy-weighted 0.7

### Throw TKR (V1 proxy)
- Consistency: spread between best and average marks
- Foul rate (higher foul rate may indicate aggressive but uncontrolled technique)
- Multi-throw capability: does she throw multiple events competently?
- Indoor-to-outdoor conversion (SP indoor to outdoor)
- At V1: proxy-weighted 0.7

## 2.3 Athletic/Endurance Cluster (AKR or EKR)

### AKR (Sprints, Hurdles, Jumps, Throws)
Raw physical tools.

- **Speed indicators:** 100m flat time (or 60m indoor) as a baseline for power/speed
- **Power indicators:** Throw distances for throwers; jump heights/distances for jumpers
- **Explosiveness:** Rate of force development indicators (jump event takeoff, throw release speed)
- **Coordination:** Multi-event ability, relay baton exchange quality
- At V1: largely proxy-weighted from marks (0.7-0.8)

### EKR (Middle Distance, Distance)
Aerobic and anaerobic capacity.

- **Aerobic base:** Cross country results, 5000m/10000m marks for distance runners
- **Anaerobic capacity:** 800m time for middle distance context, last-lap speed
- **Speed reserve:** Flat 400m time vs 800m closing speed; flat 1500m time vs 5000m closing speed
- **Durability:** Season-long consistency, competition frequency without mark degradation
- At V1: proxy-weighted from marks and race patterns (0.7-0.8)

## 2.4 Competitive Cluster (CKR)
Championship performance and consistency.

- **Championship meet performance:** Marks in conference, regional, and national championship meets vs season best
  - Bands: 90+ = PB or SB in championship meet; 80 = within 1% of SB; 70 = within 2-3%; 60 = 3-5% off; <60 = >5% off
- **Big-meet improvement:** Does she perform better in championship settings?
- **Head-to-head record:** Performance against highly-ranked competitors
- **Finals conversion:** Makes finals and improves through rounds (prelims -> semis -> finals)
- **Consistency across season:** Standard deviation of marks throughout the season
- At V1: scored from championship meet results and season-long mark trends

## 2.5 IQ Cluster (IQKR)
Tactical and strategic intelligence.

### Running Events IQKR
- **Race strategy:** Ability to execute varied race plans (front-run, sit-and-kick, move from mid-pack)
- **Pacing intelligence:** Even or negative splits when tactically appropriate
- **Competitive awareness:** Response to moves by competitors
- **Lane/position management:** In 800m/1500m, ability to avoid boxes and maintain position
- **Championship tactical acumen:** Different strategy in rounds vs finals
- At V1: proxy-weighted 0.6 (inferred from race patterns and results)

### Field Events IQKR
- **Attempt management:** Strategic use of passes (HJ/PV), attempt allocation
- **Competition management:** Adjusting opening height/mark based on competition
- **Pressure response:** Performance when behind or in medal contention
- **Progression strategy:** Moving through competition efficiently
- At V1: proxy-weighted 0.6

### Throws IQKR
- **Series management:** Building through a competition (first throw safe, later throws aggressive)
- **Foul management:** Adjusting aggression based on standing
- **Competition awareness:** Responding to competitors' marks
- At V1: proxy-weighted 0.6

# SECTION 4: ARCHETYPE LIBRARY

## 4.1 Sprint Archetypes

**1) Pure Speed Merchant** - Dominant 100m/200m, limited 400m range. PKR 100m >= 85, 100m-to-200m ratio <= 2.05.
**2) Speed-Endurance Sprinter** - Strong 200m/400m range, competitive 100m. PKR 400m >= 85, PKR 200m >= 83.
**3) Quarter-Miler** - 400m specialist with limited pure speed. PKR 400m >= 85, PKR 100m may be 80 or below.
**4) Relay Specialist** - Strong relay splits, competitive but not elite individual marks. Relay split within 0.3s of individual PB.

## 4.2 Hurdle Archetypes

**5) Sprint Hurdler** - 100mH specialist with elite flat speed. PKR 100mH >= 85, flat 100m competitive.
**6) Intermediate Hurdler** - 400mH specialist with speed-endurance base. PKR 400mH >= 85.
**7) Dual Hurdler** - Competitive in both 100mH and 400mH. PKR in both >= 82.
**8) Technical Hurdler** - Superior hurdle technique (small flat-to-hurdle differential) but moderate flat speed.

## 4.3 Middle Distance Archetypes

**9) Speed-Based Middle Distance** - Comes from 400m/sprint background, closes fast. PKR 800m >= 85, flat 400m competitive (sub-55 range).
**10) Endurance-Based Middle Distance** - Comes from 1500m/XC background, frontrunner type. PKR 1500m >= 85, strong XC results.
**11) Tactical Racer** - Moderate marks but exceptional championship performer. CKR >= 88, IQKR >= 85.
**12) 800/1500 Dual Threat** - Competitive at both events. PKR in both >= 83.

## 4.4 Distance Archetypes

**13) 5000m Specialist** - Primary 5k runner with speed. PKR 5000m >= 85.
**14) 10000m/XC Grinder** - High-volume distance runner. PKR 10000m >= 85, strong XC career.
**15) Steeplechaser** - 3000mSC specialist with barrier technique. PKR 3000mSC >= 85.
**16) Distance Generalist** - Competitive across 3k/5k/10k/XC. PKR in 2+ distance events >= 82.

## 4.5 Jump Archetypes

**17) High Jumper** - HJ specialist. PKR HJ >= 85.
**18) Long Jumper** - LJ specialist with speed. PKR LJ >= 85.

# SECTION 6: OVERRIDES

Overrides are conditions that force KR adjustment regardless of OPF math output.

- **NCAA Champion Override:** If an athlete wins an NCAA individual championship, her PKR floor is 94 regardless of regular-season marks. Championship performance is the highest-signal data point.
- **Olympic/World Medalist Override:** Floor KR 96.
- **No Wind-Legal Mark Override:** If the only competitive marks are wind-aided, KR is capped at 90 until a wind-legal mark of equivalent caliber is produced.
- **Implement Weight Override:** If there is any ambiguity about implement weight used, evaluation is BLOCKED until confirmed. Women's implements are different from men's.
- **First-Year Athlete Override:** First year of college competition - KR ceiling is soft-capped at 92 unless marks clearly exceed that range. Allow one full season to establish baseline.

# SECTION 7: SYSTEM RISKS

- **Altitude risk:** Athlete's PBs set at altitude venue; marks may not transfer to sea-level competition (sprints/jumps benefit from altitude; distance marks may be worse at altitude).
- **Facility risk:** Athlete trains without proper facilities (no indoor track, no throwing cage, short runways).
- **Coaching risk:** Athlete lacks event-specific coaching at current program.
- **Competition risk:** Athlete has limited access to high-level competitions; marks may be suppressed by lack of competition stimulus.
- **Transition risk:** Athlete recently changed events, technique, or competitive level.
- **Pregnancy/return risk:** Athlete is in post-pregnancy return window; marks are in flux.
- **Age risk (pro only):** Athlete is approaching or past typical peak competitive age for her event group.

# SECTION 8: IMPACT MODIFIERS

Impact modifiers adjust how KR is interpreted in team context (File 03) but do NOT change the KR itself.

- **Multi-Event Versatility Modifier:** Athletes who score in multiple event groups multiply their team scoring value. A sprinter who also long jumps and runs relay is worth more to a team than her KR alone suggests.
- **Relay Utility Modifier:** Athletes who contribute to relay teams add team scoring value beyond their individual KR.
- **Championship Meet Performer Modifier:** Athletes whose CKR >= 88 have outsized value in championship scoring.
- **Event Depth Modifier:** In event groups with fewer competitors at a given level, an athlete has higher marginal team value.
- **Heptathlon Point Multiplier:** Heptathletes inherently score across multiple event sessions and earn significant team points in a single entry.
