# Legend: NCAA D1 Men's Volleyball v1

---

## SCOPE

This legend defines what each KR tier means at the NCAA Division I level for men's volleyball. It is display-only - it interprets KR, it does not produce KR.

All production benchmarks are per-set rates unless noted.

**NCAA D1 Men's Volleyball Landscape:**
Only ~50 NCAA D1 men's volleyball programs exist. This is a dramatically smaller competitive landscape than any other NCAA D1 sport. The programs are concentrated in 5 conferences:

- **MPSF (Mountain Pacific Sports Federation):** UCLA, USC, Stanford, BYU, Pepperdine, UC Santa Barbara, UC Irvine, Grand Canyon, Concordia (Irvine), CSU Northridge. Historically the strongest conference.
- **EIVA (Eastern Intercollegiate Volleyball Association):** Penn State, Ohio State, Loyola Chicago, Lewis, Ball State, Purdue Fort Wayne, McKendree, Lindenwood, St. Francis (PA), Sacred Heart. Strong and growing.
- **Big West:** Long Beach State, UC San Diego, Hawaii, Cal Poly, CSUN (some overlap with MPSF historically - conference realignments ongoing). West Coast strength.
- **MIVA (Midwestern Intercollegiate Volleyball Association):** Loyola Chicago (if not EIVA), Ball State (if not EIVA), Ohio State (if not EIVA), Quincy, Purdue Fort Wayne. Midwest programs. Note: conference memberships shift - verify current alignments.
- **Conference Carolinas:** Barton, Emmanuel, King, Lees-McRae, Mount Olive, North Greenville, Belmont Abbey, and others. Smaller programs, developing conference.

**Note:** Conference memberships in men's volleyball are fluid and have changed frequently. Always verify current conference alignment via web search when evaluating.

## CONFERENCE-SPECIFIC CALIBRATION

Because men's volleyball conferences vary significantly in strength, the legend must be read with conference context.

### MPSF / Top-Tier Conferences
The MPSF is historically the strongest men's volleyball conference. Players in the MPSF face the highest quality of competition. MPSF benchmarks align directly with the legend table above. A KR of 89 in the MPSF is a true conference star.

### EIVA / Big West / MIVA
Strong conferences with programs that regularly compete for national titles. Benchmarks are within 1-2 KR points of MPSF. A KR of 89 in the EIVA is essentially equivalent to 89 in the MPSF.

### Conference Carolinas / Smaller Conferences
Developing conferences with fewer established programs. Production numbers may be slightly inflated due to weaker competition within the conference. However, KLVN normalization handles this at the input level, so the legend reads correctly after normalization.

## POSITION-SPECIFIC LEGEND NOTES

### Setters
Setter benchmarks use assists/set as the primary stat. At D1, an elite setter runs a .300+ team hitting percentage and distributes to 3+ hitters with 3.0+ kills/set each.

### Middles
Middle benchmarks emphasize hitting percentage over kill volume. A D1 middle with .380+ hitting% and 1.40+ blocks/set is elite regardless of kill volume.

### Liberos
Libero benchmarks: 4.5+ digs/set is elite. 2.35+ pass rating against D1 jump serves is elite. Liberos are evaluated on DKR, not AKR or BKR.

### Opposites
The opposite is often the primary scorer in men's volleyball. Opposite benchmarks are weighted toward kill volume and efficiency. 5.0+ kills/set from the right side is elite.

## NCAA TOURNAMENT CONTEXT

The NCAA D1 men's volleyball tournament is small:
- Typically 7-8 teams qualify (auto-bids from each conference + at-large selections)
- Format: pool play followed by single elimination in later rounds
- Host site matters enormously (home-court advantage is massive)
- Only one national champion - the tournament is brutal for seeding

Teams with Team KR of 87+ are typically in the tournament conversation. Teams with Team KR of 90+ are Final Four contenders. The margin between in and out of the tournament is razor-thin due to the small bracket.
