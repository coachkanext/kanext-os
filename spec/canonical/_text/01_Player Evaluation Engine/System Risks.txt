Severity Levels
College System Risks have two severities only:
Minor System Risk
Indicates a real but manageable issue.
Major System Risk
Indicates a significant limitation that materially impacts trust, lineup stability, or system flexibility.
There are no additional tiers.
KR Impact
When triggered, System Risks apply fixed penalties to Overall KR:
Minor System Risk: –1.0
Major System Risk: –2.0
Penalties are:
additive
applied uniformly
not scaled by position or archetype
This ensures consistency and transparency.
What System Risks Do (and Do Not Do)
System Risks DO:
reduce Overall KR
reduce System Fit KR
force separation between similar players
expose problems that attributes alone under-punish
System Risks DO NOT:
change trait scores
change cluster weights
override badge logic
directly assign or remove archetypes
Any archetype restriction occurs naturally through trait requirements.
How to Interpret System Risks
System Risks should be read as:
“This player has a specific issue that meaningfully limits their effectiveness unless actively managed.”
A player with:
0 risks → clean profile
1 minor risk → normal college rotation issue
1 major risk → notable limitation
multiple risks → compounding concerns
College vs Pro Context
College System Risks are intentionally more forgiving than Pro.
wider tolerance bands
smaller penalties
more survivable accumulation
The same risk framework is used at both levels to preserve comparability.

PRO SYSTEM RISK — SPEC EXPLANATION (v1)
Purpose
Pro System Risks exist to identify non-negotiable weaknesses that materially reduce a player’s viability at the professional level.
At the Pro level, one exploitable flaw is enough.
System Risks ensure the model reflects that reality.
They are not projections.
They are survival checks.
Why Pro System Risks Are Different from College
At the professional level:
opponents scout relentlessly
weaknesses are hunted possession after possession
margin for error is extremely small
attributes and KaNeXT badges still measure skill and upside, but they do not capture how quickly a single flaw collapses minutes, rotations, and trust.
Pro System Risks exist to force the model to reflect that compression.
When Pro System Risks Are Applied
Pro System Risks are evaluated after:
Trait scoring
Badge bonuses
Archetype assignment
They are applied as a final corrective layer.
This ensures:
upside is fully credited first
downside is never hidden by strengths
Severity Levels
Pro System Risks use only two severities:
Minor System Risk
Major System Risk
At the Pro level, even “minor” issues matter.
There are no additional tiers.
KR Impact (Critical Difference)
When triggered, Pro System Risks apply fixed penalties to Overall KR:
Minor System Risk: –2.0
Major System Risk: –4.0
Penalties are:
additive
uncapped
applied uniformly
This reflects professional reality:
one flaw can outweigh multiple strengths.
What Pro System Risks Do
Pro System Risks:
materially reduce Overall KR
materially reduce System Fit KR
compress player separation aggressively
determine rotation and roster viability
They are the decisive layer of the model.
What Pro System Risks Do NOT Do
Pro System Risks do not:
alter trait scores
alter cluster weights
override badge logic
excuse weaknesses with upside
KaNeXT badges and attributes cannot cancel a Pro System Risk.
If the risk triggers, the penalty applies.

How to Interpret Pro System Risks
Pro System Risks should be read as:
“This issue will be exploited immediately and repeatedly at the professional level.”
General interpretation:
0 risks → clean pro profile
1 minor risk → playable with protection
1 major risk → rotation concern
2 majors → roster-level problem
This is intentional and realistic.
College vs Pro Continuity
The same System Risk framework is used at both levels to preserve comparability.
What changes at Pro:
tighter thresholds
larger penalties
less tolerance
This allows clear tracking of:
college strengths → pro survivability
