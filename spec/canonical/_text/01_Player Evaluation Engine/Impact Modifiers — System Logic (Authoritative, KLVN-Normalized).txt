KR Impact Modifiers — System Logic (Authoritative, KLVN-Normalized)
Scope and Authority
This section defines Impact Modifiers as a mandatory classification layer within KaNeXT College Basketball Intelligence.
Impact Modifiers are governed exclusvively by KLVN-normalized signals and are applied after KR calculation.
No raw or level-dependent statistics may be used for modifier assignment.
Foundational Invariant (Explicit — Non-Negotiable)
All inputs used for Impact Modifier assignment are KLVN-normalized.
This includes, but is not limited to:
usage and creation load
efficiency metrics
lineup on/off impact
defensive and rebounding rates
Raw box-score values, raw percentages, or unadjusted rates may not be used.
🔒 System Invariant:
Impact Modifiers are evaluated in normalized competitive space, not absolute statistical space.
As a result:
An NAIA player and a High-Major player can qualify for the same Impact Modifier if and only if they generate equivalent normalized impact relative to their environment.
No modifier thresholds are scaled, adjusted, or re-tuned by level.
Purpose
KaNeXT Rating (KR) measures the magnitude of a player’s on-court impact after KLVN normalization.
Impact Modifiers classify the mode by which that normalized impact is produced.
KR answers how much a player impacts winning.
Impact Modifiers answer how that impact is generated.
This separation is intentional and universal across all competitive levels.
System Rules (Hard Constraints)
Each player may be assigned at most one primary Impact Modifier.
Impact Modifiers do not alter KR values, bands, or tiers.
Impact Modifiers are assigned deterministically by Nexus using KLVN-normalized signals only.
Impact Modifiers are not editable at the UI level and are not manually overridden.
Impact Modifiers describe role, not hierarchy.
At equivalent KR, no modifier is inherently superior.
Normalized Input Signals (Required)
All signals below are evaluated post-KLVN normalization, meaning they already account for:
opponent strength
pace and possession environment
role expectations
lineup context
level-specific defensive pressure
No raw inputs are permitted.
Role & Load
USG_K — normalized usage responsibility
AST_K — normalized assist involvement
TOV_K — normalized turnover burden
Efficiency
TS_K+ — normalized efficiency vs team + opponent baseline
Creation Profile
SelfCreate_K% — normalized share of self-generated offense
Lineup Impact
OnOff_ORtg_K — normalized offensive on/off impact
OnOff_Net_K — normalized net impact
Defense / Specialization
BLK_K%, STL_K%, REB_K% — normalized defensive and rebounding leverage
Minimum sample:
MIN ≥ 200 normalized minutes
Below threshold → modifier flagged Provisional
Derived Normalized Scores
Engine Load Score (ELS_K)
Quantifies offensive responsibility in normalized space.
ELS_K = 0.6 × USG_K + 0.4 × AST_K
Weights are locked and may not be modified by level.
Impact Modifier Assignment (Deterministic)
Step 0 — Data Sufficiency
If normalized minutes < 200 or required K-signals missing →
Modifier = Unclassified (Insufficient Data)
1. Primary Engine
Definition
A player whose normalized offensive impact is structurally required for team function.
All K-normalized gates required:
ELS_K ≥ 24
SelfCreate_K% ≥ 45
TS_K+ ≥ −1.0
OnOff_ORtg_K ≥ +4 or OnOff_Net_K ≥ +5
TOV_K ≤ 20
Interpretation
Offense must be organized around the player
Impact remains stable as load increases
Removal produces a measurable structural drop
2. Secondary Engine
Definition
A player who creates advantages in normalized space but does not anchor the offense continuously.
All K-normalized gates required:
ELS_K ∈ [18, 23.9]
SelfCreate_K% ≥ 35
TS_K+ ≥ 0
OnOff_Net_K ≥ +3
TOV_K ≤ 22
3. Force Multiplier
Definition
A player whose normalized impact is driven by efficiency, spacing, defense, and connective play.
All K-normalized gates required:
USG_K ≤ 22
TS_K+ ≥ +2
OnOff_Net_K ≥ +5
Meets at least two normalized multiplier triggers:
Multiplier Triggers (K-normalized):
Shooting gravity (3PA_K volume + efficiency)
Rim or foul pressure (RimA_K or FTA_K)
Defensive playmaking (STL_K% or BLK_K%)
Rebound leverage (REB_K% vs positional baseline)
4. Specialist Anchor
Definition
A player whose normalized impact is elite in one narrow domain and matchup-dependent.
All K-normalized gates required:
USG_K ≤ 20
Exactly one elite specialization signal met (highest value selected)
Situational lineup impact, not universal
Precedence Rules (Locked)
If Primary Engine gates are met → assign Primary Engine
Else if Secondary Engine gates met → assign Secondary Engine
Else if Force Multiplier gates met → assign Force Multiplier
Else if Specialist Anchor gates met → assign Specialist Anchor
Else → Unclassified
Final Lock Statement
All Impact Modifier thresholds operate exclusively in KLVN-normalized space.
No competitive level adjustments are permitted.
Universality across NAIA, D2, and D1 is a system requirement, not a tuning choice.
