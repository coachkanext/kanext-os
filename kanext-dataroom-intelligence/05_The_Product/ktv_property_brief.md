# KTV Property Brief

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

---

## What KTV Is

KTV is the media layer of KaNeXT. Every game, every sermon, every campus event, every piece of content produced inside the ecosystem is captured, distributed, and monetized through KTV. It is not a streaming app. It is the owned media surface for every institution on the platform.

The content is not licensed or aggregated from third parties. It is produced by the institutions themselves as a function of operating on KaNeXT. A school plays a game and KTV captures it. A church holds a service and KTV streams it. A campus hosts a ceremony and KTV archives it. The content is created by institutional activity. KTV is where that content lives.

Every other media platform (YouTube, ESPN+, Facebook Live) requires institutions to push content outward to someone else's surface. KTV keeps it inside the ecosystem. KaNeXT owns the ad inventory. KaNeXT owns the data. KaNeXT owns the audience relationship. No algorithm decides who sees what. No third party sells ads against the content.

Three views across all modes: Featured (curated landing), Library (full searchable catalog), and Live (real-time streaming with auto-save to VOD). Chapter markers, speed controls, captions, casting, clip creation, series management, analytics, and sharing are universal features.

---

## How It Works Per Mode

**Sports.** Most sub-NCAA programs have zero broadcast infrastructure. Games are played in front of 200 people. Recruits cannot watch film. Parents in another state have no way to watch their child play. KTV gives every program their own broadcast network. Every game livestreamed and archived. Highlights auto-clipped. Players build recruiting reels from broadcasts. A podcast network gives programs a media voice they have never had.

KTV also serves as the Film Room inside the Dashboard. Video analysis with auto-tagging, telestration tools, playlists, side-by-side comparison, opponent film, and practice film. Film and broadcast live in the same infrastructure. A game broadcast on KTV is simultaneously archived as coaching film. There is no separate film tool. They are the same system.

**Community.** Churches upload sermons to YouTube where they sit next to conspiracy theories and unwanted ads. KTV gives every organization a clean, branded, ad-free media library. Sermons, worship recordings, teaching series, children's content, and radio broadcasts in one place, owned by them. Members find last week's message in one tap. Visitors watch before they ever walk in.

**Education.** Consolidates lectures, campus event recordings, and promotional content scattered across Zoom, Drive, and YouTube into one role-gated library.

**Business.** Consolidates demos, training, webinars, and investor updates. Gated content captures leads. Internal stays internal.

**Personal.** Creators own every viewer relationship. Subscriber-only gating. Direct monetization through KPay. No algorithm. No platform cut.

## The Dual Role in Athletics Mode

Most products separate broadcast from coaching film. KTV combines them in one governed system.

A game is livestreamed to fans through KTV. That same stream is auto-archived as VOD for anyone who missed it. That same video is simultaneously processed by KVision (the AI computer vision engine) into structured, intelligence-ready data: possessions segmented, timestamps generated, statistics extracted, player and lineup data attributed, actions tagged, and box scores constructed without human intervention.

The coaching staff accesses that same video through the Film Room tab in Dashboard with intelligence overlay, telestration, and analysis tools. The scouting staff pulls opponent broadcasts from KTV for game preparation. Players build highlight reels from the same content. Recruits watch the same broadcasts when evaluating the program.

One capture event. One video file. Broadcast, archive, film, intelligence, and recruiting content all from the same source. The data tier progression (V1 through V4, detailed in the Intelligence Architecture doc) is powered by this integration. KTV plus KVision is what makes automated intelligence data collection possible at scale.

## What It Replaces

KTV consolidates a fragmented landscape of video tools that institutions currently depend on:

In Sports: Hudl (film, $900-$3,300/year), FloSports and Pixellot (streaming subscriptions), YouTube (free but uncontrolled, ad-supported, algorithm-driven). KTV provides film, broadcast, highlight generation, podcast hosting, and scouting film exchange in one governed platform. Mandate institutions receive cameras and platform access as part of the governing body agreement (economics detailed in the Mandate Strategy doc).

In Community: YouTube (sermons next to uncontrolled content), Vimeo (paid hosting), scattered platform accounts. KTV provides a clean branded library with no ads, no algorithm, and institutional control.

In Education: Panopto, Kaltura, Zoom recordings, Google Drive. KTV consolidates all institutional video with role-based access control governing who sees what.

In Business and Personal: YouTube, Vimeo, Loom, Wistia. KTV consolidates with gating, analytics, and direct monetization through KPay.

## Content Compounding

The content library on KTV never shrinks. Every game ever played at every mandate institution is archived permanently. Every sermon, every campus event, every tournament is retrievable. A player's entire career lives on KTV. A coach can review years of an opponent's games. A scout can watch a prospect's development arc across multiple seasons.

The content library compounds across all modes simultaneously. The longer the platform operates, the deeper the archive, the more valuable the intelligence derived from it, and the harder it becomes for any competitor to replicate.

## Strategic Role in the OS

KTV serves three functions simultaneously:

It is the **distribution layer** that puts every institution's content in front of its community.

It is the **acquisition engine** that converts viewers into platform users. Every parent who watches a game is inside the app, one tap from a wallet, a phone number, and a merch store.

It is the **data ingestion layer** that feeds the intelligence system. Every game processed is every game evaluated, every player rated, every team analyzed.

The cameras capture. KVision processes. KTV distributes. The intelligence engines evaluate. KPay monetizes. KTV is not a streaming service added to KaNeXT. It is the media infrastructure that makes the mandate valuable, the intelligence possible, and the platform visible.
