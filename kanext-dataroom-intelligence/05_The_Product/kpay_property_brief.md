## What KPay Is

KPay is the financial layer of KaNeXT. Every user who downloads the app is onboarded into the wallet layer. The wallet ships on Day 1 with Apple Pay and Google Pay integration and the ability to send and receive money globally, subject to partner and regulatory coverage.

KPay is not a payment processor. It is a governed settlement system. The difference matters. A payment processor moves money. KPay governs why money moves, who authorized it, what rules constrain it, and where every dollar goes, then settles the transaction as a downstream consequence of a governed decision.

Every financial action inside KaNeXT follows a single canonical chain: Event, Rules, Authorization, Payment, Settlement, Audit. An Event is any institutional action that may create a financial obligation: a game, a payroll cycle, an NIL agreement, a fundraiser, a tuition payment, a tithe. Rules are constraints attached to that Event (authority, budget, eligibility, split/earmark, and compliance). Authorization captures who approved the decision. Payment executes the money movement. Settlement distributes the funds. The Audit Record is permanent and immutable.

No money moves unless a valid, governed Event exists. This chain is immutable and inspectable. Existing tools move money or report it after the fact. KPay is designed to govern the transaction chain before settlement, prevent violations before they occur, and produce the audit trail automatically as a function of settlement.

## How It Works Per Mode

**Sports.** Program budget tracking, NIL pool with compliance-documented payments, scholarship tracking, booster revenue reconciliation, vendor payments. When a parent buys a $20 ticket at a mandate institution, KPay checks whether the event is active, whether capacity remains, and whether the ticket type is valid. Upon settlement, the ledger entries are generated automatically (illustrative split: 80% to athletics operating budget, 10% to facilities fund, 5% to student life, 5% KaNeXT settlement fee). No human touched it. No accountant processed it. NIL payments are governed Events with contract prerequisites, compliance documentation requirements, and approval chains that must clear before a dollar moves. Player view: NIL earnings with tax documentation in one place.

**Education.** Tuition collection with installment plans, financial aid tracking and disbursement (aid disbursed directly into student wallets), scholarship fund management, department budgets, and institutional accounting. The wallet IS the student's campus card: dining, bookstore, vending, library printing, laundry, and all campus commerce through a tap or QR scan.

**Community.** Fund accounting with restricted/unrestricted enforcement at the transaction level. Money given to the building fund cannot be spent on payroll. The system prevents it. This is not a reporting feature. It is structural enforcement. Designated fund balances, tax receipt generation, clergy-specific payroll (housing allowance), and board-ready financial reports.

**Business.** Full double-entry accounting with chart of accounts. Accounts receivable with aging and auto-reminders. Accounts payable with approval workflows. Payroll with tax withholding and filing. Expense management with receipt capture and approval chains. Budget versus actual tracking. Exportable financial reports. E-commerce settlement from the Store tile auto-reconciles.

**Personal.** Every revenue stream in one wallet. Digital product sales, course revenue, services, subscriptions, tips, and affiliate commissions from the Earn tile all settle into one place. Tax dashboard with real-time estimates by category. Auto-save rules for taxes. Invoice creation. The KaNeXT Card lets creators spend earnings immediately.

## Commerce Economics

When a fan pays by card, KaNeXT charges 5% on the transaction. The card network takes approximately 3% underneath, leaving approximately 2% margin. When a fan pays from their KPay wallet, the transaction is closed-loop. The card network is eliminated. The full margin is captured, or KaNeXT passes savings to the user at 3%, still earning more than the card scenario while incentivizing wallet adoption.

Institutions do not switch to KPay because KaNeXT sells them on it. They switch because every transaction already flows through the KaNeXT settlement layer from the day the platform deploys. The only thing that changes over time is where the money moves: from external processors initially to internal wallet-to-wallet settlement as adoption grows. The governance does not change. Only the plumbing changes. The plumbing change reduces transaction costs dramatically for the institution.

## The Wallet as Product

The target wallet experience includes competitive APY on balances (rate dependent on banking partner and charter status), no minimum balance requirement, and no monthly fees.

The KaNeXT Card (virtual and physical) integrates with Apple Pay and Google Pay. In-ecosystem spending is closed-loop, wallet to wallet. Outside-ecosystem spending runs through the card network, with KaNeXT earning interchange on every transaction.

Target remittance pricing is a fraction of the global average cost of over 6%. International money transfer is a core feature, not an add-on. Actual rates are dependent on corridor, partner, and regulatory requirements. For diaspora communities connected to school network institutions and churches, competitive remittance rates are a primary driver of wallet adoption.
