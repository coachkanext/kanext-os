## SCOPE

This legend defines what each KR tier means at the professional level for men's volleyball. Display-only - interprets KR, does not produce KR.

**Men's Pro Volleyball Landscape:**
There is no US professional men's volleyball league. All professional pathways lead overseas to European, Asian, and South American club leagues. The Italian SuperLega is widely regarded as the strongest men's club league in the world.

## PRO LEGEND NOTES

### Import vs Domestic Player Context
Professional volleyball leagues worldwide limit the number of foreign (import) players per team. Typically 2-3 import spots per roster. This means:
- Import players must be significantly better than available domestic talent to justify the spot
- An American player with KR 85 might be a "Solid Professional" but may not find an import spot in Italy because Italian domestic players at KR 83-84 are preferred for non-import roster spots
- Import players are expected to be immediate impact starters (KR 88+) in top leagues
- Lower-tier leagues have more relaxed import limits and are more accessible for KR 80-85 Americans

### American Player Pro Pipeline
The path for American men's volleyball players to go overseas typically follows:
1. Strong NCAA career (KR 85+, ideally at a top D1 program)
2. USA national team selection or camp invite (adds credibility)
3. Agent representation (essential for navigating overseas contracts)
4. Entry through mid-tier European league (France, Germany) or Asian league (Japan, Korea)
5. Progression to top leagues (Italy, Poland, Turkey) based on overseas performance

### National Team as Validation
USA Men's National Team selection is a strong signal for pro readiness. However, making the national team is not required. Some players go directly from college to overseas clubs without national team involvement.
