# Platform Replication Landscape

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

---

## Purpose

This document maps the comparable companies across each product layer KaNeXT spans. The reader draws their own conclusion about replication cost.

KaNeXT is not one company. KaNeXT is ten product families integrated into one governed architecture. To replicate what one founder has built would require acquiring and integrating companies across sports intelligence, institutional operating systems, AI interface layers, streaming, payments and fintech, real-time stats, video intelligence, gaming, sensor hardware, and governance and compliance.

No single comparable exists. The fragmented market across these layers tells the story.

All valuations and market capitalizations referenced are from public filings and reporting as of April 2026 where available.

## Layer 1: Sports Intelligence

KaNeXT intelligence scope: 27+ sport-specific domains, 250,000+ athlete database, league governance across 15+ amateur and professional leagues, NIL intelligence, market and betting intelligence, Smart Ball hardware intelligence across 9 sports, KLVN normalization across competitive levels, simulation engines, scouting frameworks, team intelligence, and player development intelligence.

### Hudl (Sports Video Analysis)

Hudl is the dominant video analysis platform in sports, founded 2006. It provides video capture, tagging, and review tools used by more than 153,000 active teams across basketball, football, soccer, and other sports.

**Funding and structure:** Private. $230M total raised across 6 rounds. Most recent round: Bain Capital Tech Opportunities growth investment (2020, financial terms undisclosed). Additional stakeholders include Accel, Nelnet, and Vista Equity Partners. 3,500 employees. Acquisitions completed 2024-2025 include StatsBomb (August 2024), Balltime (February 2025), FastModel Sports (March 2025), and Titan Sports (June 2025), expanding Hudl into analytics, wearables, and performance tracking.

**Scope vs KaNeXT:** Hudl does video and analytics for multiple sports. KaNeXT does intelligence (player evaluation, team construction, simulation, governance) at architectural depth that video platforms do not reach. Hudl's core product is video storage and tagging; KaNeXT's is governed intelligence reasoning. Hudl has built to multi-sport breadth through acquisitions; KaNeXT built to 27+ sports through unified architecture.

### Sportradar Group

Sportradar provides sports data services for the betting and media industries. It collects, packages, and distributes real-time sports data globally.

**Public company:** Market capitalization approximately $4.5-5B (April 2026). Trailing twelve-month revenue $1.45B. 2025 revenue grew 17% to €1,290M.

**Scope vs KaNeXT:** Sportradar is a B2B sports data infrastructure business primarily serving betting operators. KaNeXT's market and betting intelligence is one of 10+ product families. Sportradar does not do player evaluation, institutional intelligence, NIL, team operating systems, payments, or hardware. Sportradar is the market infrastructure layer; KaNeXT includes a comparable layer as one component.

### Genius Sports

Genius Sports provides sports technology across three areas: sports technology and services, betting technology and content, and media technology. It partners with leagues including the NFL and NBA for official data distribution.

**Public company:** Market capitalization approximately $1.1-1.5B (April 2026). TTM revenue $669M. Focused on data distribution, betting, and media infrastructure.

**Scope vs KaNeXT:** Comparable to Sportradar but smaller. Same scope characterization. One layer, not the full stack.

### Catapult Sports

Catapult provides wearable performance-tracking technology used by professional and college sports teams for athlete monitoring.

**Public company:** Market capitalization approximately $684M (April 2026). TTM revenue $126M. Focused on sensor wearables and performance data.

**Scope vs KaNeXT:** Catapult is a pure-play wearables hardware plus analytics company. KaNeXT's Smart Ball architecture spans 9 ball sports with integrated intelligence. Catapult is one narrow slice (wearables on the body) of what KaNeXT covers (ball sensors plus wearables plus camera vision plus unified intelligence).

### Stats Perform

Stats Perform provides sports data, content, AI, and technology to media companies, leagues, and betting operators.

**Acquisition:** Acquired by Vista Equity Partners at approximately $2.3B enterprise value.

**Scope vs KaNeXT:** Multi-sport data and content distribution. Similar characterization to Sportradar and Genius Sports. One layer, not the full stack.

### Teamworks

Teamworks is the Operating System for Sports, powering 6,500+ teams globally. Through acquisitions (INFLCR for NIL, ARMS Software for compliance, Smartabase for performance, Zelus Analytics for sports intelligence, Telemetry Sports for analytics, Basepath for NIL), Teamworks has assembled a sports operations platform.

**Private company:** Raised $235M Series F in June 2025 at pre-money valuation exceeding $1.0B, led by Dragoneer Investment Group. Serves 100% of NFL teams, 99% of Division I NCAA athletic departments, 90% of MLB and Premier League, 87% of NBA, 83% of MLS, 81% of NHL, and 65+ Olympic federations across 24 countries.

**Scope vs KaNeXT:** Teamworks is the closest comparable in scope ambition. Teamworks has assembled its stack through acquisitions (INFLCR + ARMS + Smartabase + Zelus + Telemetry + Basepath) while KaNeXT built its stack from unified architecture by one founder. Teamworks does athlete operations, NIL management, communication, compliance, performance, and acquired sports analytics (Zelus). KaNeXT extends into institutional operations beyond athletics (5 modes vs Athletics-only), payments (KPay), streaming (KTV), stats (KStat), video intelligence (KVision), gaming (KPlay), hardware (Smart Ball), enrollment engine, AI interface layer (Dipson), and 27 institutional intelligence modules across People and Talent, Operations and Infrastructure, Leadership and Governance, Growth and External, and Finance and Assets. At valuation of $1B+ for Teamworks's narrower scope and assembled architecture, the comparable establishes a floor for sports operations platforms at KaNeXT's ambition level.

## Layer 10: The Integration Premium

This layer does not map to a single comparable company. It maps to the question: what does it cost to integrate all of the above into one governed architecture?

### Salesforce as Integration Case Study

Salesforce has acquired more than 70 companies and invested tens of billions of dollars in integration over two decades. The Customer 360 initiative remains incomplete by Salesforce's own reporting. Multi-cloud integration is the largest persistent investment category at Salesforce.

### Microsoft as Integration Case Study

Microsoft's M365 and Azure platforms integrate Office, Teams, SharePoint, Dynamics, LinkedIn, and GitHub. Each acquisition cost billions. Integration across the M365 stack is a decade-long ongoing investment.

### The KaNeXT Integration Reality

KaNeXT integrated 10 product layers in one governed architecture built by one founder. The universal RBAC spans all 5 modes. Data flows through Dipson across every tile in every mode. The intelligence is called the same way from an admissions query as from a basketball scouting report. The payment processed at a volleyball game flows through the same wallet that handled tuition.

Companies that attempt this integration through acquisition fail more often than they succeed. The companies in the layers above rarely merge well. Hudl acquires StatsBomb, Balltime, FastModel, and Titan Sports. Teamworks acquires INFLCR, ARMS, Smartabase, Zelus, Telemetry, Basepath. Both companies then spend years integrating acquired products into unified workflows. The acquisition model creates product sprawl; the unified architecture model (KaNeXT) creates coherence.

The integration premium is not a number. It is the architectural coherence that companies attempting acquisition-driven integration struggle to achieve at any price.

## Cross-Layer Summary

Aggregate market value across publicly traded and privately valued companies covering portions of KaNeXT's scope:

**Sports Intelligence Layer:** ~$10-15B (Hudl, Sportradar, Genius Sports, Catapult, Stats Perform, Teamworks combined)

**Institutional Operating Systems Layer:** ~$290B+ (Workday, PowerSchool, Ellucian, Salesforce portions attributable to institutional operations, Microsoft 365 portions)

**Payments and Fintech Layer:** ~$270-290B+ (Stripe, Adyen, Block, Remitly, Chime)

**Streaming, Video Intelligence, Stats, Gaming, Hardware Layers:** ~$15-30B combined across the fragmented comparables

The aggregate market capitalization across the categories KaNeXT spans approaches or exceeds $1 trillion across publicly traded and privately valued companies, each covering portions of what KaNeXT has built in a single integrated architecture.

KaNeXT does not claim a $1 trillion replacement cost. KaNeXT demonstrates architectural breadth across layers whose aggregate market value is in that range. The replication challenge is not one company's worth of R&D. The replication challenge is coordinating investment and integration across a portfolio of acquisition targets that no company has successfully combined.
