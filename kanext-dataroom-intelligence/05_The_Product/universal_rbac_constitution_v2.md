## 1. Universal Constitution

This is the governing ladder across every mode. It does not decide the exact screen someone sees. It decides their power level in the system.

| Level | Authority | Scope | Visibility | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Observer | Personal | Public | None |
| 1 | Personal | Sub-domain | Community | Self |
| 2 | Execution | Program | Tactical | Operational |
| 3 | Program Governance | Domain | Competitive | Tactical |
| 4 | Domain Governance | Global | Strategic | Strategic |
| 5 | Institutional | Institutional | Institutional | Institutional |

**Plain-English meaning:**

- Level 0 = outside observer, mostly public-only
- Level 1 = can manage self or personal actions
- Level 2 = can do actual work in the system
- Level 3 = can run a program or department
- Level 4 = can oversee a major domain
- Level 5 = full institutional authority

---

## 2. Universal Role Ladder

Every user in the system maps to one of 7 universal roles. These roles determine power level. The mode-specific template determines what that power level looks and feels like.

| Role | Label | Authority Level | What They Can Do |
|---|---|---|---|
| R0 | Owner | 5 - Institutional | Full system authority. Creates modes, assigns roles, sees everything. |
| R1 | Top Leader | 4 - Domain Governance | Runs the institution or organization. Strategic decisions, full visibility across all domains. |
| R2 | Department Head | 3 - Program Governance | Runs a program or department. Competitive intelligence, tactical decisions within their domain. |
| R3 | Staff | 2 - Execution | Executes work within the system. Tactical visibility scoped to their lane. Operational decisions only. |
| R4 | Member | 1 - Personal | Active participant. Manages self. Sees own data, community-level visibility. |
| R5 | Follower | 0 - Observer | Passive engagement. Community-level visibility. No decision authority. |
| R6 | Public | 0 - Observer | No relationship to the organization. Public-only visibility. No decisions. |

**Key distinction between R5 and R6:** A Follower has chosen to engage (they follow, subscribe, attend). Public has no relationship at all. Both sit at Observer authority, but Followers may see community-level content that pure Public cannot.

## 3. Mode-Specific Role Templates

The universal role ladder tells you how much power someone has. The mode-specific template tells you WHO they are in that environment and WHAT they see.

Two people on the same universal level get completely different app experiences depending on the mode.

| Role | Universal Label | Athletics | Business | Education | Community | Personal |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| R0 | Owner | Owner | Owner | Owner | Owner | Owner |
| R1 | Top Leader | AD | CEO | President | Senior Leader | You |
| R2 | Department Head | Head Coach | VP | Dean | Director | - |
| R3 | Staff | Asst Coach | Manager | Faculty | Leader | - |
| R4 | Member | Player | Customer | Student | Member | Subscriber |
| R5 | Follower | Fan | Investor | Alumni / Parent / Board | Attendee / Visitor | Follower |
| R6 | Public | Public | Public | Public | Public | Public |

## 4. Mode Breakdowns

### Athletics Mode

| Role | Template | Authority | What They See | What They Can Do |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| R0 | Owner | Institutional | Everything. All teams, all sports, all intelligence, all financials, all operations. | Full system control. |
| R1 | AD | Domain Governance | All sports programs, budgets, compliance, strategic metrics, KR (full), recruiting pipeline. | Strategic decisions across athletic department. Hires coaches. Sets policy. |
| R2 | Head Coach | Program Governance | Own team roster, KR (full for own players), scouting, game ops, recruiting, scholarship allocation, film. | Tactical decisions for their program. Lineup, recruiting offers, development plans. |
| R3 | Asst Coach | Execution | Own team roster (scoped), KR (bands only), film, practice plans, assigned recruiting territory. | Operational tasks. Runs practice, scouts assigned targets, enters data. |
| R4 | Player | Personal | Own profile, own stats, own development plan, own KR (partial - self only), team schedule, community feed. | Self-management. Updates availability, reviews own film, tracks own progress. |
| R5 | Fan | Observer | Public schedule, scores, highlights, player profiles (public), team feed, merchandise. | No decisions. Follows, watches, purchases. |
| R6 | Public | Observer | Public schedule, scores, highlights. | None. |

**KR Visibility:** R0-R2 = full KR. R3 = bands only. R4 = partial self-only. R5-R6 = hidden.

### Business Mode

| Role | Template | Authority | What They See | What They Can Do |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| R0 | Owner | Institutional | Everything. All company data, financials, governance, strategy, cap table. | Full system control. |
| R1 | CEO | Domain Governance | All departments, financials (exact), strategy, governance, team performance, investor relations. | Strategic decisions. Hires VPs. Sets company direction. |
| R2 | VP | Program Governance | Own department, budget (exact), team metrics, competitive intelligence relevant to their domain. | Tactical decisions for their department. Allocates resources, sets targets. |
| R3 | Manager | Execution | Own team, operational metrics, project dashboards, task management. | Operational decisions. Manages team output, runs projects. |
| R4 | Customer | Personal | Own account, own usage, own billing, product features, support. | Self-management. Uses the product. |
| R5 | Investor | Observer | Data room (scoped by investor type), financials (exact for strategic, banded for retail), board pack (if board member). | No operational decisions. Reviews performance, provides governance input if board. |
| R6 | Public | Observer | Public website, marketing materials, press releases. | None. |

**Financial Visibility:** R0-R2 = exact. R3 = department-scoped. R4 = own billing only. R5 = exact for strategic investors and board, banded for retail. R6 = hidden.

### Education Mode

| Role | Template | Authority | What They See | What They Can Do |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| R0 | Owner | Institutional | Everything. All academic programs, financials, enrollment, compliance, accreditation. | Full system control. |
| R1 | President | Domain Governance | All departments, enrollment pipeline, financials, accreditation status, strategic metrics, housing, athletics link. | Strategic decisions. Appoints deans. Sets institutional direction. |
| R2 | Dean | Program Governance | Own school or division, faculty, curriculum, student performance, admissions (for their programs), budget. | Tactical decisions for their academic domain. Hires faculty. Approves curriculum. |
| R3 | Faculty | Execution | Own courses, own students (grades, attendance), academic calendar, advising caseload. | Operational decisions. Grades, advises, teaches. |
| R4 | Student | Personal | Own grades, own schedule, own financial aid, own housing, campus life, community feed, own athletic profile (if athlete). | Self-management. Registers for courses, pays tuition, manages campus life. |
| R5 | Alumni / Parent / Board | Observer | Public programs, events, giving history (own), alumni network. Board members see governance pack. Parents see student-shared data only. | No operational decisions. Donates, networks, provides governance input if board. |
| R6 | Public | Observer | Public website, program listings, admissions info, campus events. | None. |

**FERPA Note:** Student data (grades, financial aid, disciplinary) is visible only to the student (R4 self) and authorized institutional staff (R2-R3 in their lane, R1, R0). Parents see only what the student explicitly shares.

### Community Mode

| Role | Template | Authority | What They See | What They Can Do |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| R0 | Owner | Institutional | Everything. All ministries/programs, financials, giving, membership, operations. | Full system control. |
| R1 | Senior Leader | Domain Governance | All programs, giving reports, membership, growth metrics, strategic planning, compliance. | Strategic decisions. Appoints directors. Sets organizational vision. |
| R2 | Director | Program Governance | Own ministry or program area, volunteers, events, budget for their area, participation metrics. | Tactical decisions for their program. Plans events, manages volunteers. |
| R3 | Leader | Execution | Own team or small group, event logistics, volunteer coordination, attendance. | Operational decisions. Runs events, leads groups, coordinates volunteers. |
| R4 | Member | Personal | Own profile, own giving history, community calendar, groups, resources, announcements. | Self-management. Joins groups, gives, RSVPs, updates profile. |

| R6 | Public | Observer | Public website, service times, location. | None. |

**Giving Visibility:** R0-R1 = full giving reports (aggregate and individual). R2 = aggregate for their program only. R3-R6 = hidden. R4 = own giving history only.

### Personal Mode

| Role | Template | Authority | What They See | What They Can Do |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| R0 | Owner | Institutional | Everything. This is the same person as R1 in Personal mode. System-level access. | Full system control. |
| R1 | You | Domain Governance | Your entire personal brand. All content, all analytics, all connections, all settings. | Everything. You own your space. |
| R2 | - | - | Not used. Personal mode has no department heads. | - |
| R3 | - | - | Not used. Personal mode has no staff. | - |
| R4 | Subscriber | Personal | Subscriber-only content, direct messaging (if enabled), community feed, exclusive updates. | Self-management. Manages subscription, engages with content. |
| R5 | Follower | Observer | Public content, profile, highlights, public feed. | No decisions. Follows, views, shares. |
| R6 | Public | Observer | Public profile, public content only. | None. |

**Personal Mode Note:** R2 and R3 are intentionally empty. Personal mode is about an individual's brand, not an organization. The jump from R1 (You) to R4 (Subscriber) is by design - there is no middle management in a personal brand.

## 5. The Stack

The entire RBAC system reduces to three layers:

**Level -> Role Template -> Screen Experience**

- **Level** (Universal Constitution) = how much power a user has
- **Role Template** (Mode-Specific) = what kind of person they are in that environment
- **Screen Experience** (UI) = what they actually see and do

A Player and a Student can sit on the same universal level (R4 / Level 1 / Personal) but get completely different app experiences because the role template and screen experience are different.

---

## 6. Universal Data Sensitivity Rules

These rules apply across ALL modes:

- **Finance and payment rails:** R0-R2 only (and specific R5 external roles like investors/board with scoped access)
- **Governance and compliance:** R0-R2 only
- **Intelligence (KR, evaluations, scouting):** R0-R2 in Athletics, hidden from R4-R6. R3 sees bands.
- **Personal data (grades, medical, compensation):** Only the individual (R4 self) and authorized leadership (R0-R2 in their lane)
- **Public-facing content (schedule, video, highlights, profiles):** All levels

---

## 7. Dipson RBAC Integration

Dipson enforces RBAC BEFORE assembling any response:

- R0 query: Dipson loads full intelligence + institutional data across all modes
- R1 query: Dipson loads domain-scoped intelligence, strategic data
- R2 query: Dipson loads program-scoped intelligence, competitive data
- R3 query: Dipson loads lane-specific operational data only
- R4 query: Dipson loads self-only data
- R5 query: Dipson responds with community-level information
- R6 query: Dipson responds with public information only

The AI does not have a separate permission level. The AI inherits the permission level of the user asking the question.
