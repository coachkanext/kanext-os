## SCOPE

This legend defines what each KR tier means at the professional beach volleyball level. It covers both men's and women's professional beach volleyball across all major tours: FIVB Beach Pro Tour (Elite 16, Challenge, Futures), AVP Pro Tour, AVP Next, Athletes Unlimited Beach, NVA, and Olympic competition.

This legend is display-only. It interprets KR. It does not produce KR.

## GENDER COMPARISON NOTES

- Men's and women's pro beach volleyball are evaluated on SEPARATE legends. A KR 90 woman and a KR 90 man are both "Tour Starter" quality in their respective competitions, but the raw production benchmarks differ.
- Men's beach features higher kill rates, higher ace rates, and more blocking due to the higher net and greater physical power.
- Women's beach features longer rallies, more defensive exchanges, and more finesse shots.
- Earnings at equivalent KR tiers are generally higher for men due to historical sponsorship patterns and FIVB prize structures, though this gap is narrowing.

## EARNINGS CONTEXT

Beach volleyball earnings are fundamentally different from indoor volleyball or team sports:

**Prize Money:**
- FIVB Elite 16 events: $300K-$500K total prize pool per gender per event (as of 2024-25). Winners earn $30K-$50K per pair.
- FIVB Challenge events: $100K-$200K total prize pool. Winners earn $12K-$20K per pair.
- AVP Pro Tour events: $100K-$200K total per event (varies). Winners earn $15K-$30K per pair.
- Prize money is split between partners (typically 50/50).
- A full FIVB season of 8-10 events can yield $50K-$200K+ for a top pair.

**Sponsorship:**
- Top pairs with Olympic visibility can earn $50K-$500K+ in sponsorships annually.
- Mid-level professionals may earn $10K-$40K in sponsorships.
- Sponsorship is heavily tied to social media presence, personal brand, and market visibility.

**Expenses:**
- Travel: $20K-$40K/year for a full international tour schedule.
- Coaching: $10K-$25K/year for a dedicated coach/trainer.
- Training: $5K-$15K/year (facility access, strength and conditioning).
- Equipment: $3K-$8K/year.
- A pair competing on the full FIVB + AVP circuit can expect $40K-$80K+ in annual expenses.

**Net income:** Only the top 20-30 pairs per gender globally earn a comfortable full-time living from beach volleyball alone. Most professional beach players supplement with coaching, clinics, content creation, or other employment.

## OLYMPIC PATHWAY NOTES

- Each country can qualify up to 2 pairs per gender for the Olympics.
- Olympic qualification is based on the FIVB world ranking points system over a multi-year qualification period.
- For US players, USA Volleyball selects Olympic pairs based on ranking and internal criteria.
- Olympic medal bonuses from USOC/USA Volleyball can be significant ($37,500 for gold as of recent cycles).
- Olympic appearance itself is career-defining for sponsorship and post-career opportunities.

**KR thresholds for Olympic pathway:**

| Below 86 | Not projected for Olympic selection barring extraordinary circumstances. |
