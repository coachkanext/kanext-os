# VoIP and Communications Acquisition Brief

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

---

## Overview

KaNeXT is acquiring an operating VoIP company to power the communications layer of the OS. The acquisition provides existing telecommunications infrastructure, an established customer base producing $3.2M-$4.4M in annual recurring revenue, multi-number architecture, domestic and international call routing, number provisioning at scale, SIP trunking for PSTN connectivity, and WebRTC infrastructure for in-app calling.

The VoIP layer is not designed as a standalone profit center. It is a user acquisition and retention engine. Every user who receives a KaNeXT phone number is also onboarded into the wallet layer (generating float income), has access to content on KTV (generating advertising revenue), and can participate in commerce (generating processing revenue). The phone number is the entry point. The economic value is in everything the user does after receiving it.

Budget allocation: $15M. Existing recurring revenue continues post-acquisition and offsets operating costs while KaNeXT scales the user base. Acquisition compresses years of telecom infrastructure buildout, regulatory setup, and carrier interconnection into one move while providing an immediate revenue-generating customer base.

## The Identity Model

Every KaNeXT user holds one account with one username, which is their universal identifier across the platform. Inside the app, users communicate by username. Messaging, calling, and payments all route through the username. Most users will never type a phone number inside KaNeXT. They search for a name or username the same way they would on any messaging platform.

Phone numbers exist for external connectivity. Each institutional affiliation a user holds can carry its own number: a campus number for a student, a coaching number for a coach, a ministry number for a pastor. These are real phone numbers that receive calls from any phone worldwide. Numbers are assigned automatically when a user joins an institution. No setup, no selection, no configuration.

The purpose is institutional separation. A coach's recruiting calls to prospects are on the coaching number: auditable, compliant, documented. Personal calls stay on the personal number. A pastor's ministry communication stays on the ministry number. These contexts never cross unless the user bridges them intentionally. All communication within each institutional context is governed by RBAC and fully auditable. This serves both privacy and institutional governance. The governance dimension is the more structurally significant: all communication within each institutional context is governed by RBAC and fully auditable.

The username also serves as the payment address. Sending money to a KaNeXT username delivers funds directly to that user's wallet. One identity for communication and finance.

## Institutional Communication

The highest-frequency use of the communication layer is not international calling. It is daily communication inside institutions.

Every institution that deploys KaNeXT replaces fragmented communication tools (separate email systems, separate phone infrastructure, separate team messaging apps, separate group chats) with one governed platform.

At a school network institution: coaches message the team, coordinate with athletic trainers, and contact recruits through the same system. Faculty communicate with students through advising threads. Administrative staff coordinate across departments. The athletic director messages all coaches simultaneously. Recruiting coordinators contact prospects through compliant, documented channels with no personal numbers, no unmonitored texts, and no compliance risk.

At mandate institutions: coaches who currently use personal phones, group texts, and social media DMs to coordinate with players and recruits shift to governed communication inside the app. Opponent scouting, game preparation, travel coordination, and eligibility communication all happen in one place.

At faith institutions: ministry leaders coordinate with volunteers, pastoral care teams communicate with members, and cross-campus church networks connect globally, all inside the app, all governed, all auditable.

This is the daily utility that makes the platform sticky. A user who makes one international call per week but sends 50 messages per day inside their institution is retained by the internal communication, not the international calling.

## The Diaspora Play

Free on-network international calling is the mechanism that converts diaspora families into platform users.

Large Nigerian-American, Caribbean, Latin American, and West African diaspora communities are concentrated across major U.S. metros. These communities currently rely on WhatsApp for communication and Western Union, MoneyGram, or similar services for remittance.

KaNeXT offers a structurally different alternative. Free on-network international calls replace WhatsApp for voice communication, with the addition of institutional identities, a wallet, streaming, and commerce that WhatsApp does not provide. Competitive remittance pricing (targeting a fraction of the industry average cost) replaces traditional remittance providers.

The acquisition path is organic. A student at a mandate institution or network school calls home for free. The parent downloads the app to receive the call. The parent now has a wallet and can send or receive remittance at competitive rates. They tell family members. Family members download the app. The network grows through existing social connections without paid marketing.

## Call Types and Economics

**On-network (KaNeXT to KaNeXT):** Free. Server-to-server data packets. Negligible bandwidth cost. Works globally. This is the core value proposition.

**Off-network outbound (KaNeXT to regular phone):** $0.01-$0.03 per minute via VoIP provider infrastructure. Can be absorbed as a user acquisition cost or passed through depending on volume and strategic priority.

**Off-network inbound (regular phone to KaNeXT):** Supported. KaNeXT numbers are real phone numbers that receive calls from any phone worldwide.

**Number provisioning cost:** Approximately $1 per month per number at standard rates, declining with enterprise volume. At scale, this cost is offset by wallet float, commerce processing, and remittance revenue generated by each user.
