# Athletic Budget by Sport and Level

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

---

## Purpose

The Operating Budget shows athletic operations scaling from Year 1 through Year 5. This document breaks the Year 1 athletic budget down by sport, by level, and by expense category. It is the backup doc behind the athletic expense line in the Capital Deployment and Operating Budget.

Year 1 operates under the Partner Institution Athletics-Only Agreement. Six Varsity sports × two teams (Varsity + Development) = 12 teams across approximately 180-220 athletes. Year 1 is not yet NAIA: the NAIA application window (April 15, 2026) has passed. The next application window is October 15, 2026, with decision in April 2027 and active NAIA membership beginning Fall 2027 (Year 2). Year 1 competitive affiliation is USCAA, NCCAA, Independent, or a combination at the Varsity level, plus GAAC for Development. Year 1 scholarships are strategic (minimal scholarships deployed only where recruiting requires, with reserve for exceptional situations), not NAIA-equivalency caps.

Year 2 activates NAIA membership and scholarship equivalencies. Years 2-5 scale additional sports, full multi-level programs, and full-equivalency scholarship budgets.

KaNeXT pays above NAIA market rates for Varsity head coaches and key assistants. Year 1 focuses on building the Varsity coaching staff at premium salaries with Development coaches supporting the second team in each sport. Cross-sharing across related programs keeps the staff count efficient in Year 1.

## Scholarship Model (Year 1)

Year 1 scholarships are strategic, not NAIA-equivalency caps (NAIA membership is Year 2). Most athletes receive Title IV federal financial aid (Pell Grants and federal student loans) plus standard the partner institution institutional aid. Strategic scholarships deploy only where recruiting requires it, with reserve for exceptional situations.

| Source | Estimated Y1 Cost |
|---|---|
| Strategic scholarships (recruiting-driven, exceptional situations) | ~$1.2M-$1.8M |
| Title IV + institutional aid (standard, not athletic-specific) | n/a (standard financial aid, not athletic budget) |
| **Y1 athletic scholarship total** | **~$1.2M-$1.8M** |

Year 1 total athlete count across 12 teams: approximately 180-220 athletes (approximately 12-18 per team depending on sport). The strategic approach preserves capital for coaching compensation, recruiting infrastructure, NIL reserves, and operational quality while the athletic engine proves out. Savings from strategic scholarship allocation deploy to premium coaching compensation and facilities quality.

### Year 2+ NAIA Equivalency Activation

Upon NAIA membership approval (expected April 2027 for Fall 2027 activation), scholarship structure shifts to NAIA equivalency model. Year 2 scholarship budget scales to approximately $3-5M across the Varsity sport portfolio. Year 5 at full deployment approaches $8-10M across a full NAIA sport portfolio.

NAIA academic exemptions (high-GPA students do not count against scholarship limits) reduce effective scholarship cost per equivalency. The net effect is that NAIA equivalency budgets represent gross maximum; actual net cost depends on academic profile of recruited athletes.

## Year 2-5 Growth Assumptions

Athletic costs scale along the following drivers.

**Coaching salaries** grow significantly in Y2 as NAIA activates and additional assistants are added across all Varsity sports. Y2 coaching: approximately $5.5-6.5M. Y5 estimate at full multi-level deployment across ~16 Varsity sports: approximately $16-20M.

**Scholarships** transition from strategic ($1.5M Y1) to NAIA-equivalency model (Y2+). Y2 scholarships: approximately $3-5M. Y5 at full portfolio: approximately $8-10M. Scholarship cost also grows with COA increases (Y1 the partner institution tuition ~$7,740 rising toward approximately $20,000-$23,500 by Y5 per the Institutional Revenue Model).

**NIL** grows from $0.8M to $4-6M by Y5 as athletic revenue expands and the NIL marketplace matures. NIL allocation is guided by PTV intelligence and funded from athletic revenue as the program grows.

**Travel** grows with expanded sport portfolio, longer-distance competition, conference scheduling (NAIA competition from Y2), and additional Varsity programs. Y5 estimate: $5-7M.

**Equipment** declines from Y1 (capital buildout year) then stabilizes at $700K-$1.0M annually for replacements and new sport additions, rising to $1.5-2M by Y5 with full sport portfolio.

**Game operations** grows significantly in Y4-Y5 with the opening of the Miami Lakes campus (Y4 target) and purpose-built athletic facilities. Y5 estimate: $3-5M.

**Recruiting** grows modestly as the intelligence system makes recruiting more efficient over time. The system identifies high-fit targets from the 250,000+ athlete database, reducing the need for expensive in-person scouting travel.

## Athletic Revenue vs Athletic Cost

Year 1 athletic operations are fully funded by the $13.5M athletics allocation from the Year 1 $33M partnership raise. Athletic revenue (ticketing, guarantee game fees, media, sponsorship) ramps from modest in Year 1 to material by Year 3. The athletic operating budget reaches scale through Year 5 alongside enrollment-driven institutional revenue under the Full IOA.

Athletic revenue projection and athletic operating budget projections are documented in the Institutional Revenue Model and Operating Budget Expense Model. The athletic system operates at an operating deficit in early years, by design: Year 1-3 is the investment period during which the athletic engine is built, the brand is established, and the media/content/data flywheel begins generating the returns described throughout the data room. The deficit shrinks each year as athletic revenue scales, and crossover to athletic operating breakeven occurs between Year 4 and Year 5 per the Operating Budget Expense Model.

The athletic deficit in early years is not a failure. It is the investment period during which the athletic engine is built and the institutional partnership matures.
