## Three Investor Classes

### Class 1: Fund LP ($30M target, 1.5x fixed return)

Participation in KaNeXT Capital Fund I LLC.

- **What you put in:** Minimum investment set at the subscription agreement stage
- **What you get back:** 1.5x your capital as a fixed return obligation
- **Example:** $1M in returns $1.5M. $5M in returns $7.5M. $10M in returns $15M.
- **Equity:** None. This is a fixed-return structure, not an equity position.
- **Timeline:** Return obligation over the fund's stated term
- **Risk posture:** Structured return, not variable equity upside. Investor receives the 1.5x regardless of company outcome above or below that threshold, subject to the fund's ability to pay.

### Class 2: SAFE Participant ($3M total, $100M cap)

Parallel friends and family round. Designed for individual accredited investors at smaller check sizes than the fund.

- **What you put in:** SAFE investment amount of your choice
- **What you get:** Conversion to equity at the next priced round at the lower of: (a) the next round's pre-money valuation, or (b) the $100M cap
- **Example at $100M cap:** $100K in = approximately 0.1% equity at conversion. $500K in = approximately 0.5% equity at conversion.
- **Total SAFE dilution to founder:** Approximately 3% at the $100M cap, across all SAFE participants combined
- **Timeline:** Converts at the next priced round
- **Risk posture:** Equity exposure with cap protection. If company scales, SAFE participants get meaningful upside through equity conversion.

### Class 3: Strategic Equity Partner (selective, negotiated)

Reserved for a small number of strategic partners whose participation brings distribution, credibility, or operational value beyond capital. Equity range 5-20%, negotiated individually.

- **Who this is for:** Named media, operational, and distribution partners bringing specific strategic value
- **What you put in:** Capital plus strategic contribution (distribution, advisory, credibility)
- **What you get:** Negotiated equity position
- **Timeline:** Negotiated at time of transaction
- **Access:** By invitation only. Not an open category.

## What Protects The Capital

Year 1 is asset-light by design. The Year 1 capital is protected by:

- **Intelligence IP.** 50+ intelligence domains, 400+ files, 250,000+ athlete database. Replacement cost across the 10 product layers KaNeXT spans is substantial. See Platform Replication Landscape.
- **Live operational product.** TestFlight deployment with operational OS, Dipson AI, intelligence engines, KTV, KPay running today. Not a prototype.
- **Partnership structure.** Athletics-Only Agreement with a real ABHE-accredited institution with existing operations, enrollment, and revenue.
- **Capital gating.** Capital does not deploy until partnership is executed. No partnership, no deployment.

Miami Lakes campus acquisition and bank charter are Year 2+ capital events raised separately. Year 1 is not secured against future real estate or banking assets.

## Diligence and Next Steps

Interested investors typically proceed as follows.

1. **Review the data room** to understand the thesis, product, institutional strategy, and growth model.
2. **Schedule a founder conversation** to walk through the app, see Dipson live, and discuss alignment.
3. **Request investor-specific documentation** appropriate to your class (subscription agreement for Fund LP, SAFE agreement for SAFE participants, individually negotiated terms for Strategic Equity).
4. **Counsel review and execution** on both sides.

All subscription and SAFE documentation is provided after initial diligence conversations, subject to accreditation verification and counsel review.
