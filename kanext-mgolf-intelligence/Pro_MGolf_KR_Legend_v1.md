# PRO MEN'S GOLF PLAYER KR LEGEND -- v1

## MEN'S ONLY. No LPGA references.

## KEY CONCEPT: SCORING AVERAGE IS THE ANCHOR

Pro golf KR is anchored by adjusted scoring average relative to par across competitive events. Earnings are a secondary interpretive layer - they confirm the KR but do not produce it. A player who earns $5M but averages +1.5 relative to par is not a KR 95 just because the money is there. The scoring average is truth.

Earnings ranges are approximate and reflect typical annual earnings at each tier. Actual earnings vary by number of starts, cuts made, and tournament purse sizes. LIV Golf earnings are separated because the guaranteed-money structure inflates earnings relative to scoring performance.

## PRO PLAYER KR TIERS (DISPLAY / READ-ONLY)

### 98-100 -- Global Apex / Generational Talent
**Role:** Best player in the world or historically dominant season. Wins multiple times including major championships. Defines an era.
**Tour:** PGA Tour #1 in world ranking. Major champion. FedExCup champion.
**Scoring:** Under par to -2.0 or better for the season across all starts.
**PGA Tour Earnings:** $15M-$30M+
**LIV Golf Earnings:** $20M-$50M+ (guaranteed + individual + team)
**DP World Tour Earnings:** $5M-$10M+ (in USD)
**Context:** This tier is reserved for peak Tiger, peak Rory, Scottie Scheffler 2024-type seasons. 1-3 players globally at any given time.

### 95-97 -- Elite Tour Star / Major Contender
**Role:** Top-10 player in the world. Wins PGA Tour events regularly. Major championship contender every year. Multiple top-10s in majors.
**Tour:** PGA Tour top-10 in FedExCup. All starts, all cuts. Ryder Cup / Presidents Cup lock.
**Scoring:** -0.5 to -1.5 relative to par for the season.
**PGA Tour Earnings:** $8M-$18M
**LIV Golf Earnings:** $12M-$25M+
**DP World Tour Earnings:** $3M-$8M
**Context:** The established stars. Xander Schauffele, Collin Morikawa, Wyndham Clark tier.

### 92-94 -- High-Impact Tour Player / Consistent Winner
**Role:** Wins on tour. Top-30 in world ranking. Makes the Tour Championship or equivalent season-ending event. Reliable top-25 finisher.
**Tour:** PGA Tour full exempt, comfortable status. Occasional major contention.
**Scoring:** Even par to -0.5 relative to par.
**PGA Tour Earnings:** $4M-$10M
**LIV Golf Earnings:** $8M-$15M
**DP World Tour Earnings:** $1.5M-$4M
**Context:** Strong tour player who wins occasionally and contends regularly. The backbone of the top tier.

### 89-91 -- Solid Tour Pro / Established Earner
**Role:** Makes most cuts. Occasional top-10. Keeps full tour status comfortably. Reliable professional.
**Tour:** PGA Tour full exempt. DP World Tour top-50 in rankings.
**Scoring:** Even par to +0.5 relative to par.
**PGA Tour Earnings:** $2M-$5M
**LIV Golf Earnings:** $5M-$10M
**DP World Tour Earnings:** $800K-$2M
**Context:** The "solid pro" - makes a good living, plays every week, occasionally pops up on leaderboards. Not a star but firmly established.

### 86-88 -- Tour Regular / Card Keeper
**Role:** Keeps tour status but does not contend for wins regularly. Makes 60-70% of cuts. Works to maintain status each season.
**Tour:** PGA Tour retains card. DP World Tour mid-tier. Korn Ferry Tour top performer (too good for Korn Ferry, marginal on PGA).
**Scoring:** +0.5 to +1.0 relative to par.
**PGA Tour Earnings:** $1M-$2.5M
**LIV Golf Earnings:** $3M-$6M (floor guaranteed)
**DP World Tour Earnings:** $400K-$1M
**Korn Ferry Earnings:** $200K-$500K
**Context:** The bubble zone between comfortably touring and fighting for status. One bad stretch and they are back in Q-School.

### 83-85 -- Marginal Tour / Korn Ferry Anchor
**Role:** Fights for PGA Tour status. Comfortable on Korn Ferry Tour. Wins or contends on developmental tour. Conditional PGA Tour status through Q-School or medical/injury reshuffle.
**Tour:** Korn Ferry Tour full status. PGA Tour conditional. DP World Tour bottom-third.
**Scoring:** +1.0 to +1.5 relative to par.
**PGA Tour Earnings:** $500K-$1.2M (limited starts)
**Korn Ferry Earnings:** $150K-$350K
**DP World Tour Earnings:** $150K-$500K
**Challenge Tour Earnings:** $100K-$250K
**Context:** The grind. These players are good enough to compete professionally but not established enough to relax.

### 80-82 -- Developmental Tour / Journeyman
**Role:** Competes on Korn Ferry, PGA Tour Americas, Challenge Tour, Asian Tour, or similar developmental circuits. Occasionally earns PGA Tour starts through open qualifying.
**Tour:** PGA Tour Americas, Challenge Tour, Asian Tour, Japan Golf Tour. Korn Ferry conditional.
**Scoring:** +1.5 to +2.5 relative to par at tour events.
**PGA Tour Americas Earnings:** $50K-$150K
**Asian Tour Earnings:** $80K-$200K
**Japan Golf Tour Earnings:** $100K-$300K
**Challenge Tour Earnings:** $50K-$150K
**Context:** Professional golf is the job but making a sustainable living is a challenge. Most players at this level need supplemental sponsorship or family support.

### 77-79 -- Mini-Tour / Fringe Professional
**Role:** Competes on mini-tours (PGA Tour Canada, Latin America Tour, Sunshine Tour lower events, US mini-tours). Tries to qualify for higher tours through Q-School each year.
**Tour:** PGA Tour Canada, Sunshine Tour, mini-tours. Q-School cycle.
**Scoring:** +2.5 to +3.5 relative to par at mini-tour events.
**Earnings:** $20K-$80K (often less than expenses)
**Context:** The hardest path in professional sports. These players are extraordinarily talented by any normal standard but are below the threshold for sustainable professional golf. Most exit to club professional, teaching, or golf industry careers within 2-3 years.

### 74-76 -- Below Tour Viability / Amateur-Level Professional
**Role:** Attempts professional golf but cannot sustain tour status at any meaningful level. Monday qualifiers, local events, pro-ams.
**Scoring:** +3.5 to +5.0 relative to par.
**Earnings:** Under $20K (net negative after expenses)
**Context:** Professional aspiration without professional viability. Should strongly consider alternative career paths in golf (teaching, club management, golf media, equipment industry).

### Below 74 -- Not Professionally Viable
Below professional competitive threshold. The gap between this level and any sustainable tour is too large to bridge through development alone.

## EARNINGS CONTEXT NOTES

### PGA Tour Prize Money (2025-26 reference)
- Elevated events (signature events): $20M purse, winner gets $3.6M
- Regular events: $7M-$9M purse, winner gets $1.26M-$1.62M
- Major championships: $15M-$20M purse, winner gets $3M-$4M+
- FedExCup bonus: $25M+ pool distributed to top finishers
- Average PGA Tour player (makes most cuts, no wins): $1.5M-$3M

### Korn Ferry Tour Prize Money (2025-26 reference)
- Regular events: $1M purse, winner gets $180K
- Finals events: larger purses
- Top 30 earn PGA Tour cards

### LIV Golf Structure
- Guaranteed money for all players (no cut, everyone gets paid)
- Individual + team competition purses
- Total purse per event: $20M-$25M
- Individual winner: $4M per event
- Last place individual: $120K+ per event
- Team component adds additional earnings

### DP World Tour Prize Money (2025-26 reference)
- Major co-sanctioned events: $8M-$12M
- Regular events: $2M-$5M
- Rolex Series: $8M-$9M
- Top-10 in Race to Dubai: $1M+ bonus
