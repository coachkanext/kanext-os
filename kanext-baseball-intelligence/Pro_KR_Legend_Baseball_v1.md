# PRO BASEBALL PLAYER KR LEGEND -- v1

## Scope
Universal KR for professional baseball players worldwide on a 0-100 scale. Covers MLB, NPB, KBO, minor leagues, and all professional circuits.

## KEY CONCEPT: ENTRY vs PEAK (Baseball-Specific)

Baseball's draft-to-MLB pipeline is fundamentally different from basketball:

- **Entry KR** = Day 1 as a professional. This is the player's MINOR LEAGUE starting point, NOT their MLB Day 1 value. A first-round draft pick typically starts at High-A or AA, not MLB. Entry KR reflects what the player is when they first enter pro ball.
- **Peak KR** = Best realistic MLB outcome after 3-5 years of minor league development. What the player becomes when fully developed. This is what teams draft for.
- **Current KR** = Present-day evaluation of an active pro. Used for trade/free agent/roster decisions.

Entry KR does NOT correlate with draft position. A polished college senior might have a HIGHER entry KR than the #1 overall pick (a raw high schooler). The #1 pick is drafted for peak ceiling, not Day 1 production.

## DRAFT-RANGE OUTPUT PRIORITY

| Draft Range | Primary KR | What Team Is Buying |
|------------|-----------|-------------------|
| #1-10 | Peak Ceiling | Best talent available. Highest upside over 5-year development window. Org fit matters but ceiling dominates. |
| #11-40 (Competitive Balance) | Peak + Probability | High ceiling but probability of reaching it matters. Development track record of org weighs in. |
| #41-80 | Median Outcome | Safer bets. College performers with clearer floors. Can contribute to MLB within 2-3 years. |
| #81-160 | Median + Role | Role players, specialists, org depth. Some will surprise. |
| #161+ / UDFA | Entry KR + Upside Tool | Lottery tickets. One elite tool (velo spike, raw power) or polished college arms who eat innings. |

## TIMELINE: DRAFT TO MLB

| Draft Profile | Typical Timeline to MLB | Entry Level |
|--------------|------------------------|-------------|
| HS hitter (1st round) | 4-5 years | Single-A or Complex League |
| HS pitcher (1st round) | 3-5 years | Single-A |
| College junior (D1 Power) | 2-3 years | High-A or AA |
| College senior (polished) | 1-2 years | AA or AAA |
| College closer/reliever | 1-2 years | High-A to AAA |
| International signing (Latin America, 16-17 yr old) | 5-7 years | Dominican Summer League / Complex |

## PRO PLAYER KR TIERS -- HITTER TRACK

### 98-100 -- Generational / Inner-Circle HOF
**Role:** League-defining hitter. MVP perennial. Warps opposing pitching staffs.
**Production:** .320+/.420+/.600+, 40+ HR, 130+ RBI, 6+ bWAR. Triple Crown contender.
**MLB Salary:** $35M-$55M+ AAV
**Draft context:** PEAK tier only. Appears in projections for generational HS talents. If a prospect's peak maps here, they go #1 regardless.

### 94-97 -- Elite Franchise Hitter
**Role:** Perennial All-Star. Top-10 MLB hitter. 5+ bWAR consistently.
**Production:** .300+/.390+/.550+, 30-40 HR, 100+ RBI.
**MLB Salary:** $25M-$45M AAV
**Draft context:** PEAK for top-5 picks. What the #1 overall hitter is drafted to become.

### 90-93 -- All-Star Caliber Hitter
**Role:** Core hitter on a contending team. 3-5 bWAR. Closes games.
**Production:** .280+/.365+/.500+, 25-32 HR, 85-100 RBI.
**MLB Salary:** $15M-$30M AAV
**NPB/KBO:** Franchise import or domestic MVP.
**Draft context:** PEAK for picks #3-10. 3-year outcome for top picks developing well.

### 86-89 -- Quality MLB Regular
**Role:** Everyday starter on a good team. 2-3 bWAR.
**Production:** .265+/.340+/.445+, 18-25 HR, 70-85 RBI.
**MLB Salary:** $8M-$18M AAV
**Draft context:** ENTRY for polished college juniors drafted in rounds 1-2. MEDIAN outcome for mid-first-round college bats.

### 82-85 -- Solid MLB Contributor
**Role:** Starting lineup or strong platoon. 1-2 bWAR.
**Production:** .250+/.320+/.400+, 12-18 HR, 55-70 RBI.
**MLB Salary:** $3M-$10M AAV
**Draft context:** ENTRY for most college starters. MEDIAN for later first-round.

### 78-81 -- MLB Fringe / AAA Star
**Role:** Up-and-down player. AAA everyday starter. MLB spot start / September call-up.
**Production (AAA):** .290+/.370+/.480+, 20+ HR at AAA.
**Production (MLB):** .230-.250, replacement-level when called up.
**MLB Salary:** $720K-$3M (league min to pre-arb)
**Draft context:** ENTRY for most drafted college hitters in rounds 2-5.

### 73-77 -- Upper Minor League
**Role:** AA-AAA player. Developing or organizational depth.
**Production (AA):** .270+/.350+/.440+, 15-20 HR.
**Draft context:** ENTRY for HS draft picks in years 1-2.

### 68-72 -- Minor League Regular
**Role:** A-ball to AA. Early development or org filler.
**Production (High-A):** .260+/.330+/.400+.
**Draft context:** ENTRY for most HS hitters and late-round college picks.

### 60-67 -- Low Minors / Extended Spring
**Role:** Complex league to Low-A. Very raw.
**Draft context:** ENTRY for international signings (16-17 year olds) and raw HS picks.

### Below 60 -- Non-Professional
Not viable at professional level.

## PRO PLAYER KR TIERS -- PITCHER TRACK

### 98-100 -- Generational / Inner-Circle HOF Arm
**Role:** Cy Young perennial. 200+ K, sub-2.50 ERA in a full season. Anchors a rotation for a decade.
**Production:** sub-2.50 ERA, 11.0+ K/9, sub-1.00 WHIP, 200+ IP, 6+ bWAR.
**MLB Salary:** $35M-$50M+ AAV
**Draft context:** PEAK tier only. Reserved for once-a-decade arms.

### 94-97 -- Elite Frontline Starter / Dominant Closer
**Role:** Cy Young candidate. Top-5 MLB pitcher. Ace of aces.
**Starter Production:** 2.50-3.00 ERA, 10.0-11.0 K/9, sub-1.05 WHIP, 190+ IP.
**Closer Production:** sub-2.00 ERA, 13.0+ K/9, 40+ SV, sub-0.90 WHIP.
**MLB Salary:** $25M-$42M AAV
**Draft context:** PEAK for top-5 pitching picks.

### 90-93 -- All-Star Caliber Arm
**Role:** #1/#2 starter on a contending team. 3-5 bWAR.
**Starter Production:** 3.00-3.50 ERA, 9.0-10.0 K/9, 1.05-1.15 WHIP, 170+ IP.
**Closer Production:** 2.00-2.80 ERA, 11.0-13.0 K/9, 30+ SV.
**MLB Salary:** $15M-$28M AAV
**Draft context:** PEAK for first-round college arms.

### 86-89 -- Quality MLB Starter / Elite Reliever
**Role:** Solid mid-rotation starter or high-leverage RP. 2-3 bWAR.
**Starter Production:** 3.50-4.00 ERA, 8.0-9.0 K/9, 1.15-1.25 WHIP, 150+ IP.
**Reliever Production:** 2.80-3.40 ERA, 10.0+ K/9, setup role.
**MLB Salary:** $8M-$18M AAV
**Draft context:** ENTRY for polished college arms (3rd-year juniors with 3 pitches). MEDIAN for first-round college pitchers.

### 82-85 -- Back-End Starter / Quality Reliever
**Role:** #4/#5 starter or quality middle relief. 1-2 bWAR.
**Starter Production:** 4.00-4.50 ERA, 7.0-8.0 K/9, 1.25-1.35 WHIP, 130+ IP.
**Reliever Production:** 3.40-4.00 ERA, 8.5+ K/9.
**MLB Salary:** $3M-$10M AAV
**Draft context:** ENTRY for most drafted college pitchers. MEDIAN for later rounds.

### 78-81 -- MLB Fringe / AAA Dominant
**Role:** Spot starter, long relief, AAA rotation anchor.
**AAA Production:** 3.00-3.80 ERA, 9.0+ K/9.
**MLB Production:** 4.50-5.20 ERA when called up. Useful in a pinch.
**MLB Salary:** $720K-$3M
**Draft context:** ENTRY for most drafted pitchers in rounds 3-10.

### 73-77 -- Upper Minor League Pitcher
**Role:** AA-AAA developing arm or organizational depth.
**AA Production:** 3.50-4.20 ERA, 8.0+ K/9.
**Draft context:** ENTRY for HS arms in years 2-3.

### 68-72 -- Minor League Arm
**Role:** A-ball to AA. Early development.
**High-A Production:** 4.00-4.80 ERA.
**Draft context:** ENTRY for HS pitchers and late-round college arms.

### 60-67 -- Low Minors / Raw Arm
**Role:** Complex to Low-A. Learning to pitch at a professional level.
**Draft context:** ENTRY for international signings and raw HS arms.

### Below 60 -- Non-Professional
Not viable at professional level.
