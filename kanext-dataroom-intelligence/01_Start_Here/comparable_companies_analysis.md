# Comparable Companies Analysis

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

---

## Purpose

This document presents the market context for KaNeXT's intelligence architecture. The landscape of sports intelligence, institutional software, and adjacent categories is highly fragmented. Different companies solve different parts of what KaNeXT has built in a single integrated architecture. This document presents the public and private valuations of those companies, what each covers, and lets the reader evaluate KaNeXT's scope in that context.

KaNeXT does not claim a specific replacement cost or enterprise value in this document. It presents the market data and lets the reader evaluate what one integrated intelligence operating system across 50+ domains, 27+ sports, institutional functions, league governance, NIL, market intelligence, and hardware is worth relative to a landscape where these capabilities exist as separate companies each valued in the hundreds of millions to billions of dollars.

All valuation data cited reflects publicly reported figures as of research date April 2026.

## Category 1: Governed Intelligence Operating Systems

Companies that build governed intelligence infrastructure that institutions depend on operationally. Closest structural parallel to what KaNeXT has built across institutional and sport intelligence.

### Palantir Technologies (NASDAQ: PLTR)
- **Market capitalization:** ~$350B (April 2026)
- **Revenue (TTM):** $4.47B
- **Cumulative R&D since 2003 founding:** $4.5-6B estimated
- **Employees:** ~4,430
- **Coverage:** Government intelligence (55% of revenue), commercial enterprise intelligence (45%). Platforms: Gotham (government), Foundry (enterprise), Apollo (deployment), AIP (AI platform).
- **Scope limitation:** Government and commercial enterprise intelligence only. Does not cover sports, education institutional operations, athletics, or consumer-facing applications.

### Databricks
- **Valuation:** ~$62B (private)
- **Coverage:** Data lakehouse and AI platform for enterprise data engineering and analytics. Foundational data infrastructure layer.
- **Scope limitation:** Infrastructure platform; not a vertical intelligence system for any specific industry.

### Snowflake (NYSE: SNOW)
- **Market capitalization:** ~$60B
- **Coverage:** Cloud data warehousing and analytics platform for enterprises.
- **Scope limitation:** Horizontal data infrastructure; not a vertical intelligence operating system.

## Category 2: Sports Intelligence and Analytics

Companies focused on sports data, analytics, video, and performance intelligence. The most fragmented competitive landscape in the KaNeXT scope.

### Hudl (Private)
- **Revenue:** $730M+ (2024)
- **Customers:** 325,000+ teams globally
- **Employees:** 3,500
- **Total raised:** $230M across six rounds; valuation undisclosed as private. Founded 2006. Major investor: Bain Capital Tech Opportunities.
- **Coverage:** Video analysis, smart camera hardware, livestreaming, recruiting support, performance analytics across 30+ sports. Recent acquisitions include StatsBomb (2024), BlueFrame, Titan Sports (2025), FastModel (2025), Balltime (2025).
- **Scope limitation:** Video-centric workflow. Does not include institutional operations, league governance, NIL management, market intelligence, or comprehensive evaluation architecture across the full sport-athletic-institutional stack.

### Sportradar Group (NASDAQ: SRAD)
- **Market capitalization:** ~$4.5-5B (April 2026)
- **Revenue (TTM):** $1.45B
- **Coverage:** Sports betting data, media data services, official league data partnerships (Premier League, La Liga, WTA, FIBA). Integrity services, iGaming content.
- **Scope limitation:** Betting and data distribution focused. Acquired Synergy Sports for basketball analytics. Does not cover institutional intelligence, athlete development evaluation, recruiting intelligence at program level, or academic-institutional integration.

### Stats Perform (Private, Vista Equity Partners)
- **Total raised:** $745M
- **Coverage:** Sports data collection (500,000+ matches, 3,900 competitions annually), AI/ML predictive models. Owns Opta brand. Clients: ESPN, Google, Bet365, William Hill, FIBA, La Liga, WTA, Serie A, Premier League.
- **Employees:** 3,270
- **Scope limitation:** Sports data and predictive analytics focused. Does not cover institutional operations, NIL intelligence, athlete life-cycle development, or cross-institutional deployment.

### Genius Sports (NYSE: GENI)
- **Market capitalization:** ~$2-3B
- **Coverage:** Sports data, betting technology, fan engagement. Went public via $1.5B SPAC merger (2021). Acquired Second Spectrum (spatial sports tracking) for approximately $200M.
- **Scope limitation:** Betting, broadcasting, and fan engagement focused. Does not cover institutional operations or comprehensive evaluation architecture.

### Catapult Sports (ASX: CAT)
- **Market capitalization:** ~$500M-$1B (Australian publicly traded)
- **Coverage:** Wearable performance monitoring hardware, GPS tracking, biomechanics. Elite and professional sports focus.
- **Scope limitation:** Sensor hardware and physical performance tracking. Does not include video analysis, evaluation systems, recruiting, or institutional operations.

### Synergy Sports (Now part of Sportradar)
- **Acquired for:** ~$100M estimated (2022)
- **Coverage:** Basketball video and analytics; expanded to baseball, softball, volleyball. Professional league partnerships.
- **Scope limitation:** Video-centric analytics across limited sport portfolio. Does not cover player evaluation modeling, institutional operations, league governance, or broader intelligence architecture.

## Category 3: Institutional Education Software

Companies that build enterprise software for higher education institutions. Structural parallel to KaNeXT's institutional operating system layer.

### Ellucian (Private, Blackstone + Vista Equity Partners)
- **Acquisition valuation:** $5B+ (June 2021 Blackstone/Vista purchase)
- **Previous acquisition:** $3.5B (2015 TPG/Leonard Green purchase)
- **Coverage:** Higher education ERP and SIS. Serves 2,800+ institutions across 50 countries, 20M+ students. Acquired Anthology's SIS/ERP business December 2025 after Anthology's Chapter 11 bankruptcy, adding 260+ institutions.
- **Scope limitation:** Administrative ERP and SIS. Does not cover athletics, intelligence, evaluation, media, communications, or consumer-facing platform functions.

### Anthology (Filed Chapter 11, 2025)
- **Outcome:** Bankruptcy reorganization; SIS/ERP business sold to Ellucian December 2025
- **Prior scope:** Higher education ERP, SIS, and learning management (Blackboard). Serves 150M+ users prior to reorganization.
- **Scope limitation:** Administrative education software. Financial distress illustrates that even category leaders at significant scale face existential risk under fragmented vendor models.

### Workday (NASDAQ: WDAY)
- **Market capitalization:** ~$65-70B
- **Coverage:** Enterprise HR, finance, and planning. Expanding into higher education administration.
- **Scope limitation:** Horizontal enterprise software adapted for higher education; not purpose-built for the integrated academic-athletic-institutional-community model.

### Handshake (Private)
- **Latest valuation:** $3.5B
- **Coverage:** Career services, student-employer matching, campus recruiting.
- **Scope limitation:** Career services niche only. Does not cover athletics, evaluation, academic operations, or broader institutional functions.

## Category 4: Athlete Management and NIL Intelligence

Companies serving the athlete monetization and representation layer. New category emerged post-NCAA NIL policy change (July 2021). Total NIL market projected to reach $2.5B+ annually.

### Teamworks / INFLCR (Private)
- **Scope:** Teamworks is the athlete management platform; acquired INFLCR (NIL content management) in 2019. Appointed by NCAA as the third-party administrator for the NIL registry (2024).
- **Valuation:** Estimated $500M+ range based on venture funding and NCAA registry selection.
- **Coverage:** Athlete content, communication, NIL compliance tracking, athlete life cycle operations. Serves professional sports, collegiate athletics, Olympic/National Governing Bodies, military/tactical organizations.
- **Scope limitation:** Operations and compliance focused. Does not include evaluation intelligence, recruiting analytics at program depth, institutional integration beyond athletics operations, or cross-sport intelligence architecture.

### Opendorse (Private)
- **Latest raise:** $20M Series B (2024)
- **Coverage:** NIL marketplace, compliance, fair market value assessment, brand deal facilitation. Serves 100,000+ college athletes, $250M+ transactions processed since 2021.
- **Scope limitation:** NIL marketplace only. Does not cover athletic intelligence, recruiting evaluation, institutional operations, or coaching tools.

### Learfield (Private, private equity-backed)
- **Coverage:** Collegiate multimedia rights, sponsorships, and NIL. Operates CLC brand (trademark licensing for colleges).
- **Valuation:** $1B+ estimated. Private equity portfolio company.
- **Scope limitation:** Multimedia rights and sponsorship management. Does not include evaluation, recruiting intelligence, or institutional operations.

### Altius Sports Partners (Private)
- **Coverage:** NIL strategy, collective development, compliance advisory.
- **Scope limitation:** Consulting and advisory services; not an intelligence platform.

## What Makes KaNeXT Structurally Different

Each of the comparables above solves one component of the operational need. KaNeXT solves them as one architecture.

**No existing company combines:**
- 27+ sport domains at evaluation depth (beyond Hudl's video, beyond Synergy's basketball-only, beyond Stats Perform's data)
- Institutional operations intelligence (comparable to the scope Palantir covers in government/enterprise, applied to education institutions)
- League governance across NCAA D1/D2/D3, NAIA, NJCAA, USCAA, NCCAA, professional leagues worldwide
- NIL intelligence integrated with recruiting and compliance
- Market intelligence for betting and gaming integration
- Hardware intelligence across 9 ball sports (Smart Ball)
- Media infrastructure (KTV), financial infrastructure (KPay), communication infrastructure (VoIP)
- Consumer-facing platform (institutions, families, athletes, fans, communities) built on the same architecture

Every comparable above is a partial solution requiring integration with other partial solutions. Institutions currently buy Hudl plus Ellucian plus Teamworks plus separate enrollment software plus separate commerce plus separate video plus separate compliance plus separate recruiting plus separate media. Each integration point is a cost, a failure point, and an architectural compromise.

KaNeXT is the first platform building the integrated alternative. The operational thesis is that the integration premium (reduced vendor fragmentation, unified data architecture, governed intelligence across all domains) produces value that exceeds the sum of the components.
