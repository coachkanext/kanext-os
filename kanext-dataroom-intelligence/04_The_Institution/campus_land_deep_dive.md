## Purpose

This document details the 596-acre Miami Lakes campus acquisition thesis. The acquisition is a Year 2+ capital event, separate from the Year 1 $33M partnership raise. It is included in the data room because the Miami Lakes campus is a central component of the long-term institutional strategy, and investors will ask about the path to the flagship campus.

## The 596-Acre Parcel

**Location:** Miami Lakes, Miami-Dade County. Between I-75 and Florida's Turnpike. Highway frontage on two major corridors.

**Seller context:** Graham Companies has been the primary landowner and developer in Miami Lakes since the 1960s, developing a 3,000-acre master-planned community from what was originally a 7,000-acre dairy farm. The company remains privately held and continues to hold significant undeveloped acreage in the area. The parcel is not listed for sale. Acquisition is an off-market negotiated transaction with a generational hold-and-develop family.

**Target price:** Approximately $420M, implying approximately $705,000 per acre.

**Market context:** Miami-Dade County has the highest land values in Florida. Small parcels (under 5 acres) average $250,000-$450,000 per acre. Larger undeveloped parcels (100+ acres) trade at lower per-acre prices due to scale, but parcels with highway frontage in high-growth submarkets command premiums. A 2020 land swap in the Miami Lakes area between Triple Five and Graham Companies valued parcels at approximately $500,000 per acre. Accounting for 5-8% annual appreciation since 2020, highway frontage premium, and the strategic nature of this being Graham's last major development parcel, the realistic market range is $400,000-$750,000+ per acre. The $420M target price provides negotiation headroom at the upper end of this range, reflecting the off-market premium expected when approaching a seller who is not actively looking to sell.

Miami-Dade taxable property values have risen approximately 8.5-12.7% annually from 2023 through 2025, with countywide taxable values reaching $511.8 billion in 2025. Miami-Dade land sales volume surged 111% year-over-year in 2025. The land appreciates whether or not the institutional thesis executes because it is Miami-Dade commercial real estate in a market with structural demand driven by population growth, migration, and limited supply.

## Why the Full 596 Acres

The entire parcel is acquired for three strategic reasons.

**First, to eliminate encumbrance risk.** A partial purchase creates boundary lines with adjacent landowners, access disputes, easement complications, and neighboring development that KaNeXT does not control. Owning the full parcel means no third party can build next to the campus in ways that compromise institutional planning.

**Second, to lock in pre-development pricing.** Once vertical construction begins on any portion of the parcel, the remaining acreage appreciates significantly. Adjacent undeveloped land near an active university campus in Miami-Dade County would command a substantial premium over pre-construction pricing. Buying the full site before breaking ground secures the entire footprint at current market rates.

**Third, long-term expansion capacity.** The campus does not need to use all 596 acres on day one. Phase 1 construction occupies a fraction of the site. The remaining acreage provides decades of expansion capacity without requiring additional land acquisition.

## Comparable Campus Investments

**Grand Canyon University.** GCU invested $1.9B in campus modernization and expansion between 2009 and 2024. The campus grew from approximately 100 acres to over 300 acres with 72 new structures. Enrollment grew to over 125,000 total.

**High Point University.** HPU grew from 1,450 students in 2005 to 6,335 on-campus by 2024 with over $3B in total campus investment across approximately 500 acres. Tuition rose from approximately $16,000 to $45,312 as campus quality improved.

**Liberty University.** Liberty reached over 140,000 students by 2024 with annual revenue of $1.2B and $3.5B in net assets across approximately 7,000 acres.

The Miami Lakes acquisition and subsequent phased construction are the KaNeXT equivalent of the campus-investment component that drove GCU, HPU, and Liberty to their current scale. KaNeXT starts with structural advantages (integrated OS, athletic engine, intelligence architecture) that none of those institutions had.

## Phased Construction and Relocation

Upon acquisition, construction phases across Years 2 through 4. The partner institution continues operations at South Florida through the construction period. Phased relocation begins as Miami Lakes buildings open. Majority operations shift to Miami Lakes in Year 4. Full Miami Lakes operations in Year 5.

Construction capital is a separate capital event from the acquisition capital and from the Year 1 partnership raise. Construction financing structure (balance of equity capital, debt, and operating revenue) is determined at the time of the Year 2+ capital events based on operational results and market conditions.
