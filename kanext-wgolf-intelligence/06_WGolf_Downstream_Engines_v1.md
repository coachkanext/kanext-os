# DEVELOPMENT INTELLIGENCE ENGINE

## Purpose
Projects player growth trajectory, identifies development priorities, and creates actionable improvement roadmaps. Consumes player KR and component KRs from upstream. Never modifies them.

## Development Projection Model

### KR Growth Rates by Level and Age

| Level | Typical Annual KR Growth (Ages 18-22) | Ceiling Growth (Elite Development) |
|-------|---------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| NCAA D1 Power 4 | +1.5 to +3.0 per year | +4.0 per year (rare) |
| NCAA D1 Mid-Major | +1.0 to +2.5 per year | +3.5 per year |
| NCAA D1 Low-Major | +1.0 to +2.0 per year | +3.0 per year |
| NCAA D2 | +0.8 to +2.0 per year | +2.5 per year |
| NAIA | +0.8 to +1.8 per year | +2.5 per year |
| NJCAA | +1.0 to +2.5 per year | +3.0 per year (often higher due to rapid development at young ages) |

### KR Growth Rates for Professional Players

| Tour Level | Typical Annual KR Growth (Ages 22-28) | Notes |
|-----------|---------------------------------------|-------|
| LPGA Tour | +0.5 to +1.5 per year | Growth slows at the highest level. Physical peak 24-30. |
| Epson Tour | +1.0 to +2.5 per year | Higher growth as players develop on the developmental tour |
| LET | +0.5 to +2.0 per year | Varies by competition level within LET |
| KLPGA | +0.5 to +1.5 per year | Strong development infrastructure in Korean golf |
| JLPGA | +0.5 to +1.5 per year | Competitive tour with good development |

### Development Ceiling Calculation
**Player KR Ceiling = Current KR + (Annual Growth Rate x Remaining Development Years) + Coaching Multiplier**

Remaining Development Years = Years until physical peak (typically age 27-30 for women's golf)
Coaching Multiplier = +0 to +3 based on coaching quality and development environment

**Maximum ceiling growth from any starting point: +12 KR over 4 years** (for college players).
**Maximum ceiling growth for professionals: +8 KR over 4 years** (growth rate slows).

## Development Priority Identification

### Gap Analysis Process
1. Score all 5 component KRs
2. Identify the largest gap between current KR and archetype ceiling KR for each component
3. Weight gaps by OPF importance:
   - BKR gap x 0.35 = weighted ball-striking development priority
   - SKR gap x 0.25 = weighted short game development priority
   - CKR gap x 0.20 = weighted course management development priority
   - MKR gap x 0.12 = weighted mental game development priority
   - AKR gap x 0.08 = weighted physical development priority
4. Rank by weighted gap (highest = top priority)

### Development Responsiveness by Component

| Component | Development Responsiveness | Timeline to Measurable Improvement | Notes |
|-----------|---------------------------|--------------------------------------|-------|
| BKR | Moderate | 3-12 months | Swing changes take time. GIR% improvement is gradual. |
| SKR | High | 1-6 months | Short game responds fastest to focused practice. Chipping, bunker play, and scrambling improve quickly with quality coaching. |
| CKR | Moderate-High | 2-8 months | Course management improves with experience and coaching. Mental models for decision-making develop with deliberate practice. |
| MKR | Low-Moderate | 6-24 months | Mental game is the slowest to develop. Requires sports psychology, competitive reps, and maturation. Cannot be rushed. |
| AKR | Moderate | 3-12 months | Physical fitness, flexibility, and power respond to training. Distance gains of 10-15 yards possible with proper physical training. |

# PRO TRANSITION INTELLIGENCE ENGINE

## Purpose
Evaluates professional readiness, identifies the optimal professional pathway, and projects career earnings and tour status. Consumes player KR from upstream. Never modifies it.

## Professional Pathway Overview (Women's Golf)

### LPGA Tour Access Points

**1. LPGA Q-Series (Primary)**
The main qualification pathway. Held annually at Magnolia Grove (Mobile, Alabama).
- Format: 8 rounds (144 holes) of stroke play over 2 stages
- Entry: Open to professionals and eligible amateurs
- Top finishers earn LPGA Tour status for the following season
- Typically 40-50 players earn some level of LPGA status
- Categories: Full exempt status (top ~20), conditional status (next ~25), Epson Tour status (remaining qualifiers)

**2. Epson Tour Promotion**
- Top 10 on the Epson Tour season-ending money list earn LPGA Tour cards
- The primary "earn your way up" pathway
- Players who dominate the Epson Tour (typically KR 80+) graduate to the LPGA

**3. Sponsor Exemptions**
- LPGA events offer limited sponsor exemptions to non-exempt players
- Useful for amateurs and conditional-status players to gain LPGA experience

**4. Monday Qualifiers**
- Some LPGA events have Monday qualifiers for non-exempt players
- Extremely competitive: 100+ players competing for 4-6 spots

**5. College Direct**
- Exceptional college players may skip the developmental tour and go directly to LPGA Q-Series
- Typical profile: All-American, top 5 in individual NCAA Championship, scoring average under 71.5

### Epson Tour (Developmental)
- 20+ events per season
- Purses: $200K-$300K per event (growing)
- Top 10 on money list earn LPGA promotion
- Q-Series finishers who don't earn full LPGA status often receive Epson Tour status
- The primary development and proving ground for future LPGA players

### Ladies European Tour (LET)
- 25+ events across Europe, Middle East, Africa, and Asia (some joint with LPGA)
- Dual-ranking events count for both LET and LPGA rankings
- LET Q-School provides access (held annually, typically in Morocco or Spain)
- Strong European players often play both LET and LPGA
- Solheim Cup representation is a significant career milestone

### KLPGA (Korean Ladies Professional Golf Association)
- One of the strongest women's tours globally
- South Korea produces a disproportionate share of elite women's golfers (30-40% of LPGA Tour winners in recent years)
- KLPGA Tour has 25+ events with growing purses
- KLPGA qualification is through their own Q-School
- Many KLPGA players eventually transition to the LPGA Tour
- Strong domestic sponsorship and fan base

### JLPGA (Japan Ladies Professional Golf Association)
- Large, well-organized tour with 35+ events
- Highest total purse of any women's tour outside the LPGA
- Strong domestic viewership and sponsorship (golf is extremely popular in Japan)
- JLPGA qualification through their own Q-School and pro test
- International players compete on the JLPGA, particularly from South Korea, China, and Taiwan
- Fewer players transition from JLPGA to LPGA compared to KLPGA, but it does happen

### Women's All Pro Tour
- US-based developmental mini-tour
- For players not yet ready for the Epson Tour
- Smaller purses ($15K-$25K per event)
- Competitive reps and scoring experience

Beyond overall KR, certain component KR thresholds indicate professional readiness:

| Component | Minimum for Pro Readiness | Why |
|-----------|--------------------------|-----|

If any component KR is below its minimum threshold, flag as a development priority before turning professional.

### Endorsement Income (Women's Golf)
Women's golf endorsement income has grown significantly but remains below men's:
- **Top 5 LPGA (KR 96+):** $2M-$10M+ in endorsements (equipment, apparel, corporate)
- **Top 20 LPGA (KR 92+):** $500K-$2M in endorsements
- **Top 50 LPGA (KR 88+):** $100K-$500K

- **Epson Tour:** Minimal endorsement income ($0-$25K typically)
- **KLPGA domestic:** Strong domestic sponsorship ($100K-$500K for top Korean players)

### Pregnancy/Motherhood Impact on Pro Career

**The system must explicitly model pregnancy impact for professional golfers.**

**Typical Timeline:**
- Tournament withdrawal: Varies by trimester. Most players stop competing by mid-second trimester.
- Recovery and return: 6-18 months post-childbirth to return to competitive golf
- Return to pre-pregnancy production: 6-24 months after return to competition
- Total career interruption: 12-36 months

**Financial Impact:**
- Lost earnings during absence: Calculate based on pre-pregnancy annual earnings
- LPGA medical extension: Retains exempt status for one year
- Epson Tour protections: Similar medical extensions
- LET/KLPGA/JLPGA: Varies by tour, generally some maternity protections

**KR Impact:**
- Produce BOTH current KR (post-return) AND pre-pregnancy baseline KR
- Flag the gap as Pregnancy/Motherhood Suppression
- Project return to pre-pregnancy KR based on timeline and observed recovery rate
- Do NOT treat pregnancy suppression as permanent decline
- The Motherhood Return Override (+1.0 KR) applies when a player returns to pre-pregnancy production within 18 months

**Career Longevity Impact:**
- Women's golf careers can extend well into the late 30s and 40s (Juli Inkster won at 45, Annika returned at 52)
- Pregnancy typically causes a 1-3 year "pause" but does not end a career
- Some players report improved mental perspective and motivation after becoming mothers

# COACHING IMPACT MODIFIER

## Purpose
Quantifies the coaching staff's contribution to player development and program performance. Used as an input to Team KR (10% weight) and as a factor in development projections.

## Coaching Impact Scoring

| Factor | Weight | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Player Development Rate | 35% | Average annual KR improvement for players who have been in the program 2+ years |
| Recruiting Success | 25% | Average recruit KR vs program budget tier. Are they getting talent appropriate to their resources? |
| Retention Rate | 15% | Percentage of scholarship players who stay for full eligibility (transfers out are negative) |
| Championship Performance | 15% | Team performance at conference and NCAA championships vs regular season (do they peak at the right time?) |
| Culture/Intangibles | 10% | Player testimonials, team chemistry indicators, academic success rate. Coach input or V2+ required. |

## Coaching Impact Score Ranges

| Score | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| 90-100 | Elite coaching staff. Develops players significantly, recruits at or above program tier, retains talent, peaks at championships. |
| 80-89 | Strong coaching. Good in most areas. Clear positive impact on player development. |
| 70-79 | Competent. Players develop at expected rates. Recruiting matches program resources. |
| 60-69 | Below expectations for available resources. Some area (development, recruiting, or retention) is lagging. |
| Below 60 | Underperforming. Players not developing, talent leaving through transfers, or championship performance consistently poor. |

Programs with historically strong coaching impact (not exhaustive):

# TRANSFER PORTAL INTELLIGENCE

## Portal Flow in Women's Golf

Women's golf transfer portal activity has increased significantly. Key dynamics:

### Common Transfer Patterns
1. **NJCAA to D1/D2:** Players develop at NJCAA for 2 years, then transfer up
2. **D1 Low-Major to D1 Power 4:** Players outgrow their program level
3. **D1 Power 4 lateral:** Players transfer for playing time, coaching fit, or geographic preference
4. **D2/NAIA to D1:** Players who prove they can compete at a higher level
5. **International to US college:** Growing trend of international players entering US college golf

### Portal Evaluation Protocol
1. Assess the player's KR at her current level
2. Apply KLVN to translate KR to the target level
3. Evaluate fit with the target program:
   - Lineup spot: Where would she slot into the top 5?
   - Team KR impact: How much does Team KR improve?
   - Scholarship cost: How much of the remaining scholarship budget does she require?
   - Development ceiling: How much room for growth at the new level?
   - Academic fit: Does the school offer her academic program?
4. Output: Transfer recommendation with projected Team KR impact and scholarship efficiency

# GOVERNANCE

- Downstream engines consume upstream KRs. They never modify them.
- Development projections are estimates with stated confidence ranges.
- Pro transition projections include both optimistic and conservative scenarios.
- Career earnings projections are based on historical tour earnings data and may not reflect future purse growth.
- Coaching Impact Modifier is updated annually at season's end.
- All outputs are deterministic given the same inputs.
- Pregnancy/motherhood suppression is handled with full transparency: current and pre-pregnancy baselines are always reported together.
