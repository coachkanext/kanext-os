# Valuation Framework

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

---

## Purpose

This document presents four frameworks for evaluating KaNeXT's value. Each framework applies a different lens: strategic asset base (current, planned, and long-term), vertical SaaS with embedded fintech, intelligence operating system, and full platform execution. The frameworks are presented in ascending order of execution dependency, from the current asset floor (least execution-dependent) to full platform value (most execution-dependent).

KaNeXT is architecturally deep but commercially early. These frameworks are intended to help investors assess what the system is worth under different assumptions about execution, not to assert a fixed valuation. Framework 1 distinguishes current owned assets from planned acquisitions and long-term strategic value to avoid conflating what exists today with what execution is designed to produce.

---

## Framework 1: Strategic Asset Base

This framework evaluates what KaNeXT owns and operates today versus what enters the asset base through subsequent capital events and execution. The framework is structured in three tiers to distinguish what is currently owned from what is planned for acquisition from what represents longer-horizon strategic value.

**Tier A** is what exists and is owned today.

**Tier B** is what enters the asset base through subsequent Year 2 and beyond capital events (Miami Lakes acquisition, bank charter, infrastructure companies).

**Tier C** is long-term strategic value that builds through execution and time (developed campus, bank at scale, intelligence IP at mature commercial deployment).

The Year 1 $33M capital raise is protected by Tier A assets. Tier B and Tier C build through subsequent capital events and execution.

### Tier B: Planned Acquisitions (Subsequent Capital Events)

These assets are not owned today. They enter the asset base through capital events beyond the Year 1 $33M raise. Each is a separate transaction requiring separate capital allocation.

### 596 Acres in Miami Lakes

The 596-acre parcel is targeted for off-market acquisition from Graham Companies at a negotiated price of approximately $420M. The acquisition is planned for Year 2 through a separate capital event distinct from the Year 1 fund. The land is not currently owned by KaNeXT.

Upon acquisition, the land would enter the KaNeXT asset base. Commercial land currently listed in Miami Lakes averages approximately $1.1M per acre. Miami-Dade County has the highest land values in the state of Florida, with small commercial parcels trading at $450,000-$2.1M per acre depending on size, zoning, and location. Even applying the volume discount appropriate for a 596-acre assemblage, current market value likely exceeds the negotiated purchase price.

Miami-Dade taxable property values have risen approximately 8.5-12.7% annually from 2023 through 2025, with countywide taxable values reaching $511.8 billion in 2025. Miami-Dade land sales volume surged 111% year-over-year in 2025, with median residential land prices per square foot quadrupling in some categories. This is not a static market. The land appreciates whether or not the institutional thesis executes because it is Miami-Dade commercial real estate in a market with structural demand driven by population growth, migration, and limited supply.

**Conservative Miami Lakes land value at acquisition: $500-658M.** This asset is not available as collateral for the Year 1 capital raise. It enters the asset base upon Year 2 acquisition through a separate capital event.

### Bank Charter and Infrastructure Companies

The bank charter, camera company, VoIP company, enrollment company, and fulfillment center represent strategic infrastructure targeted for acquisition and operational integration through subsequent capital events. Each acquisition is a separate transaction requiring separate capital allocation.

A Black-owned MDI backed by predictable student tuition deposits is structurally unique in the banking landscape. Student tuition is among the most predictable deposit sources available: enrollment is known in advance, tuition schedules are published, financial aid disbursements are calendared. Institutional payroll, vendor payments, mandate commerce settlement, and wallet deposits layer on top. Major banks actively seek MDI deposit relationships for CRA credit, creating an organic deposit growth channel with zero marketing cost.

Total MDI deposits nationally reached $381 billion in assets across 153 institutions. Black MDIs averaged $305 million in deposits per bank. At the projected deposit trajectory of $750M-1B+ by Year 5 with appropriate capital ratios, the bank could be valued at $100-200M+ using standard community bank multiples of 1.0-1.5x book value.

**Strategic infrastructure acquisition cost: approximately $50M estimated through future capital events.** These assets are not available as collateral for the Year 1 capital raise. They enter the asset base upon completion of each acquisition through separate capital events.

## Framework 3: Intelligence Operating System (Palantir Parallel)

This framework treats KaNeXT as a governed intelligence operating system for institutions, structurally parallel to Palantir's position as a governed intelligence OS for government and enterprise.

### The Structural Parallel

Palantir builds governed intelligence infrastructure that makes data operationally useful inside large organizations. KaNeXT builds governed intelligence infrastructure that makes data operationally useful inside educational institutions, athletic programs, and community organizations. Both are model-agnostic (they use foundation AI models but do not build them). Both derive competitive advantage from the governance layer, not the model layer. Both create institutional dependency through deep integration (high switching costs once deployed).

### Palantir as Structural Context

Palantir's 2025 revenue was approximately $4.48B, with 2026 guidance of $7.18-7.20B and a market capitalization in the range of $350-430B. This implies a historically elevated EV/Revenue multiple that reflects the market's assessment of governed intelligence infrastructure as a premium category. Palantir's adjusted gross margin exceeds 80% and operating margins have expanded above 30%.

KaNeXT is not Palantir. Palantir is a 22-year-old public company with thousands of employees and billions in cumulative investment. KaNeXT is pre-revenue with a single founder. The comparison is structural, not financial: both build governed intelligence infrastructure that becomes deeply integrated into institutional operations, both are model-agnostic, and both create high switching costs once deployed.

The relevance of Palantir for KaNeXT's valuation is not that KaNeXT should trade at Palantir's multiple. It is that the market has demonstrated willingness to assign substantial premiums to companies that build governed intelligence operating systems with institutional dependency. If KaNeXT executes on its revenue projections, the intelligence OS category would suggest multiples meaningfully above standard vertical SaaS.

| Scenario | Multiple | Y5 Revenue | Implied Valuation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative (premium SaaS) | 15x | $650M | $9.75B |
| Moderate (intelligence OS premium) | 20-25x | $650M | $13-16B |

These scenarios reflect the range between premium vertical SaaS and intelligence infrastructure multiples. The specific multiple KaNeXT would command depends on growth rate, margin profile, competitive position, and market conditions at the time of any transaction.

## Framework 4: Full Platform Execution (Upside Scenario)

This framework is explicitly a full-execution scenario, not a near-term valuation expectation. It assumes every major thesis component executes successfully over a 10-year horizon. It is included because it represents what the system is designed to become, not because it represents a likely near-term outcome.

### The Remittance Multiplier

This is the element most likely to produce non-linear valuation outcomes. The global remittance market reached approximately $905 billion in 2024 and is projected to grow at 12%+ CAGR. The market is expected to reach $382 billion in digital remittance revenue by 2031. The average global cost of sending $200 in remittances is 6.49%. To Sub-Saharan Africa, the most expensive region, costs average 8.78%. KaNeXT offers 0.2%.

Sports is inherently global. An athlete from Nigeria, Jamaica, the Dominican Republic, Cuba, or Brazil plays at the partner institution or another platform institution. Their family downloads the app to watch games on KTV. They discover remittance at 0.2% instead of 6.49-8.78%. The fee difference saves $12-17 on every $200 sent. They tell every family member. Every friend. Every person in their community who sends money internationally. Each new user creates more users through the same economic logic: everyone wants cheaper fees. Sports is the acquisition engine. Remittance is the retention and multiplication engine. The network grows organically because the value proposition is universally compelling.

Over $96 billion flowed into Africa in remittances in 2024 alone. Latin America and the Caribbean received $156 billion. Consumer-to-consumer cross-border payments totaled $2 trillion globally. Even capturing a small fraction of diaspora remittance corridors connected through the platform's VoIP and wallet infrastructure represents significant transaction volume flowing through KPay and the owned bank charter, generating interchange, deposit float, lending income, and processing revenue.

Every remittance user is also a potential wallet holder, a potential KTV viewer, a potential merch purchaser, and a potential enrollment lead. The remittance layer does not just generate transaction revenue. It multiplies the entire platform's user base through organic network expansion that compounds with every new corridor connected.

### The Bank as Platform Multiplier

The bank's value in this framework goes far beyond traditional community banking economics. At $750M-1B+ in deposits sourced from institutional tuition, mandate commerce settlement, wallet balances, and diaspora remittance, the bank becomes a platform-scale financial institution with: deposit spread income on the entire balance, lending capacity backed by the deposit base, interchange on every KaNeXT Card transaction outside the ecosystem, treasury services for every institution on the platform, CRA-motivated corporate deposits flowing in from major banks, and remittance processing revenue on every international transfer.

The deposit growth trajectory is powered by structural sources that grow with the platform, not by traditional branch-based deposit gathering. Every new mandate institution, every new wallet holder, every new remittance user, and every new institution on the platform adds deposits. The bank grows as the platform grows.

### Full Execution Scenario Range

Under full execution assumptions across all revenue streams (institutional, platform, intelligence, banking, remittance, gaming, media, data licensing, international), with network effects compounding across the mandate, the institutional distribution, the diaspora pipeline, and the bank deposit base, a 10-year enterprise value in the range of **$15-30B+** is conceivable.

This is not a projection. It is a scenario that illustrates what the compounding of multiple individually substantial revenue streams could produce if the system executes as designed. Each stream (institutional revenue, platform commerce, intelligence licensing, banking, remittance) individually supports a multi-billion dollar business. The full platform thesis is that they operate simultaneously inside a single governed platform where each component reinforces every other. The probability-weighted value is somewhere between Framework 1 and Framework 4, depending on the investor's confidence in execution across each layer.

## What the Investor Is Actually Buying

The investor is not buying a single asset. The investor is buying a system in which every component reinforces every other component:

The intelligence makes the athletic program win. Winning drives enrollment and media visibility. Enrollment drives tuition revenue. Tuition flows through the bank as deposits. The bank generates lending and interchange income. The mandate deploys cameras that create content. Content drives KTV viewership. Viewership drives app installs. App installs create wallet holders. Wallet holders transact through KPay. Transactions generate commerce processing revenue. The VoIP layer connects diaspora families. Diaspora families discover remittance. Remittance creates more wallet holders. More wallet holders create more deposits. More deposits grow the bank. The intelligence is held in-house during Year 1-3 as the partner institution's competitive moat, then licenses commercially at institutional-IP tier from Year 3+ with pricing established by the publicly attributable competitive results accumulated during the validation phase. Every component feeds every other component.

The valuation framework that applies depends on how many of these components the investor believes will execute. At the asset-base level (Framework 1), current and near-current assets provide substantial coverage of the Year 1 capital commitment, with additional strategic value building as execution progresses. At full execution (Framework 4), the compounding effects across all components illustrate the scale of what the system is designed to produce. The probability-weighted value sits between these frameworks based on the investor's confidence in execution across each layer.

## Why the Fund Structure, Not Pure Equity

A company with this depth of proprietary intelligence, a live deployed product, 250K+ athlete database, 50+ domains, and a clear path to multi-billion dollar revenue could raise an equity round at a $1-5B+ pre-money valuation in the current market. Pre-revenue AI companies with defensible architectures and large TAMs have consistently raised at $500M-2B+ valuations. Companies with live products, proprietary data, and no direct competitors command premiums above that range.

KaNeXT structured the Year 1 raise to preserve founder equity and minimize dilution.

The Year 1 structure is a $30M fund with 1.5x fixed return to LPs plus a $3M SAFE at $100M cap. The fund component raises $30M with clean 1.5x obligation ($45M). The SAFE component raises $3M with minimal dilution at a $100M valuation cap (approximately 3% dilution if converted at cap). Total Year 1 capital: $33M.

The LP (fund side) gets a clean 50% return on deployed capital. The return is structured against the Tier A asset base described earlier in this framework: proprietary intelligence IP, the live deployed product, and the Year 1 partnership structure. Miami Lakes and banking infrastructure are Tier B Year 2+ capital events and are not pledged against the Year 1 raise. The SAFE side gets minimal dilution entry into an intelligence operating system with the architectural depth described above.

The founder retains approximately 97% of the company through Year 1. The fund obligation ($45M at 1.5x) is a fraction of even the most conservative company valuation at scale.

What the LP is buying: a strong risk-adjusted return on capital deployment into a live operational product with deep intelligence IP, a real institutional partnership, and capital gating that ensures deployment only occurs upon partnership execution. What the LP is NOT buying: ownership of the intelligence, the platform, or the operational company.

What the SAFE holder is buying: minimal dilution entry into an intelligence operating system that is commercially early but architecturally deep.

This is the right structure for all parties. The LP gets a fixed return that does not depend on the intelligence system performing, backed by the Tier A asset base (intelligence IP replacement cost, live deployed product, Year 1 partnership) plus capital gating on partnership execution. The SAFE holder gets favorable entry into the upside. The founder retains substantial ownership of a company whose value could reach $3-30B+ if the platform executes as designed.
