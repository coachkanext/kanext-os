# Fulfillment Center Brief

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

---

## Overview

KaNeXT is building an in-house fulfillment operation for merchandise production, warehousing, and distribution across the entire platform. Every mandate institution, every school network institution, every athlete NIL store, every brand store across all five modes, and every KaNeXT-owned event is served by a single production operation.

The fulfillment center is not a third-party print shop. It is owned infrastructure that produces merch on demand, ships directly, and settles every transaction through KPay's governed settlement chain. Every order is a commerce event that generates 5% processing revenue for KaNeXT in addition to production margin and revenue share.

Budget allocation: $5M.

---

## The Product Tiers

**Tier 1 - Standard.** Print-on-demand merchandise available to every mandate institution and brand on the platform at zero setup cost. T-shirts, hoodies, hats, and accessories with school or brand identity. A fan orders a hoodie. The order routes to the fulfillment center. The hoodie is produced, packed, and shipped. No inventory risk for the school or brand.

**Tier 2 - Premium.** Higher quality blanks, expanded product selection, and custom design options for schools and brands with growing demand. Still print-on-demand for most items, with pre-produced inventory on proven sellers to improve quality and reduce per-unit cost.

**Tier 3 - Designer and Exclusive.** Limited-edition drops with premium materials and packaging. Athlete-designed collections. Conference championship gear. Event-specific merchandise. Higher retail prices and higher margins at every level.

---

## Unit Economics

Illustrative example: a $45 hoodie (Tier 1 or Tier 2). Approximately $25 COGS (blank, printing, packaging, shipping). Approximately $12 to the school or brand (revenue share). Approximately $6 KaNeXT production margin. Plus $2.25 in 5% commerce processing on the transaction through KPay.

Tier 3 products carry higher retail prices and proportionally higher margins. A $95 limited-edition hoodie with premium blank and designer graphics generates more margin at every level: production, revenue share, and processing.

At scale, per-unit costs decrease across all tiers. Bulk blank purchasing reduces material cost. Pre-produced items reduce per-unit production cost. The fixed cost base is shared across every storefront on the platform. Margin improves with every brand added and every order fulfilled.

---

## Phased Scaling

**Phase 1: Miami Hub.** Located in the Miami metro area. Handles all production and shipping for the initial mandate rollout, school network institutions, and early brand stores. Equipment: commercial-grade DTG printers, heat presses, embroidery machine, vinyl cutter, shipping station, and initial blank inventory. Setup cost: $95,000-$150,000. Staffing: 4-5 full-time and 1-2 part-time employees. Target capacity: 500-1,000 orders per week scaling to 2,000+ as adoption grows.

**Phase 2: Central US Hub.** Once order volume exceeds 3,000 per week, a second fulfillment center opens in a central US location to reduce average shipping times to 2-4 days for the majority of the country. Mirrors Miami equipment and staffing.

**Phase 3: Pre-Produced and Hybrid Model.** For schools, athletes, and brands with proven sales volume, transition from pure print-on-demand to a hybrid model. Pre-produced inventory on top sellers improves quality, reduces per-unit cost by 20-30%, and enables same-day shipping.

---

## Who It Serves

**Mandate institutions (1,000+ at full deployment).** Each receives a managed Tier 1 merch store at zero cost as part of the mandate package. The school's branding is applied to standard product templates. Revenue share flows back to the school through KPay.

**School network institutions.** Full Tier 1-3 product access. Uniforms, game-day merchandise, campus bookstore inventory, alumni merchandise, and event-specific products.

**Athlete NIL stores.** Individual athletes across the platform can sell branded merchandise through their Personal mode Earn tile. Production and fulfillment are handled by the center. The athlete receives revenue share. KaNeXT earns production margin and commerce processing.

**Brand stores across all modes.** A church sells ministry-branded merchandise. A business sells branded products. A creator sells merch through their Personal brand. Every storefront across every mode is a potential fulfillment customer.

**KaNeXT-owned events.** Tournament merchandise, showcase event gear, and conference championship products are all produced and fulfilled by the same operation.

---

## Strategic Value

The fulfillment center turns every brand on the platform into a merch store without the brand needing to source products, manage inventory, or handle shipping. The school does nothing. The athlete does nothing. The church does nothing. They choose designs, set prices, and the fulfillment center handles everything else.

At 1,000+ mandate institutions plus network institutions plus athlete NIL stores plus brand stores across all modes plus tournament properties, the fulfillment center serves thousands of storefronts through a single production operation. The fixed cost base is shared across every storefront. Each brand added increases volume without proportionally increasing cost.

Every transaction flows through KPay. Every order is a governed commerce event. The fulfillment center is the physical goods layer that turns the platform's commerce architecture into tangible products shipped to real people.
