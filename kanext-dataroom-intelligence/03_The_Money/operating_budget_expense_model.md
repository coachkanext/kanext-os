# Operating Budget and Expense Model

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

---

## Purpose

The Institutional Revenue Model presents gross revenue to KaNeXT across the Year 1 partnership. This document presents the cost side: what it costs to operate the partnership across all functions. This answers "what are the actual yearly operating costs?" with a structured estimate.

All figures are forward-looking estimates. Actual costs depend on hiring timelines, construction schedules, enrollment pace, vendor negotiations, and market conditions. A detailed line-item operating budget is maintained internally and updated quarterly.

---

## Cost Structure Overview

Under the Athletics Operating Agreement and subsequent Full IOA, operating costs are split between two entities:

**KaNeXT (Operating Partner)** funds and operates: technology infrastructure, athletic operations, enrollment and marketing, campus operations (housing, dining, facilities on KaNeXT-owned land from Year 3), media production, financial infrastructure, communications, and all non-academic institutional functions. Funded from KaNeXT's 60% tuition share under the Full IOA structure, 100% of non-tuition revenue, and fund capital during the ramp period.

**the partner institution (University)** funds from its 40% tuition allocation: faculty salaries and benefits, academic program development, accreditation compliance, academic advising, registrar and financial aid administration, library and academic resources, institutional governance, and board operations. The partner institution controls this allocation with sole discretion consistent with its ABHE accreditation obligations.

Year 1 operates under the Athletics-Only Agreement where KaNeXT funds athletic operations and platform deployment directly. Full IOA revenue splits activate Year 2 upon ABHE substantive change approval and DOE CIO determination.

## Operating Margin Analysis

These margins reflect the partner institution institutional revenue only. The revenue in this table is exclusively from the Institutional Revenue Model: tuition, housing, dining, athletics, and operating partnership fees. It does not include any revenue from the Platform and Intelligence Revenue Architecture: commerce processing across the distribution network, wallet float, remittance, advertising, intelligence licensing, data licensing, OS licensing, bank revenue, or any other platform revenue stream.

All platform and intelligence revenue is additional income on top of these margins. Most platform revenue streams operate at significantly higher margins than institutional operations (software and intelligence licensing at 80-90%+, commerce processing at 60%+, wallet float at near-100%, data licensing at 90%+). The actual total KaNeXT margin at scale, including platform and intelligence revenue, would be materially higher than what this institutional-only analysis shows.

This is intentional. The institutional base case is presented in isolation to demonstrate that the partner institution operations alone generate strong and growing margins without relying on any platform or intelligence revenue to be profitable. Everything from the platform and intelligence architecture is upside.

Year 1 through Year 3 operate at institutional operating losses funded by the $33M capital raise plus platform and intelligence revenue streams running in parallel. Operating margin improves materially from Year 1 to Year 5 because revenue scales faster than costs. This is the projected operating profile if enrollment, tuition, and athletic revenue assumptions perform as modeled. Actual margins depend on the pace and magnitude of enrollment growth, housing occupancy from Miami Lakes, athletic revenue maturation, online program scaling, and Full IOA activation timing. The margins shown here are achievable under the assumptions but should not be read as guaranteed outcomes.

## Enrollment Sensitivity

The operating model is sensitive to enrollment pace. The following illustrates the impact of a 25% enrollment shortfall across all years:

| Metric | Y1 Base | Y1 Downside | Y3 Base | Y3 Downside |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Total enrollment | 2,100 | 1,575 | 11,000 | 8,250 |
| KaNeXT revenue (midpoint) | $1.8M | ~$1.3M | $55M | ~$42M |
| KaNeXT operating expense | $31.6M | ~$28M | $87.5M | ~$77M |
| Operating outcome | ($29.8M) | ($26.7M) | ($32.5M) | ($35M) |

Costs do not decrease proportionally with enrollment because athletics, technology, campus infrastructure, and corporate expenses are largely fixed or semi-fixed. Enrollment and marketing costs flex partially. Housing and dining costs flex with occupancy. Faculty costs (University-side) flex with course sections. The downside scenario illustrates that the institutional operating loss in early years widens under enrollment shortfall. Capital raised is sized to support operating losses through Year 3 on the base case; a sustained 25% shortfall extends the time to operating profitability and may require bridge capital for Year 4.

Platform and intelligence revenue streams operating in parallel provide partial offset against institutional operating losses and are not included in this sensitivity table.

## Fixed vs Variable Cost Structure

Understanding which costs flex with enrollment and which do not is critical to evaluating operating risk.

**Largely fixed (do not decrease significantly with lower enrollment):** Athletic operations (coaching staff, scholarships, travel, equipment are committed before enrollment is known). Technology and platform (engineering team, cloud infrastructure, AI costs). Corporate and administrative (executive team, legal, finance, insurance). These represent approximately 70% of Year 1 KaNeXT operating expense.

**Semi-variable (flex partially with enrollment):** Enrollment and marketing (spending can be reduced but at the cost of future growth). Campus operations (some facilities costs are fixed, but housing/dining staffing and food costs flex with occupancy). Online program delivery (adjunct faculty and support scale with online enrollment).

**Variable (flex directly with enrollment):** Faculty costs on the University side scale with course sections needed. Housing and dining food and staffing costs scale with occupancy.

## Year 1 Cash Flow Bridge

Year 1 KaNeXT institutional revenue ($1M-$2.5M) is materially below Year 1 operating expense ($31.6M). The Year 1 operating gap is funded from the $33M capital raise ($30M fund plus $3M SAFE). Year 1 capital deployment prioritizes athletic operations ($13.5M), platform infrastructure, enrollment engine launch, and institutional support payments to the partner institution.

Years 2-3 continue to require fund capital for operating losses as revenue ramps. Year 4 is the crossover year where institutional operating income moves positive on the base case. Year 5 institutional operations generate substantial positive operating income.

Platform and intelligence revenue streams running in parallel provide additional cash flow not included in this institutional-only analysis. The full KaNeXT revenue picture (institutional plus platform plus intelligence) provides materially stronger cash flow support than the institutional-only view.

## Key Assumptions and Sensitivities

**Faculty costs** assume a student-to-faculty ratio consistent with ABHE standards and premium Christian higher education benchmarks. Average loaded faculty cost rises as the partner institution invests in faculty compensation above the Christian university market per the institutional quality investment thesis.

**Coaching salaries** assume competitive D1 mid-major compensation with intelligence-system and NIL advantages contributing to the competitive recruiting edge. Head coach salaries range across sport and level. The total coaching compensation line grows with sport portfolio expansion from Year 1 6 sports toward Year 5 full athletic department.

**Housing and dining** margins are significant because KaNeXT owns the Miami Lakes facilities and operates them directly rather than outsourcing to a third-party management company. The $1/year the partner institution ground lease structure means KaNeXT captures housing, dining, and campus commerce revenue while the partner institution captures tuition share.

**Technology costs** are lean relative to revenue because the intelligence architecture is specified IP, reducing (but not eliminating) R&D uncertainty. Ongoing platform engineering, maintenance, security, integration, and infrastructure scaling are real costs that grow with institutional complexity and user base. The estimates above account for this growth.

**The largest cost sensitivity is enrollment pace.** If enrollment grows slower than projected, revenue declines faster than costs can be reduced. The fixed-cost structure described above means margin compression is the primary risk in a slower-enrollment scenario. The intelligence architecture (PLKR, RKR, PKR, RCKR described in the enrollment funnel model) is specifically designed to detect pace deterioration early and trigger intervention.
