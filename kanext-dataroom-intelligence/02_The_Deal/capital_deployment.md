# KaNeXT Capital Deployment

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

---

## Overview

The Year 1 capital raise funds the Year 1 partnership launch. Total target is $33M across two parallel instruments: a $30M fund and a $3M SAFE. Capital deploys upon partnership execution with the partner institution (South Florida Bible College & Theological Seminary, a forty-year-old ABHE-accredited evangelical Christian institution), against the Year 1 operating scope defined in the Athletics Operating Agreement and supporting documents.

The Year 1 capital is a concentrated bet on one transformation. It does not fund campus acquisition, bank charter, platform infrastructure buildout, or mandate rollout. Those are future capital events raised separately against proven execution at the partner institution. This capital funds the partnership launch, the athletic program buildout, platform deployment, operational infrastructure, and Year 1 buffer.

---

## Capital Structure

**$30M Fund (KaNeXT Capital Fund I LLC).** Structured for a 1.5x return to limited partners, total return obligation of $45M, with zero equity dilution to the KaNeXT operating entity or founder. The fund is the primary capital vehicle. Limited partners receive a fixed return structure rather than equity participation, preserving the operating company's capitalization structure and the founder's ownership.

**$3M Parallel SAFE.** Friends & Family round sized at $3M with a $100M valuation cap. Approximately 3% dilution at the cap. Converts to equity at the next priced round. Structured parallel to the fund, not as a replacement, targeting individual accredited investors with ticket sizes typical for F&F participation.

**Combined total: $33M.** The fund provides the primary deployment capital. The SAFE adds working capital and buffer while expanding the relational network of individual investors participating in the raise.

Full fund mechanics, SAFE conversion terms, and investor economics are detailed in the term sheet document.

## Year 1 Deployment Breakdown

The $30M deployment covers the operational scope for Year 1 partnership launch at the partner institution.

| Category | Amount |
|---|---|
| Athletics | $13.5M |
| Housing + Feeding | $4.0M |
| KaNeXT Core Operations | $6.4M |
| Partner Institution Support | $2.1M |
| Buffer / Reserve | $4.0M |
| **Total** | **$30.0M** |

### Athletics ($13.5M)

Full operational funding for six Varsity programs with two teams per sport (Varsity plus Development = 12 teams) in Year 1. Year 1 scholarship structure is strategic: minimal scholarships deployed only where recruiting requires it, with strategic budget reserved for exceptional situations. Most athletes receive Title IV federal financial aid plus standard institutional aid. NAIA Varsity scholarship equivalencies activate in Year 2 upon NAIA membership approval. NIL distribution through KPay operates from Day 1. Championship-caliber coaching staffs funded above NAIA market rates. Full athletic training and medical support. Weight room and training facility equipment and buildout. Athletic facility access through commercial gym partnerships and junior college arrangements. Game operations, travel, equipment, recruiting, and athletic department administration. Capital reserved from strategic scholarship savings deploys to premium program quality across coaching compensation, facilities, recruiting infrastructure, and NIL reserves.

### Housing + Feeding ($4.0M)

Master-leased cluster housing in the South Florida market proximate to athletic facility partners. Housing infrastructure for all Varsity scholarship athletes and development tier athletes electing cluster housing. Team rooms, study space, residence life support, nutrition integration. Team dining infrastructure through contracted food service operations.

### KaNeXT Core Operations ($6.4M)

Founder and team compensation and benefits. Corporate infrastructure: office, legal, accounting, insurance, technology. Platform operations: server and infrastructure costs for KaNeXT OS, Dipson, KTV, KPay, KStat, KVision, and supporting systems. Ongoing engineering and platform development. Intellectual property protection. Corporate governance and compliance. Investment in institutional quality at the partner institution, including faculty compensation uplift above the Christian university market, academic program development, student services excellence, and academic infrastructure that supports the tuition trajectory described in the institutional revenue model.

### Partner Institution Support ($2.1M)

Direct financial support to the partner institution through the partnership structure. Operational Partnership Fee paid annually. Philanthropic Contribution to the institution. Institutional integration infrastructure. Compliance and coordination costs associated with the Athletics Operating Agreement.

### Buffer / Reserve ($4.0M)

Working capital and contingency reserve. Protects against unforeseen capital needs, timing mismatches between expenses and revenue, and operational variance in the first year of a new partnership. The buffer is standard prudence at this scale and stage.

## Deployment Principles

**Partnership-gated.** Capital deployment is gated on execution of the Athletics Operating Agreement with SFBC. The raise and the partnership are sequenced, not simultaneous. Capital does not flow before partnership execution.

**Asset-light Year 1.** No campus acquisition, no facility construction, no hardware manufacturing, no bank charter. Facilities are accessed through leasing and partnerships. Housing is master-leased. The platform operates on existing cloud infrastructure. Year 1 is an operational deployment, not an infrastructure build.

**Concentrated, not diversified.** The capital funds one partnership at one institution. No parallel deployments at other institutions. No speculative allocations to future opportunities. Every dollar supports the partner institution transformation.

**Software-first.** Platform deployment at the partner institution and at GAAC operates on the existing KaNeXT OS, which is live and operational. The software is the product. Hardware and infrastructure buildouts (cameras, fulfillment, VoIP telephony infrastructure, bank operations) are future capital events not required for Year 1 success.

**Buffer-preserved.** The $4M buffer exists because first-year operations at new partnerships always encounter variance. The buffer is not padding. It is working capital that makes Year 1 survivable through the normal turbulence of launch.

## Forward Capital Architecture

The Year 1 raise is the first deployment in a multi-stage capital plan. Subsequent capital is raised against proven execution at the partner institution. Each future capital event is scoped, structured, and priced at the time of deployment based on operational results and market conditions.

**Campus Acquisition Capital.** Miami Lakes campus land acquisition and early development. Activates when the partner institution reaches the fully-built state described in the institutional strategy documents.

**Platform Infrastructure Capital.** Camera company acquisition, fulfillment center operations, VoIP telecommunications infrastructure. Activates as mandate deployment scales demand for hardware and operational support.

**Bank Charter Capital.** Community bank acquisition and Minority Depository Institution capitalization. Activates when deposit volume from platform commerce justifies the charter.

**Mandate Rollout Capital.** Governing body mandate deployment across NAIA, NJCAA, USCAA, and NCCAA. Activates after GAAC proves the model.

Each capital event is concentrated on a specific milestone, not speculative buildout. The Year 1 raise is the foundation that earns KaNeXT the right to raise the subsequent capital. No commitments, timelines, or valuations for future raises are implied in the Year 1 capital documents.

## Founder Economics

**Year 1 founder cash compensation: $85,000.**

This is the salary figure for the Year 1 budget. The founder retains 100% equity ownership of OSK Group LLC, which owns KaNeXT LLC and all subsidiaries. The low cash compensation reflects founder alignment with investor outcomes: the returns are in the execution, not in short-term salary.

Standard corporate infrastructure (benefits, business expenses, technology, travel) is handled through ordinary operating expenses within the $6.4M Core Operations line. The salary figure represents direct cash compensation to the founder as an employee of the operating entity.
