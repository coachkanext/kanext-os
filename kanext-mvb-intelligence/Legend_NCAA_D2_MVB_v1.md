## SCOPE

This legend defines what each KR tier means at the NCAA Division II level for men's volleyball. Display-only - interprets KR, does not produce KR.

All production benchmarks are per-set rates unless noted.

**NCAA D2 Men's Volleyball Landscape:**
NCAA D2 men's volleyball has a very limited number of programs. Fewer than 30 programs compete at this level. The competitive landscape is small and concentrated regionally.

Key D2 programs include schools in the GNAC (Great Northwest Athletic Conference), California Collegiate Athletic Association (CCAA), and a handful of independents. Some D2 programs compete at a level comparable to lower-tier D1 programs.

**Scholarship context:** D2 men's volleyball has the same 4.5 scholarship equivalents as D1. Walk-on dependence is equally high.

## D2-SPECIFIC NOTES

- D2 programs often compete against D1 programs in non-conference matches. Players who perform well in those matchups demonstrate they may be underleveled at D2.
- International recruiting is present but less common than at D1 due to budget constraints.
- Walk-on development is even more critical at D2 because recruiting budgets are smaller.
- Some D2 programs are transitional - they may move to D1 or drop the sport entirely. Program stability is a factor in evaluation.
