# Legend: Club Men's Beach Volleyball v1

---

## SCOPE

This legend covers men's club beach volleyball competition. Men's beach volleyball is NOT an NCAA-sanctioned sport as of the 2025-26 season. Men compete at the club level, through intramural programs, or in independent tournaments. Some universities have club beach volleyball teams that compete against each other, but there is no NCAA championship structure.

The competition level varies widely. Top club programs (particularly in Southern California and Hawaii) produce players who go on to compete on the AVP tour. Most club programs are recreational or semi-competitive.

All production benchmarks are per-match rates. Matches are best of 3 sets (sets to 21/15).

## CONTEXT NOTES

### Why Men's Club Beach Matters
- Men's beach volleyball at the Olympic and professional level (AVP, FIVB) is thriving. The US has historically produced top international men's beach pairs.
- The pathway from club to pro is less structured than women's (no NCAA pipeline). Players must self-develop through club, AVP Next, and AVP qualifier events.
- Indoor college men's volleyball (NCAA D1/D2, MPSF, EIVA, etc.) provides the primary athletic development pipeline. Most top US men's beach players were indoor college players who transitioned after college.
- Club beach serves as a proving ground and development platform for players with pro aspirations.

### Indoor-to-Beach Translation for Men
The Indoor-to-Beach Translation Engine (File 06) applies to men's players as well. Key differences from women's translation:
- Men's indoor volleyball features more power serving and higher contact points, which translate directly to beach
- Men's beach volleyball at the pro level demands exceptional jumping ability on sand, which is harder for taller/heavier players
- The physical demands of men's beach (higher net at 7'11 5/8" vs women's 7'4 1/8") mean height and reach are even more important for blockers

### Competition Variability
Club-level competition varies dramatically:
- A match between UCLA Club and USC Club top pairs may approach NCAA Women's Beach top-program quality
- A match between two small school club teams may be significantly below that level
- KLVN lambda accounts for this variability (top club = 0.680, standard club = 0.580)
