# ALL-STAR CHEER PLAYER KR LEGEND v1
## Lambda = 1.050 (All-Star Level 6 International Open is the highest competitive tier in cheerleading)

Interpretation anchor: USASF All-Star cheerleading at Level 6 (International Open). This is the pinnacle of competitive cheerleading worldwide. Teams compete at NCA All-Star Nationals, UCA International All-Star, The Summit, and the Cheerleading Worlds (organized by USASF/IASF). Athletes at this level have typically trained since childhood, often with 8-12+ years of competitive experience. Programs like Cheer Athletics, Top Gun, World Cup, Prodigy, CEA, and other elite gyms produce the athletes at the top of this legend. Level 6 is unrestricted difficulty - all legal skills are available.

**Note:** This legend is calibrated to Level 6 International Open. For lower All-Star levels (L1-L5), apply the KLVN level adjustment from the College_Cheer_KLVN_Lambdas file to shift the tier descriptions down accordingly. An athlete with KR 85 at Level 4 is elite FOR Level 4 but would read differently against this Level 6 legend.

**94-100 - Worlds Champion Caliber / Generational Talent.**
The best in the world. Headline athlete on a Worlds-winning team. Every skill is elite. Performance is captivating. This athlete could succeed in any role and elevates every section of every routine they touch. Multiple Worlds medals. The athlete other gyms build around if they transfer. Potential professional performer (Cirque du Soleil, touring shows). This tier has maybe 20-30 athletes worldwide in any given year.

**90-93 - Worlds Medalist / Elite Franchise.**
Worlds-level athlete. Anchor of a Worlds-qualifying team. Multiple elite skills. Performs the hardest skills in the routine at the highest execution level. First-team All-Star All-American equivalent. Programs design routines around this athlete's abilities.

**86-89 - Worlds Competitor / National Elite.**
Competes at Worlds or the highest national competitions. Strong across all component KRs. May have one or two elite-level skills and strong supporting skills. Valuable starter on any elite program. Conference/regional champion at minimum.

**82-85 - National Competitor / Summit-Level.**
Competes at Summit, NCA, or equivalent national events. Strong skills across the board. May be the best athlete on a strong regional program or a solid contributor on a national-level program.

**78-81 - Strong Regional / National Roster.**
On the roster of a nationally competitive program. Contributes clean skills at the L6 level. May not perform the hardest skills but executes their assignment at a high level. Or: the star of a strong regional program.

**74-77 - Regional Competitor / Developing National.**
Competitive at the regional level. Making the transition to national competition. Skills are at the L6 legal minimum with some advanced elements. Developing toward national relevance.

**70-73 - Standard Competitor / Solid L6.**
Performs L6-legal skills cleanly. Competes at regional events. Solid athlete who fulfills their role. Not a standout but not a liability.

**66-69 - Transitional / New to L6.**
Recently moved up from L5 or returning to L6 after time away. Some skills are below L6 expectations. Needs a season to acclimate. Or: strong L5 athlete not yet ready for full L6 demands.

**62-65 - Below L6 Standard / L5 Elite.**
Better classified as an elite L5 athlete. Would dominate at L5 but is below the standard for L6. If competing on an L6 team, is the weakest link.

**58-61 - L5 Competitor / L6 Developmental.**
At the L5 competitive standard. Not L6-ready. Development plan focused on gaining L6-required skills.

**Below 58 - Below L5 Competitive Standard.**
Below the competitive threshold for serious All-Star competition. Recreational or lower-level development.
