## NJCAA EVALUATION CONTEXT

**NJCAA women's golf has 8 tuition-only scholarships at D1 level.** NJCAA D2 and D3 have fewer or no scholarships.

**NJCAA is a 2-year system.** Players compete for 2 years then transfer to a 4-year institution. Every NJCAA player is evaluated with transfer destination in mind.

**NJCAA golf is smaller than NJCAA basketball or other sports.** Fewer programs, smaller fields, and less media coverage. This makes data gathering harder. Scoring averages and tournament results may require school website searches.

**NJCAA national championship is the premier data point.** Strong performances at the NJCAA national tournament are the most reliable evaluation signals for projecting transfer success.

**Development trajectory is critical at NJCAA.** Because players are only at the school for 2 years, the trajectory (improvement from freshman to sophomore year) is often more important than the absolute numbers. A player who drops from 80.0 to 75.0 scoring average in one year shows elite development capacity.

**Common transfer destinations from NJCAA:**
- NJCAA star (KR 85+) - D1 Power 4 or strong mid-major
- NJCAA solid (KR 80-84) - D1 mid-major or low-major, strong D2
- NJCAA average (KR 75-79) - D2, NAIA
- NJCAA developing (KR below 75) - NAIA, D3
