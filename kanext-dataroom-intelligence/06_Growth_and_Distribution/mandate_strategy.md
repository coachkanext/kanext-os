# Governing Body Mandate Strategy

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

---

## The Problem

There are over 1,000 institutions competing in sub-NCAA athletics across the core governing bodies (NAIA, NJCAA, USCAA, NCCAA) plus select high school programs in the elite prep, grassroots, and feeder tier that KaNeXT deploys to by invitation. These institutions collectively field thousands of teams and hundreds of thousands of student-athletes. Most have zero video infrastructure, zero automated statistics, zero scouting intelligence, zero digital ticketing, and zero managed merchandise capability. Their games go unrecorded. Their athletes are invisible to scouts and recruiters. Their families cannot watch from home.

This is not a technology gap. It is an infrastructure gap that will not close through market forces. These schools have no video budget. No SaaS company can profitably serve institutions that cannot pay monthly subscriptions. The economics of traditional sports technology do not work at this level.

KaNeXT solves this by giving everything away for free and monetizing the commerce that flows through the platform once institutions adopt it.

---

## Deal Structure

KaNeXT approaches governing bodies with a model in which member schools adopt KaNeXT as the institutional technology, streaming, and commerce platform under a governing body agreement.

**Duration:** 25 years from execution date. Either party may elect non-renewal or opt-out at Year 15 upon 12 months' prior written notice, subject to definitive agreement terms.

**Exclusivity:** KaNeXT serves as the sole technology, streaming, commerce, and intelligence provider for all participating schools and governing bodies.

**Cost to school:** $0. **Cost to governing body:** $0.

**KaNeXT monetization:** 5% flat processing on all transactions flowing through the platform. Schools keep 95% of revenue that did not exist before KaNeXT arrived.

## The GAAC Relationship Is Strategic, Not Incidental

GAAC is not simply the conference the partner institution happens to belong to. It is the first mandate deployment, the proof-of-concept for the full mandate playbook, and a relationship with active conference expansion ambitions that align directly with KaNeXT's rollout.

The principal funder of the partner institution is also the GAAC conference owner. He is actively building GAAC into a scaled Christian college athletic league structured similarly to NCCAA, NAIA, and USCAA. His expansion roadmap includes bringing additional small Christian colleges under GAAC governance, formalizing the conference infrastructure, and growing the athletic footprint across the Christian higher education ecosystem. KaNeXT sits inside that expansion plan, not the other way around.

GAAC member schools fit the exact profile the mandate was designed for. Enrollment typically 250 students or less. Many institutions less than 3 years old. No existing video infrastructure, no streaming, no automated statistics, no digital ticketing, no scouting systems. The same operational gap that defines every target governing body, concentrated in the conference where the partner institution already holds charter membership and where the conference owner is personally committed to platform deployment.

The GAAC deployment sequence. Year 1: free cameras, free OS, free streaming, free stats capture deployed to every GAAC member school through the conference owner's direct relationship. Precursor proof of the full mandate model. Every GAAC game produced, every student and family onto the app, every transaction through KPay. Year 2-3: GAAC expansion under the conference owner's roadmap. Additional Christian colleges onboard to GAAC membership, each receiving the full platform at no cost. The conference grows and the mandate footprint grows with it through a single operator relationship rather than 100+ separate school-by-school sales cycles. Year 2-3 parallel: NAIA, USCAA, NCCAA mandate pursuit using the GAAC deployment as proof. The Christian college athletic ecosystem is largely interconnected. Once GAAC proves out, the pitch to the broader ecosystem is not hypothetical.

The strategic consequence: KaNeXT's first mandate is not a cold pitch to a governing body. It is a warm deployment through a committed strategic partner who owns the conference, funds the partner institution, and has independent ambition to scale the conference into a national-footprint Christian athletic league. The relationship reduces time-to-mandate from years to quarters and de-risks the full mandate thesis by proving it first inside an owner-operator relationship rather than inside a governing body committee.

## What Every School Receives

**Hardware.** Two free Cam Sport cameras per school (approximately $130 COGS each). One indoor, one outdoor, covering the school's two primary athletic venues. The mandate covers every sport the school offers. A school with basketball, volleyball, soccer, baseball, and track uses the same cameras across all sports. Schools wanting additional venue coverage or enhanced capabilities purchase upgrades through the camera product line.

**Streaming.** Every game captured is streamed live on KTV and archived as VOD. The 48-hour replay standard ensures uploads occur after any existing broadcast windows close. KTV does not compete with existing conference media deals. It fills the gap where nothing exists. Schools can monetize live access (per-game pricing, season passes) and keep revenue minus the 5% processing fee.

**Automated statistics.** KVision processes every game into structured data: box scores, play-type tagging, possession-level efficiency, and player tracking. StatKeeper provides manual stat entry from day one. KVision upgrades every school to automated processing as the system deploys.

**Intelligence (Year 3+ licensing).** During the partner institution's Year 1-3 validation phase, the deep evaluation intelligence remains exclusive to the partner institution as the competitive moat. Mandate institutions receive the platform (OS, KTV, KPay, KVision, KStat) during this period but not the full evaluation engine. Small-scale ancillary intelligence products may be available Year 1-2 at commodity pricing. Full commercial intelligence licensing opens Year 3+ at institutional-IP tier after the partner institution demonstrates publicly attributable competitive outcomes (championships, D1 upsets, pro placements, enrollment growth).

**Merch store.** A fully managed, school-branded merchandise store. KaNeXT handles design, production, fulfillment, shipping, and customer service through the fulfillment center. Revenue share to school: 25-30% (industry standard through Fanatics is approximately 15%). Schools do zero work.

**Platform.** Full KaNeXT institutional operating system. In-app ticketing. In-app concessions. Scheduling tools. Eligibility and compliance tracking. Institutional communication through the VoIP layer. KPay wallet and commerce processing. Dipson AI assistance.

## The Pitch

The pitch is structurally difficult to refuse. Every member school receives free cameras, free streaming, free merch stores, free recruiting tools, free stats, free scheduling, and a free institutional operating system. The governing body pays nothing. The schools pay nothing. KaNeXT monetizes through 5% flat commerce processing on transactions. Schools keep 95% of revenue that did not exist before KaNeXT arrived.

Every objection has a structural answer. Cost: free. Hardware: provided. Broadcast rights: the 48-hour replay standard respects existing agreements. Vendor lock-in: uploads are camera-agnostic. Competitive imbalance: every school gets the same platform with no feature gating.

Simultaneous outreach across the core governing bodies is critical. If KaNeXT approaches one body first and word spreads, competitors could attempt counter-offers to the remaining ones. Approaching all at once creates urgency. The first body to sign signals legitimacy. The remaining bodies face the choice of joining or being left out. HS program invitations sequence behind collegiate body sign-ons, anchored to feeder relationships with partner institutions and mission alignment.

## Deployment Cost

The total deployment cost for the full mandate footprint is estimated at $2.6M-$4.5M:

Camera hardware: approximately 2,000+ units at approximately $130 COGS each = approximately $260,000-$300,000. Platform deployment: marginal cost per school on existing infrastructure. Fulfillment center setup costs are covered under the separate fulfillment budget. Operational support, onboarding, and training are the primary variable costs.

The cameras are the cheapest part. The value they create (KTV content, KVision data, platform adoption, commerce activity) is orders of magnitude larger than the hardware cost.

## What the Mandate Creates

**App installs.** The video mandate is projected to deliver an estimated 1.5 to 1.8 million app installs in Year 1. Parents and families of athletes account for the largest share. Prospective recruits download to evaluate schools. Fans and alumni follow their programs. Opposing coaches scout opponents. Scouts discover players.

**Commerce volume.** Every ticket sold, every hoodie ordered, every concession purchased, every donation made at a mandate institution flows through KPay at 5% processing. Revenue that did not exist before (because the infrastructure did not exist) is created by the platform and monetized through commerce processing.

**Intelligence data.** Every game filmed is processed by KVision into structured data that feeds the intelligence system. At 1,000+ institutions across 20+ games per sport per season, the mandate produces 20,000+ games of intelligence data per year covering competitive levels that no existing analytics provider covers.

**Wallet adoption.** Every user inside the app is one tap from a KPay wallet. The mandate creates the audience. KTV creates the viewing funnel. The wallet captures downstream financial activity.

**Deposit volume.** Commerce settlement deposits flow into KaNeXT Bank. Every mandate institution adds transaction volume to the deposit base. The mandate is a deposit acquisition engine for the bank charter.

## The Lock-In Progression

Video is the wedge. It is not the destination.

The school starts with video upload and replay (basic mandate compliance). Within weeks, roster management and scheduling move into KaNeXT because the data is already there. Within months, ticketing flows through KaNeXT settlement because the audience is already watching through the app. Within a semester, auto-generated stats and player profiles make KaNeXT the tool coaches use for game preparation. Within a year, recruiting tools and scouting intelligence make KaNeXT the system through which the school recruits.

The video mandate is the first touch. The institutional operating system is the outcome.

## Global Expansion

The problem the mandate solves is universal. Every country has sub-professional and amateur sports with zero video infrastructure. The mandate playbook is identical in every market: identify the governing body, pitch free cameras, free platform, free viewing, mandate upload.

The domestic deployment across the core governing bodies and select HS programs is the proof case. International expansion follows the same playbook through FIBA affiliates, national federations, and international leagues. The architecture is sport-agnostic. Basketball is the entry point, but KVision's Sport Truth Adapters process any sport.

## Terminology

For clarity across all data room documents:

**School Network** or **network schools**: institutions KaNeXT owns or controls through IOAs. Revenue from these schools is included in the institutional base case.

**Mandate institutions**: independent institutions using KaNeXT through governing body agreements. KaNeXT does not own or operate them. Revenue from mandate institutions is excluded from the institutional base case and documented separately.

**Platform**: the technology, financial, media, commerce, and intelligence infrastructure that runs across all institutions on all layers.
