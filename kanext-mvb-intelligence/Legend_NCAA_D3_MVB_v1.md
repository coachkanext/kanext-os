# Legend: NCAA D3 Men's Volleyball v1

## SCOPE

This legend defines what each KR tier means at the NCAA Division III level for men's volleyball. Display-only - interprets KR, does not produce KR.

All production benchmarks are per-set rates unless noted.

**NCAA D3 Men's Volleyball Landscape:**
NCAA D3 men's volleyball has more programs than D2 (approximately 80-100 programs), making it the largest division for men's volleyball by program count. D3 programs are concentrated in the Northeast, Midwest, and California.

Key D3 conferences include:
- **CVC (Continental Volleyball Conference)**
- **UVC (United Volleyball Conference)**
- **Midwest Collegiate Volleyball League (MCVL)**
- **NECVA (New England Collegiate Volleyball Association) - various tiers**
- Various regional conferences

**Scholarship context:** D3 offers zero athletic scholarships. All players are student-athletes receiving only academic or need-based aid. This means talent distribution is more even and programs compete on coaching quality, academic reputation, campus culture, and geographic convenience.

## D3-SPECIFIC NOTES

- D3 men's volleyball has a legitimate NCAA tournament with a larger bracket than D1 (more auto-bids due to more conferences).
- The top D3 programs (Springfield, Stevens Institute, Carthage, Nazareth) compete at a level that overlaps with weaker D2 programs.
- No athletic scholarships means recruiting is about academic fit, geography, and campus visit experience.
- Many D3 men's volleyball players are multi-sport athletes (volleyball + another sport).
- Late developers who picked up volleyball in high school often find D3 to be the ideal development environment.
- The absence of scholarship pressure creates a different team dynamic - more genuine student-athlete culture.
- D3 volleyball is growing. New programs are being added regularly.

---

## D3 NCAA TOURNAMENT CONTEXT

The D3 men's volleyball NCAA tournament is larger than D1's:
- Conference champions receive automatic bids
- At-large selections fill the bracket
- Pool play into elimination rounds
- Less media attention but highly competitive

Teams with Team KR of 85+ are typically tournament-caliber at D3.
