# Tournament and Event Properties

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

---

## Overview

KaNeXT owns and operates tournament and event properties across sports. These are not sponsorship activations or one-off showcases. They are owned institutional events where every KaNeXT engine is active, every dollar flows through governed settlement, every fan is inside the app, and every game feeds the intelligence system and content library simultaneously.

The events serve three functions. They are proof events (the entire KaNeXT ecosystem operating live on a national stage). They are content events (multi-day productions feeding KTV, generating broadcast value, and compounding the media library). And they are distribution events (concentrated app installs from every participating team's fanbase, every broadcast viewer, and every family following the action).

Basketball is the first sport because the intelligence deployment is deepest. The model is portable to any sport once the infrastructure is proven.

## The Postseason Tournament

Every year, hundreds of college basketball teams finish their season without a postseason invitation. D1 teams that narrowly missed the NCAA Tournament (Power Conference programs, strong mid-majors, conference tournament losers with 20-win seasons). Lower-division champions whose seasons ended without a national stage against higher-level competition. D2 champions, D3 champions, NAIA champions have never had the opportunity to play D1 competition in a structured, high-stakes environment.

KaNeXT builds the postseason that does not currently exist.

The tournament is a bracket format with escalating prize money at every round. The champion receives a package significant enough to meaningfully impact a program's recruiting budget. Athletic directors have incentive to participate because the money is real, the exposure is national through KTV and broadcast partnerships, and the competition is legitimate.

The competitive structure creates something unprecedented: cross-division competition where the intelligence system provides the analytical framework. KR ratings, team evaluations, and simulation projections give the tournament a data-driven narrative layer that traditional postseason events lack. Every game validates (or challenges) the intelligence publicly. The tournament becomes a live test of the evaluation system in front of a national audience.

The tournament starts with basketball. Once the model is proven, it extends to every sport where teams finish without a postseason invitation and lower-division champions have no opportunity to compete against higher-level programs. Soccer, baseball, volleyball, softball, football: the structure is repeatable.

## Showcase and Cultural Events

Separate from the postseason tournament, KaNeXT owns regular-season showcase events designed to put the partner institution and KaNeXT-powered teams on a national stage alongside cultural programming.

**Memorial Day Weekend Showcase.** Early-summer event featuring competitive games alongside community programming. D1 teams invited for guarantee-game-style competition. Faith-based community celebration, entrepreneurship programming, community engagement. ESPN+ broadcast potential through participating D1 media agreements.

**MLK Weekend Classic.** Mid-season event built around the Martin Luther King Jr. holiday. Cross-division competition. Cultural and community programming. National broadcast window during a weekend when college basketball has significant viewership.

**Additional properties** are developed as the calendar and the sports portfolio expand. Any sport where a marquee matchup generates media value and puts KaNeXT-powered teams on a national stage is a candidate for an owned event property.

These events are not just basketball games. The cultural programming, community engagement, and institutional storytelling generate media value beyond the athletic competition. The combination of high-level competition and mission-aligned programming produces more coverage and more brand value than either would alone.

## How the Events Operate

Every KaNeXT-owned event runs on the full platform.

**Intelligence.** The KaNeXT intelligence engines are active across every game: evaluating players, computing team strength, updating the 250,000+ athlete database, governing pregame through postgame intelligence, publishing simulations, and tracking development. The event is not just entertainment. It is a live intelligence operation producing structured data from every possession.

**Settlement.** All financial activity flows through governed settlement. Ticketing, sponsorship, broadcast revenue, vendor contracts, concessions, merchandise, travel stipends, and prize payouts all follow the canonical settlement chain with revenue splits defined before the first game tips.

**Content.** All games are broadcast live through KTV. Highlights generated automatically. Content enters the permanent library. Every game from every KaNeXT-owned event is available on-demand indefinitely, building a growing archive of high-level competition with intelligence overlay.

**Distribution.** All fans are inside the app. Tickets purchased through KPay. Merch through the fulfillment center. Brackets, KR ratings, and simulation projections accessible in-app. Every fan who attends or watches a KaNeXT-owned event is a potential platform user with one tap to a wallet, a phone number, and a merch store.

## Revenue Streams

Each event generates revenue across multiple channels simultaneously.

Ticket sales and gate revenue. Sponsorship and partnership deals. Broadcasting rights (KTV plus external broadcast partners). Merchandise sales through the fulfillment center. Concessions and vendor fees. Prize money underwriting (structured so that prize payouts are funded from event revenue, not fund capital). Every dollar settles through KPay.

The events are designed to be self-sustaining or profitable from Year 1 because the infrastructure (KTV, KPay, intelligence, fulfillment, cameras) already exists. The marginal cost of producing an event on existing infrastructure is substantially lower than building event infrastructure from scratch.

## Strategic Value

The tournament and event properties are not standalone businesses. They are proof, content, and distribution concentrated into multi-day productions.

**Proof.** The intelligence system makes public predictions. The tournament produces public outcomes. Every result that matches the intelligence projections builds credibility. Every upset that the intelligence flagged as possible builds credibility. The events are the highest-visibility demonstration of what the intelligence system can do.

**Content.** A multi-day tournament produces dozens of games across multiple days. Each game feeds KTV, the intelligence system, and the content library. A single tournament weekend can produce more content than many programs produce in an entire season.

**Distribution.** Every participating team brings its fanbase. Every fanbase downloads the app to watch, buy tickets, follow brackets, and purchase merch. A 16-team tournament with average fanbases of 5,000-10,000 supporters per team delivers 80,000-160,000 potential app installs in a single weekend.

**Intelligence validation.** The tournament produces outcomes against which the intelligence system's predictions can be publicly measured. This is the live proving ground for the KR system, the simulation engine, and the evaluation methodology.
