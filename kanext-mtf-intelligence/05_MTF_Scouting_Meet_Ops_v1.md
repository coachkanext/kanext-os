# MEN'S TRACK AND FIELD - SCOUTING AND MEET OPS v1

---

## 0. Purpose

Scouting and Meet Ops provides actionable intelligence before, during, and after competitions. Unlike team sports where in-game adjustments change lineups and play calls, track and field meet ops focuses on entry decisions, warm-up timing, tactical guidance for distance races, and relay adjustments.

---

## 1. PRE-MEET SCOUTING

### 1.1 Opponent Scouting Report

**For each opponent team, compile:**
- Top athlete in each event (name, SB, PR, KR if evaluated)
- Scoring threats by event (athletes likely to score)
- Relay competitiveness (projected times)
- Known strengths and weaknesses by event group
- Recent meet results and trends (improving or declining)
- Home-venue advantage factors (altitude, surface, elevation)

**Data Sources:**
1. TFRRS (primary for NCAA results and rankings)
2. Conference results/statistics websites
3. Program websites for roster information
4. World Athletics database for international athletes
5. Web search for recent meet recaps and results

### 1.2 Head-to-Head Analysis

For each event:
- List your athlete vs opponents' athletes
- Compare SBs and PRs
- Project placement order
- Identify toss-up events (marks within 1-2% of each other)
- Identify concede events (opponent clearly superior) and dominate events (you clearly superior)

### 1.3 Heat Sheet Analysis (Championship Meets)

When heat sheets are released before a championship meet:
- Identify heat assignments for your athletes
- Note which heat appears strongest (athletes may need to run faster in a strong heat to advance)
- Identify tactical considerations (e.g., known front-runners in your athlete's heat)
- Lane assignments for sprint events (inside vs outside lane implications)
- Flight assignments for field events (earlier vs later flights, conditions)

### 1.4 Venue Analysis

- Track surface type and condition
- Altitude (above 1000m requires distance adjustment awareness)
- Typical wind patterns at venue
- Field event runway/ring conditions
- Warm-up area proximity and quality
- Indoor: banked vs flat track, tight turns, surface
- Spectator environment (home crowd, neutrals)

### 1.5 Entry Recommendations

Based on scouting analysis, recommend:
- Optimal event entries for each athlete (max 3 individual events + relays at most meets)
- Which events to prioritize (highest point yield)
- Which events to concede (no realistic scoring opportunity)
- Relay team composition

## 2. MEET-DAY OPS

### 2.1 Schedule Management

Track meets run on complex schedules with events overlapping. Meet-day ops manages:
- Event start times for all entered athletes
- Warm-up timing (typically 60-90 min before running events, 30-60 min for field events)
- Conflicts: when an athlete is entered in events with overlapping schedules
- Check-in deadlines for each event
- Flight/heat call times for field events

### 2.2 Running Event Tactics (Pre-Race Brief)

**Sprints (100m, 200m):**
- Race plan: execute clean start, drive phase, maintain form
- No tactical complexity - run your race
- Note lane assignment and any curve start implications

**400m:**
- Split target: front half / back half split
- Pace plan: slightly positive split is normal (first 200m 0.5-1.0s faster than second)
- Lane awareness: where are the strongest competitors? Push early vs conserve?

**800m:**
- Tactical plan based on heat/field:
  - Fast race (rabbit, time trial): sit and follow, kick final 200m
  - Slow race (tactical): decide whether to push pace or wait for kick
  - Position: where to be at 200m, 400m, 600m marks
- Key opponents and their racing styles (front-runner, kicker, etc.)

**1500m/Mile:**
- Detailed tactical plan based on field:
  - Identify front-runners and kickers in heat
  - Position strategy: rail vs outside, covered vs exposed
  - When to move (with 600m to go? 400? 300? 200?)
  - Response plan for surges
- Split targets for even-pace vs tactical race

**5000m/10000m:**
- Lap-by-lap pace awareness
- Pack positioning strategy
- When to make a decisive move
- How to respond to surges
- Hydration/fueling (10000m in warm conditions)

**Steeplechase:**
- Water jump strategy (attack vs conservative)
- Barrier rhythm
- Pace plan (front-running is more common in steeple due to barrier positioning)

**Hurdles:**
- Start and first-hurdle plan
- Rhythm focus areas
- Wind adjustment for approach (110mH)
- Stride pattern plan for 400mH (which hurdles to switch lead leg if needed)

### 2.3 Field Event Tactics (Pre-Event Brief)

**High Jump / Pole Vault:**
- Opening height selection
- Progression strategy (when to pass)
- Attempt allocation at each height
- Competitor awareness (who passes what heights)
- PV: pole selection plan (starting pole, when to move up)

**Long Jump / Triple Jump:**
- Approach mark verification in warm-up
- Target mark for competition
- Attempt strategy: solid first attempt for safety, then attack
- Wind adaptation (adjust approach if windy)
- Foul management (if fouling on first attempt, adjust step)

**Throws:**
- Warm-up throws to find rhythm
- Attempt strategy: safe first throw, then attack
- Technical focus for the day (specific cue)
- Competition awareness: what mark is needed to make finals?

### 2.4 In-Meet Adjustments

**Between Rounds (Running Events):**
- Recovery protocol between prelim and final (timing, nutrition, warm-down/warm-up)
- Tactical adjustment based on prelim results and competitors advancing
- Split analysis from prelim to adjust final race plan

**Between Attempts (Field Events):**
- Technical adjustment based on early attempts
- Coaching cues between throws/jumps
- Competition position awareness (what mark is needed to advance to top-8 finals?)

**Relay Adjustments:**
- If an athlete underperformed in individual event, consider relay leg swap
- Exchange zone positioning adjustments based on warm-up
- If a relay team member is injured or fatigued, activate alternate

### 2.5 Multi-Day Meet Management (Conference/National Championships)

- Event scheduling across days
- Recovery between day 1 and day 2 events
- Athlete load management (how many events per day)
- Weather monitoring across multi-day meet
- Decathlete management (10 events across 2 days - the most complex load management)

## 3. POST-MEET ANALYSIS

### 3.1 Performance Review

For each athlete:
- Mark achieved vs SB vs PR
- Place finish vs projected finish
- Positive or negative variance from projection
- Component KR observations:
  - PKR: Did the athlete perform at expected level?
  - TKR: Any technical observations from meet performance?
  - CKR: How did the athlete perform under competition pressure?
  - IQKR: Were race tactics / competition decisions effective?

### 3.2 Team Scoring Analysis

- Actual team score vs projected score
- Events where team outperformed projection (and why)
- Events where team underperformed projection (and why)
- Swing events that could have changed outcome
- Relay performance review

### 3.3 Scouting Update

- Update opponent athlete SBs and PRs based on meet results
- Note any emerging athletes who performed above expectations
- Note any athletes who appear to be underperforming (injury? overtraining?)
- Update conference/regional/national landscape projections

### 3.4 Actionable Takeaways

- Training adjustments suggested based on meet performance
- Event entry changes for next meet
- Relay composition changes
- Tactical adjustments for athletes in distance/mid-distance events
- Technical focus areas identified from competition

## 4. CONFIDENCE GATES

### 4.1 Pre-Meet Scouting Confidence

| Data Availability | Confidence Level |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| Full season SBs for all athletes on TFRRS | High (85%+) |
| Partial season data, some athletes missing | Moderate (70-84%) |
| Limited data, early season meet | Low (50-69%) |
| No data on opponents | Insufficient - flag |

### 4.2 Simulation Confidence (for pre-meet scoring projections)

| Meet Type | Typical Confidence |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Dual meet, mid-to-late season | 80-90% |
| Conference championship | 70-85% |
| Regional qualifying | 65-80% |
| National championship | 55-75% |

### 4.3 Post-Meet Analysis Confidence

| Observation | Confidence Impact |
|-------------|------------------|
| Mark verified on TFRRS | No adjustment |
| Mark self-reported / unverified | -10% |
| Wind reading unavailable | -5% for affected events |
| Weather significantly affected meet | Note conditions, reduce CKR inference |
