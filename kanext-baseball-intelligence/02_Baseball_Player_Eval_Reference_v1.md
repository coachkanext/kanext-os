# HITTER TRAIT LIBRARY

## KaNeXT Hitter Trait Clusters — Canonical 5

### 1. Hitting (HKR)
Sub-traits defining pure hit tool and contact ability.

#### 1a) Contact Rate
- **Inputs:** K% (inverse), contact rate (foul + in-play / swings), zone contact %, chase contact %
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: K% ≤ 10%, contact rate ≥ 88%
  - 80: K% 11-15%, contact rate 83-87%
  - 70: K% 16-20%, contact rate 78-82%
  - 60: K% 21-25%, contact rate 72-77%
  - <60: K% > 25% or contact rate < 72%
- Box-score mode: PROXY — inputs: K%, AB, H
  - Score: 0.60 × Band(invK%) + 0.40 × Band(BA)

#### 1b) Batting Average / Hit Tool
- **Inputs:** BA, BABIP, line drive %, hard hit %
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: BA ≥ .340, BABIP ≥ .380
  - 80: BA .310-.339, BABIP .350-.379
  - 70: BA .280-.309, BABIP .320-.349
  - 60: BA .250-.279, BABIP .290-.319
  - <60: BA < .250 or BABIP < .290
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(BA)

#### 1c) Bat-to-Ball Quality
- **Inputs:** hard hit %, line drive %, ground ball %, barrel rate (if available via TrackMan)
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: hard hit ≥ 45%, LD ≥ 25%
  - 80: hard hit 38-44%, LD 21-24%
  - 70: hard hit 32-37%, LD 18-20%
  - 60: hard hit 26-31%, LD 15-17%
  - <60: hard hit < 26% or LD < 15%
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: 0.50 × Band(SLG) + 0.50 × Band(BA) (weak proxy)

#### 1d) Situational Hitting
- **Inputs:** BA w/RISP, 2-out RBI rate, clutch hitting metrics, sac bunt/fly proficiency
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: RISP BA ≥ .370, elite 2-out production
  - 80: RISP BA .330-.369
  - 70: RISP BA .290-.329
  - 60: RISP BA .250-.289
  - <60: RISP BA < .250
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(RBI context-adjusted) (low confidence)

#### 1e) Two-Strike Hitting
- **Inputs:** BA with 2 strikes, K% with 2 strikes, approach change (expanded zone swing rate change)
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: 2-strike BA ≥ .280, minimal K% increase
  - 80: 2-strike BA .240-.279
  - 70: 2-strike BA .200-.239
  - 60: 2-strike BA .170-.199
  - <60: 2-strike BA < .170
- Box-score mode: UNSCORED (null)

### 2. Plate Discipline / Power (PKR)
Sub-traits defining power output and approach quality.

#### 2a) Walk Rate / Plate Discipline
- **Inputs:** BB%, OBP-BA (isolated walk contribution), pitches per plate appearance
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: BB% ≥ 15%, P/PA ≥ 4.2
  - 80: BB% 12-14%, P/PA 3.9-4.1
  - 70: BB% 9-11%, P/PA 3.6-3.8
  - 60: BB% 6-8%, P/PA 3.3-3.5
  - <60: BB% < 6% or P/PA < 3.3
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(BB%)

#### 2b) Chase Rate / Swing Decisions
- **Inputs:** O-Swing% (chase rate), Z-Swing% (zone swing), SwStr% (whiff rate)
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: chase rate ≤ 20%, whiff rate ≤ 15%
  - 80: chase rate 21-25%, whiff rate 16-20%
  - 70: chase rate 26-30%, whiff rate 21-25%
  - 60: chase rate 31-35%, whiff rate 26-30%
  - <60: chase rate > 35% or whiff rate > 30%
- Box-score mode: UNSCORED (null) — requires pitch-level data

#### 2c) Raw Power (Home Runs / ISO)
- **Inputs:** HR, ISO (SLG-BA), HR/FB%, max exit velocity (TrackMan)
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: ISO ≥ .280, HR ≥ 20 (56-game season)
  - 80: ISO .220-.279, HR 14-19
  - 70: ISO .160-.219, HR 8-13
  - 60: ISO .100-.159, HR 4-7
  - <60: ISO < .100 or HR < 4
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: 0.60 × Band(ISO) + 0.40 × Band(HR)

#### 2d) Hard Hit Rate / Exit Velocity
- **Inputs:** avg exit velocity, hard hit % (≥95 mph), barrel % (TrackMan/Statcast)
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: avg EV ≥ 92 mph, hard hit ≥ 48%
  - 80: avg EV 89-91, hard hit 40-47%
  - 70: avg EV 86-88, hard hit 33-39%
  - 60: avg EV 83-85, hard hit 26-32%
  - <60: avg EV < 83 or hard hit < 26%
- Box-score mode: UNSCORED (null) — requires TrackMan

#### 2e) Slugging Efficiency
- **Inputs:** SLG, OPS, wOBA, wRC+
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: wRC+ ≥ 160, OPS ≥ 1.050
  - 80: wRC+ 140-159, OPS .950-1.049
  - 70: wRC+ 115-139, OPS .830-.949
  - 60: wRC+ 95-114, OPS .720-.829
  - <60: wRC+ < 95 or OPS < .720
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: 0.50 × Band(OPS) + 0.50 × Band(SLG)

### 3. Fielding (FKR)
Sub-traits defining defensive value at position.

#### 3a) Range / Defensive Runs
- **Inputs:** range factor, DRS (defensive runs saved), UZR (if available), OAA (outs above average)
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: elite range for position, top 5% of level
  - 80: above-average range, top 20%
  - 70: average range
  - 60: below-average range
  - <60: liability range
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(assists/G for IF, putouts/G for OF) (weak proxy)

#### 3b) Fielding Percentage / Reliability
- **Inputs:** fielding %, errors, error rate per chance
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: FPCT ≥ .985 (IF) / ≥ .995 (OF)
  - 80: FPCT .975-.984 (IF) / .990-.994 (OF)
  - 70: FPCT .965-.974 (IF) / .985-.989 (OF)
  - 60: FPCT .955-.964 (IF) / .975-.984 (OF)
  - <60: FPCT < .955 (IF) / < .975 (OF)
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(FPCT)

#### 3c) Arm Strength / Accuracy
- **Inputs:** arm strength grade (20-80 scale if available), outfield assists, catcher pop time, IF throw velocity
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: 70+ arm on 20-80 scale, elite OF assists or pop time ≤ 1.85
  - 80: 60-65 arm, above-avg assists or pop time 1.86-1.95
  - 70: 50-55 arm, average assists or pop time 1.96-2.05
  - 60: 40-45 arm, below-avg
  - <60: 30 arm, liability
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(OF assists/G or CS% for catchers) (low confidence)

#### 3d) Double Play Ability (IF only)
- **Inputs:** DP turned, DP pivot speed, feeds/turns/starts per opportunity
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: elite DP rate, clean turns/feeds
  - 80: above-average DP
  - 70: average
  - 60: below-average, slow pivot
  - <60: liability, avoids contact
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(DP/G) for IF positions; UNSCORED for OF

#### 3e) Catcher-Specific: Receiving / Framing
- **Inputs:** framing runs (if available), called strike rate above expected, receiving grade
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: elite framing, consistently expands zone
  - 80: above-average framing
  - 70: average receiving
  - 60: below-average, loses strikes
  - <60: poor receiver, costs team strikes consistently
- Box-score mode: UNSCORED (null) — requires pitch-level data

#### 3f) Catcher-Specific: Throwing / CS%
- **Inputs:** CS%, pop time, pickoff assists
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: CS% ≥ 40%, pop time ≤ 1.85
  - 80: CS% 33-39%, pop time 1.86-1.92
  - 70: CS% 26-32%, pop time 1.93-2.00
  - 60: CS% 20-25%, pop time 2.01-2.08
  - <60: CS% < 20% or pop time > 2.08
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(CS%)

### 4. Speed / Baserunning (SKR)
Sub-traits defining running game and baserunning value.

#### 4a) Stolen Bases / Success Rate
- **Inputs:** SB, SB%, SB attempts, caught stealing
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: SB ≥ 30 & SB% ≥ 85%
  - 80: SB 20-29 & SB% ≥ 80%
  - 70: SB 12-19 & SB% ≥ 75%
  - 60: SB 5-11 & SB% ≥ 70%
  - <60: SB < 5 or SB% < 70%
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: 0.60 × Band(SB) + 0.40 × Band(SB%)

#### 4b) Sprint Speed / Raw Speed
- **Inputs:** 60-yard dash time, sprint speed (ft/s if Statcast), home-to-first time
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: 60-yard ≤ 6.4s or sprint speed ≥ 30 ft/s
  - 80: 60-yard 6.5-6.6 or sprint speed 28.5-29.9
  - 70: 60-yard 6.7-6.9 or sprint speed 27.0-28.4
  - 60: 60-yard 7.0-7.2 or sprint speed 25.5-26.9
  - <60: 60-yard > 7.2 or sprint speed < 25.5
- Box-score mode: UNSCORED (null) — requires timed data

#### 4c) Extra Bases Taken / Baserunning Aggression
- **Inputs:** extra bases taken %, first-to-third on singles, scored from second on singles, advanced on fly balls
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: takes extra base ≥ 55% of opportunities
  - 80: 45-54%
  - 70: 35-44%
  - 60: 25-34%
  - <60: < 25%
- Box-score mode: UNSCORED (null)

#### 4d) Baserunning Runs / Value
- **Inputs:** BsR (baserunning runs above average), total bases taken, outs on bases
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: elite BsR, never thrown out on bases
  - 80: above-average BsR
  - 70: average baserunner
  - 60: below-average, occasional baserunning mistakes
  - <60: baserunning liability, frequent outs
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(SB - CS) (weak proxy)

### 5. Baseball IQ (IQKR — Hitter)
Sub-traits defining mental processing and awareness.

#### 5a) Plate Approach Adjustments
- **Inputs:** performance improvement across ABs in same game (2nd/3rd time through), adjustment to pitcher style, count leverage
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: elite in-game adjustment, consistently better in later ABs
  - 80: above-average adjustment
  - 70: average
  - 60: slow to adjust
  - <60: no discernible adjustment patterns
- Box-score mode: UNSCORED (null)

#### 5b) Situational Awareness
- **Inputs:** productive outs (advancing runners, sac flies), RISP performance relative to overall, runner-on-third production
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: consistently produces in high-leverage situations
  - 80: above-average situational play
  - 70: average
  - 60: below-average situational awareness
  - <60: poor in situational play
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(RBI/opportunity estimate)

#### 5c) Baserunning Decision Quality
- **Inputs:** outs on bases rate, correct reads on fly balls, stolen base timing, hit-and-run execution
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: near-zero baserunning errors, elite reads
  - 80: rare mistakes
  - 70: average decisions
  - 60: occasional poor decisions
  - <60: frequent baserunning blunders
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(CS rate inverse) (low confidence)

#### 5d) Defensive Positioning Instincts
- **Inputs:** pre-pitch positioning adjustments, first-step accuracy, anticipation of batted ball direction
- Box-score mode: UNSCORED (null) — requires film

#### 5e) Clutch Performance / Pressure
- **Inputs:** performance in high-leverage PAs, late-and-close situations, postseason/rivalry performance
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: dramatically elevated in high leverage
  - 80: performs at or above baseline in pressure
  - 70: neutral — no significant split
  - 60: slight decline in pressure
  - <60: significant decline in clutch situations
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(late-and-close OPS vs overall OPS) (low confidence)

## KaNeXT Pitcher Trait Clusters — Canonical 5

### 1. Velocity / Stuff (VKR)
Sub-traits defining pitch quality and raw stuff.

#### 1a) Fastball Velocity
- **Inputs:** average FB velocity, max FB velocity, velocity sustain (late-inning velocity vs early)
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: avg FB ≥ 95 mph, sustains deep in games
  - 80: avg FB 92-94
  - 70: avg FB 89-91
  - 60: avg FB 86-88
  - <60: avg FB < 86
- Box-score mode: UNSCORED (null) — requires radar/TrackMan

#### 1b) Fastball Movement / Ride
- **Inputs:** induced vertical break (IVB), horizontal break, spin rate, extension
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: elite ride (IVB ≥ 18") or elite run/sink with above-avg velo
  - 80: above-avg movement profile
  - 70: average movement
  - 60: below-average movement, flat FB
  - <60: hittable FB, poor shape
- Box-score mode: UNSCORED (null)

#### 1c) Breaking Ball Quality
- **Inputs:** whiff% on breaking balls (curve/slider), chase rate, spin rate, break depth/horizontal
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: whiff% ≥ 35% on breakers, elite chase rate generation
  - 80: whiff% 28-34%
  - 70: whiff% 22-27%
  - 60: whiff% 16-21%
  - <60: whiff% < 16%
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: 0.70 × Band(K/9) + 0.30 × Band(opponent BA) (weak proxy for stuff)

#### 1d) Offspeed Quality
- **Inputs:** changeup whiff%, velo differential from FB, platoon split reduction, chase rate on offspeed
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: elite changeup with ≥ 30% whiff rate, neutralizes opposite-side hitters
  - 80: quality changeup, above-avg whiff
  - 70: functional offspeed, average whiff
  - 60: inconsistent offspeed
  - <60: no reliable offspeed pitch
- Box-score mode: UNSCORED (null)

#### 1e) Overall Stuff+ / Pitch Quality Composite
- **Inputs:** Stuff+ (if available), aggregate pitch quality scores, swing-and-miss rate across arsenal
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: Stuff+ ≥ 130 equivalent
  - 80: Stuff+ 115-129
  - 70: Stuff+ 100-114
  - 60: Stuff+ 85-99
  - <60: Stuff+ < 85
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(K/9) (moderate proxy)

### 3. Durability (DKR)

  - 70: IP 80-94, avg 5.5-5.9
  - 60: IP 60-79, avg 4.5-5.4

  - 80: 28-34 appearances
  - 70: 22-27 appearances
  - 60: 15-21 appearances
  - <60: < 15 appearances

#### 3b) Deep-Game Performance (SP only)

  - 70: QS% 45-59%
  - 60: QS% 30-44%
  - <60: QS% < 30%
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(QS%)

#### 3c) Health / Workload History
- **Inputs:** games missed to injury, DL stints, pitch count trends, velocity decline patterns
- College bands (v0):

  - 80: minor injury history, ≤ 1 missed start
  - 70: some injury concern, ≤ 3 missed starts
  - 60: significant injury history
  - <60: major arm injury (TJ surgery, shoulder surgery)
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(starts made / starts scheduled) (low confidence)

#### 3d) Pitch Count Efficiency

- College bands (v0):

  - 80: 14.1-15.0 P/IP
  - 70: 15.1-16.0 P/IP
  - 60: 16.1-17.5 P/IP
  - <60: > 17.5 P/IP
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(IP/start proxy for efficiency)

### 4. Repertoire (RKR)

#### 4a) Pitch Mix Diversity
- **Inputs:** number of pitches thrown ≥ 10% of the time, effective pitch count (pitches with ≥ 50th percentile quality)
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: 4+ quality pitches, all above average
  - 80: 3 quality pitches + 1 show pitch
  - 70: 3 quality pitches
  - 60: 2 quality pitches
  - <60: 1 pitch (fastball only)
- Box-score mode: UNSCORED (null)

#### 4b) Putaway Pitch Quality
- **Inputs:** 2-strike whiff rate, putaway rate (K per 2-strike count), best secondary pitch quality
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: putaway rate ≥ 35%, elite 2-strike whiff
  - 80: putaway rate 28-34%
  - 70: putaway rate 22-27%
  - 60: putaway rate 16-21%
  - <60: putaway rate < 16%
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(K/9) (moderate proxy)

#### 4c) Platoon Resistance (L/R Splits)
- **Inputs:** OPS split (same-side vs opposite-side hitters), BA split, wOBA split
- College bands (v0):
  - 90: OPS split ≤ .030 (essentially no platoon disadvantage)
  - 80: OPS split .031-.060
  - 70: OPS split .061-.100
  - 60: OPS split .101-.150
  - <60: OPS split > .150
- Box-score mode: PROXY — Score: Band(overall effectiveness with limited split data) (low confidence)

# OFFENSIVE SYSTEM DEMAND PROFILES

## UI Offensive System Set — Baseball (5 Systems)

### 1. Launch Angle / Power (Houston/NYY Model)
**Philosophy:** Optimize for damage per contact. Sacrifice some contact rate for power output. Launch angle optimization, elevated fly ball approach, three-true-outcomes tolerance.
**Key Demands:**
- HKR: Bat-to-Ball Quality (hard hit priority over BA) — Weight: Medium
- PKR: Raw Power ≥ 78, Hard Hit Rate ≥ 78, Slugging Efficiency ≥ 80 — Weight: Critical
- FKR: Positional flexibility secondary — Weight: Low
- SKR: Not prioritized — Weight: Low
- IQKR: Count leverage, launch angle awareness — Weight: Medium
**System Profile:** High HR, high K%, high SLG, lower BA tolerance, ISO-driven
**Archetype Fit (A-tier):** Power Hitter, Middle-of-Order Run Producer, Five-Tool Player
**Archetype Fit (B-tier):** Power-Speed Hybrid, OBP Machine
**Archetype No-Match:** Contact Hitter (Pure), Speed Merchant, Platoon Bat

### 2. Contact / Speed (Kansas City Model)
**Philosophy:** Put the ball in play, use speed to create pressure, manufacture runs. Low strikeout, high contact, aggressive baserunning. Force errors and defensive misplays.
**Key Demands:**
- HKR: Contact Rate ≥ 80, Batting Average ≥ 78 — Weight: Critical
- PKR: Walk Rate ≥ 72 (not power-dependent) — Weight: Medium
- FKR: Range and reliability — Weight: Medium
- SKR: Sprint Speed ≥ 78, Stolen Bases ≥ 76, Baserunning Aggression ≥ 75 — Weight: Critical
- IQKR: Baserunning Decision Quality ≥ 78, Situational Awareness ≥ 76 — Weight: High
**System Profile:** High BA, low K%, high SB, aggressive on bases, manufactured runs
**Archetype Fit (A-tier):** Table Setter, Contact Hitter, Speed Merchant, Five-Tool Player
**Archetype Fit (B-tier):** Power-Speed Hybrid, Super Utility
**Archetype No-Match:** Power Hitter (Pure), Platoon Bat

### 3. Balanced / Complete (Los Angeles Dodgers Model)
**Philosophy:** No single approach dominates. Balanced lineup construction with OBP floor, moderate power, capable defense, and enough speed. Adaptable game-to-game.
**Key Demands:**
- HKR: Contact Rate ≥ 74, BA ≥ 74 — Weight: Medium
- PKR: Walk Rate ≥ 74, Power ≥ 74 — Weight: Medium
- FKR: Range ≥ 72, reliability ≥ 74 — Weight: Medium
- SKR: Sprint Speed ≥ 70, Baserunning ≥ 70 — Weight: Medium
- IQKR: Situational Awareness ≥ 76 — Weight: High
**System Profile:** Balanced slash lines, versatile lineup, multiple ways to score
**Archetype Fit (A-tier):** Five-Tool Player, OBP Machine, Power-Speed Hybrid
**Archetype Fit (B-tier):** All archetypes except extreme specialists fit at B-tier
**Archetype No-Match:** None — balanced system accommodates all profiles with diminishing fit for extreme specialists

### 4. Small Ball / Traditional
**Philosophy:** Manufacture runs through bunting, hit-and-run, stolen bases, sacrifice plays, and aggressive baserunning. Prioritizes contact and execution over power.
**Key Demands:**
- HKR: Contact Rate ≥ 80, Situational Hitting ≥ 80, Two-Strike Hitting ≥ 76 — Weight: Critical
- PKR: Walk Rate ≥ 72 — Weight: Medium
- FKR: Reliability ≥ 76 — Weight: Medium
- SKR: Sprint Speed ≥ 76, Stolen Bases ≥ 76, Baserunning Decision Quality ≥ 78 — Weight: Critical
- IQKR: Situational Awareness ≥ 80, Baserunning Decisions ≥ 80 — Weight: Critical
**System Profile:** Low power, high BA, high SB, sac bunts, hit-and-run, run manufacturing
**Archetype Fit (A-tier):** Table Setter, Contact Hitter, Speed Merchant
**Archetype Fit (B-tier):** Complete Catcher, Super Utility
**Archetype No-Match:** Power Hitter (Pure), OBP Machine (if walks-only, no speed)

### 5. OBP-Focused / Moneyball
**Philosophy:** On-base percentage is the foundational offensive metric. Walks are as valuable as hits. Patient at-bats, deep counts, wear down opposing pitchers. Power is welcome but OBP floor is non-negotiable.
**Key Demands:**
- HKR: Contact Rate ≥ 72 — Weight: Medium
- PKR: Walk Rate ≥ 82, Chase Rate ≥ 80, Plate Discipline ≥ 80, Pitches/PA ≥ 78 — Weight: Critical
- FKR: Not specifically prioritized — Weight: Low
- SKR: Not specifically prioritized — Weight: Low
- IQKR: Plate Approach Adjustments ≥ 78, Situational Awareness ≥ 74 — Weight: High
**System Profile:** High OBP, high BB%, deep counts, pitch count damage to opposing staff
**Archetype Fit (A-tier):** OBP Machine, Table Setter, Contact Hitter
**Archetype Fit (B-tier):** Power Hitter (if walks too), Five-Tool Player
**Archetype No-Match:** Speed Merchant (if low OBP), Platoon Bat (if low walk rate)

**Key Pitcher Demands:**
- VKR: Stuff ≥ 76 — Weight: High

# SYSTEM RISKS

## System Risks — Baseball v1

### Purpose
System Risks identify specific weaknesses that break or limit how a team system functions. They capture damage that individual trait scores alone do not.

### Severity Levels
- **Tier 1 Major (Scheme-Breaking):** Cannot be managed through roster construction
- **Tier 2 Major (Scheme-Limiting):** Manageable with complementary personnel
- **Minor:** Marginal but measurable

### KR Impact
| Severity | College | Pro |
|----------|---------|-----|
| Tier 1 Major | -2.0 | -4.0 |
| Tier 2 Major | -1.5 | -2.5 |
| Minor | -1.0 | -1.0 |

### HITTER SYSTEM RISKS

**TIER 1 MAJOR — 4 Total**

1. **Strikeout Liability (Major)**
- Trigger: K% ≥ 30% with ≤ .220 BA
- System damage: Dead at-bats. No contact pressure. Pitchers work around the lineup.

2. **Defensive Black Hole**
- Trigger: FKR ≤ 55 at a premium defensive position (C, SS, CF)
- System damage: Opponent targets the position. Run prevention collapses.

3. **Baserunning Catastrophe**
- Trigger: ≥ 8 outs on bases in a season (non-CS) OR CS rate ≥ 40% with ≥ 15 attempts
- System damage: Kills rallies. Removes runners from scoring position. Morale damage.

4. **Complete Non-Factor**
- Trigger: wRC+ ≤ 70 AND FKR ≤ 65 AND SKR ≤ 65
- System damage: Provides zero value in any dimension. Replacement-level across the board.

**TIER 2 MAJOR — 4 Total**

5. **Power Void (Lineup)**
- Trigger: ISO ≤ .080 with ≥ 200 AB
- System damage: Lineup has no extra-base threat at this spot. Pitchers attack with impunity.
- Position-scaled: 1B/DH/LF → -2.0 | 3B/RF → -1.5 | 2B/SS/CF → -1.0 | C → -0.5

6. **Platoon Exposed**
- Trigger: OPS ≤ .550 vs one handedness with ≥ 50 PA
- System damage: Opponent exploits platoon disadvantage with bullpen matchups.

7. **Speed Void (Contact/Speed system only)**
- Trigger: Sprint Speed bottom 20% at position AND SB ≤ 2 in system requiring speed
- System damage: Contact/Speed or Small Ball system cannot function without speed in the lineup.

8. **Situational Failure**
- Trigger: RISP BA ≤ .180 with ≥ 40 RISP AB
- System damage: Rally killer. Strands runners consistently.

**MINOR — 4 Total**

9. **Elevated Strikeout Rate** — K% 25-29%
10. **Limited Range** — Range below positional average but not liability
11. **Slow Starter** — BA ≤ .220 in first 15 games with significant improvement after
12. **One-Dimensional** — Only 1 component KR ≥ 75, all others ≤ 65

### PITCHER SYSTEM RISKS

**TIER 1 MAJOR — 4 Total**

1. **Walk Machine (Major)**
- Trigger: BB/9 ≥ 5.5 OR BB% ≥ 14%
- System damage: Free baserunners destroy pitching-driven systems. Pitch count inflation, bullpen stress.

2. **Home Run Prone**
- Trigger: HR/9 ≥ 1.8 OR HR/FB% ≥ 20%
- System damage: Gives up big innings. Momentum killer. Particularly devastating in small parks.

3. **Velocity Cliff**
- Trigger: FB velocity dropped ≥ 4 mph from prior season AND no injury explanation
- System damage: Stuff quality declining. Previous performance not sustainable.

4. **Injury Bomb**
- Trigger: 2+ TJ surgeries OR currently returning from major arm surgery with ≤ 30 IP since return
- System damage: Cannot be relied upon for workload. Rotation construction around uncertainty.

**TIER 2 MAJOR — 3 Total**

5. **Third-Time-Through Collapse**
- Trigger: ERA 3rd time through ≥ 2× ERA 1st time through with ≥ 30 PA sample
- System damage: Starter cannot go deep. Forces early bullpen usage.

6. **Platoon Exploitable (Pitcher)**
- Trigger: OPS allowed ≥ .900 vs one handedness with ≥ 50 PA
- System damage: Opponent stacks lineup from advantaged side.

7. **Fastball Avoidance**
- Trigger: FB usage ≤ 35% AND FB quality below average (whiff < 18%, called str < 28%)
- System damage: Hitters can sit offspeed. Limited approach options when behind in count.

**MINOR — 3 Total**

8. **Elevated Walk Rate** — BB/9 4.0-5.4
9. **Fly Ball Tendency** — FB% ≥ 45% (risk in hitter-friendly parks)
10. **Inconsistency** — ERA std deviation across starts ≥ 3.0 (boom-or-bust)

# IMPACT MODIFIERS

## Impact Modifiers — Baseball v1

### Purpose
KR measures how much a player impacts winning. Impact Modifiers classify the MODE by which that impact is produced.

### Assignment Rules
One modifier max per player. Evaluate in strict precedence. First match wins.

### Hitter Impact Modifiers

**1) Primary Offensive Engine**
- Trigger: wRC+ ≥ 145, wOBA ≥ .400, ≥ 55% of team's lineup run production comes through this player's PA/running game
- Note: Lineup is built around this player.

**2) Secondary Offensive Engine**
- Trigger: wRC+ 120-144, wOBA .360-.399, provides consistent but not anchor-level production

**3) Force Multiplier**
- Trigger: wRC+ ≤ 130, but On/Off team scoring differential ≥ +1.5 runs/game
- Multiplier Triggers (need ≥ 2): OBP ≥ .400, SB ≥ 20, FKR ≥ 82, Clutch wOBA ≥ .380

**4) Specialist Anchor**
- Trigger: One elite dimension (e.g., SB ≥ 40 OR FKR ≥ 88 OR CS% ≥ 45% at C)
- Exactly one elite signal true. If 2+, route to Force Multiplier.

### Pitcher Impact Modifiers

**1) Primary Staff Anchor**
- Trigger: ≥ 20% of team IP, ERA ≤ 75th percentile at level, team W% in their starts ≥ .600

**2) Bullpen Anchor**
- Trigger: ≥ 20 high-leverage appearances, ERA ≤ 2.50 in high leverage, WPA top 10% of RP at level

**3) Innings Stabilizer**
- Trigger: ≥ 100 IP, ERA within 0.50 of team ERA, QS% ≥ 55%
- Note: Not dominant, but reliable depth.

**4) Matchup Weapon**
- Trigger: Elite performance vs one handedness (OPS ≤ .500 vs target hand, ≥ 30 PA)

# BPIS (Baseball Player Impact Score)

## KaNeXT — Baseball Player Impact Score (BPIS)
### (Underlying Metric: BPR — Baseball Performance Rating)

### 1. Purpose
BPIS measures actual on-field impact in a single game, relative to competition level. It answers: "When this player was in the game, how much better or worse was the team because of them?"

### 2. Core Principles
- BPIS = 0 → average impact for the player's competitive level
- BPIS > 0 → positive impact
- BPIS < 0 → negative impact
- Level-relative, not universal

### 3. Internal Interpretation Bands
- +8 and above → Game-warping (e.g., complete game shutout, 4-HR game)
- +4 to +7 → Clear winning driver
- +1 to +3 → Reliable positive contributor
- -1 to +1 → Neutral
- -2 to -4 → Negative impact
- -5 to -7 → Strong negative (e.g., error-filled game, blown save)
- -8 and below → Severe negative

### 4. Relationship Between BPIS and KR
Same as basketball: KR = Player Identity; BPIS = Single-Game Reality. They are related but not redundant.

### 5. Governance
BPIS is never edited manually, never coach-adjustable, never sandbox-editable.
