# DEVELOPMENT INTELLIGENCE ENGINE

## 0. Purpose
Takes everything the system knows about a player and translates it into actionable intelligence for placement, development, and decision-making.

Answers five questions:
1. Where are you now? - Truth summary across every level and system
2. Where should you be? - Best-fit targets ranked by actual team impact
3. What are you worth there? - Value at each target (scholarship, financial aid implication)
4. What's the gap? - Specific traits holding you back, with exact deltas needed
5. What's the path? - Prioritized development roadmap with projected impact

Does NOT evaluate players. Does NOT modify Player KR, Team KR, archetypes, or any upstream output.

## 1. Inputs (Non-Negotiable)
A) Player Identity + Record
B) Player KR Outputs (Truth): Final KR, component KRs (AKR, SKR, QKR, DKR, IQKR), confidence_pct
C) Archetype + Badges + Impact Modifiers + System Risks
D) Full System Fit Profile (all 6 offensive + 5 defensive system fit scores)
E) Trait Scores (Raw): All scored traits
F) Level Interpretation: Level Tier Map at every relevant level
G) Physical Profile: height, weight, speed (if tested), age
H) Other Sports History: track times, soccer/basketball background, athletic testing from other sports

## 2. Outputs

### OUTPUT A: Truth Summary - "Where Are You Now?"
A1) KR Identity Card: Final KR, range, confidence, component KRs, archetype, badges, impact modifier, system risks.
A2) Level Tier Map: What the KR means at every relevant level. One player, one KR, multiple legend reads.
A3) System Fit Profile: Top-3 best-fit offensive systems and top-3 best-fit defensive systems, with fit % and demand tier.

### OUTPUT B: Placement Targeting - "Where Should You Be?"
B1) Level-Appropriate Targets: Programs where this player's KR falls in the "High-Impact Starter" or "Solid Starter" tier. Playing at a level where you're a bench player is development death.
B2) System-Fit Targets: Programs running systems where this player's archetype is A or B tier demand.
B3) Financial Aid Probability: Likelihood of athletic aid at each target level, given current scholarship landscape.

### OUTPUT C: Player Value - "What Are You Worth?"
C1) Transfer Portal Value: KR x System Fit% x Eligibility Remaining x Position Value. Higher for QBs and WR1s.
C2) Two-Way Value Premium: Players who contribute on both sides are worth more at roster-limited programs.
C3) Financial Aid Value: Athletic + academic + need-based aid estimate at target levels.

### OUTPUT D: Gap Analysis - "What's Holding You Back?"
D1) Trait Gap Table: For each scored trait, show current value, target value for the next tier, and the delta.
D2) Highest-Leverage Gaps: The 3-5 traits where improvement would produce the largest KR lift, accounting for position weighting.
D3) System Risk Resolution: Which trait improvements would remove current system risks? What's the threshold?

### OUTPUT E: Development Roadmap - "What's the Path?"

#### Position-Specific Development Plans

**QB Development:**
- Year 1 priorities: Accuracy (short), Decision-Making Under Pressure, Release Speed
- Year 2 priorities: Accuracy (intermediate/deep), Scramble Ability refinement, Play Calling/Audible
- Year 3 priorities: Arm Talent polish, Leadership, Full route tree command
- Measurable targets: Completion % progression, INT rate decline, TD:INT improvement
- Athletic targets: Speed maintenance, agility improvement, scramble efficiency

**WR Development:**
- Year 1 priorities: Route Running - Precision (master 3-4 routes perfectly), Hands/Catching consistency
- Year 2 priorities: Route Running - Breadth (expand to 6-8 routes), YAC improvement, Evasion Moves
- Year 3 priorities: Full route tree, Double-move mastery, Two-way contribution
- Measurable targets: Catch rate progression, YPR improvement, drop rate decline
- Athletic targets: Speed maintenance or improvement, agility refinement

**RB Development:**
- Year 1: Vision/Patience (reading defensive alignment), Ball Security, Receiving ability
- Year 2: Evasion Moves expansion, Route running from backfield
- Year 3: Complete back capability (run, catch, create)
- Measurable targets: YPC progression, receiving production growth
- Athletic targets: Agility, lateral quickness

**Center Development:**
- Year 1: Snap consistency (zero bad snaps is the goal), Basic receiving routes post-snap
- Year 2: Expand receiving route tree, Pre-snap read communication
- Year 3: Full receiving threat, Play design understanding, Leadership in formation communication
- Measurable targets: Bad snap reduction, receiving production growth
- Critical note: Center development is the most unique in flag football. No other sport has a position that starts every play with a specific physical action (snap) and then must immediately transition to a completely different skill (receiving).

**Defensive Development:**
- Year 1: Flag Pull technique (THE fundamental skill), Man coverage basics (footwork, hip turn, mirror)
- Year 2: Zone coverage (pattern reads, QB eye reading), Ball skills development
- Year 3: Defensive versatility (rush + cover + spy), Leadership in coverage calls
- Measurable targets: Flag pull rate improvement, INTs per season, PBU rate
- Athletic targets: Lateral quickness, recovery speed

**Two-Way Player Development:**
- Year 1: Master primary side first. Secondary side is effort and athleticism.
- Year 2: Develop technical skills on secondary side. Maintain primary side production.
- Year 3: Equal contribution on both sides. Endurance training is critical.
- Special consideration: Two-way fatigue management. Practice and game load must be monitored. Overuse leads to decline on both sides.

#### Development Timeline Expectations

Flag football skill development is FASTER than tackle football for several reasons:
1. No blocking/tackling technique to learn (eliminates years of technique development)
2. Fewer positions to master (7 vs 22)
3. Skills transfer from other sports (track speed, soccer agility, basketball court vision)
4. Lower injury risk allows more practice volume

Expected KR improvement trajectories:
- Multi-sport athlete entering flag football: +5-10 KR in Year 1 (rapid skill acquisition)
- Experienced flag player transferring up a level: +2-5 KR in Year 1 (competition adjustment)
- Returning starter developing within level: +3-6 KR per year (steady improvement)
- Maximum realistic improvement: +15 KR over 3 years (rare, requires elite coaching and athlete commitment)

# PRO TRANSITION INTELLIGENCE ENGINE

## 0. Purpose
Evaluates whether a college flag football player has a pathway to professional or international competition, and what that pathway looks like.

## 1. Pro Pathway Landscape (2026)

### NFL / TMRW Sports Professional Flag Football League
- Announced March 2026 (NFL + TMRW Sports partnership)
- Men's and women's leagues planned
- Launch timeline aligned with run-up to 2028 Olympics
- Structure, roster sizes, draft format TBD
- Expected to be the premier professional destination

### 2028 Olympics (Los Angeles)
- Flag football added as Olympic sport
- Team USA will be selected by USA Football
- Highest-profile international competition
- Selection based on national team tryouts, not draft
- Approximately 12-15 player rosters expected
- Tournament format (short competition window)

### WFA FLAG (Women's Football Alliance - Flag Division)
- National Flag Tour running since 2025
- 16-team national championship at Pro Football Hall of Fame
- Club-based teams, not franchise-based
- $10,000 grand prize for national champion
- Currently the highest-level women's flag competition in the US

### SCWPFFL (SoCal Women's Pro Flag Football League)
- 8 county-based teams in Southern California
- All-Star Tour launching summer 2026
- Full regular season planned for May 2027
- 9-player rosters per team
- Regional league, not national

### International (IFAF)
- International Federation of American Football governs global flag football
- World Championship events
- National team selection through USA Football
- Growing international competition in Europe, Asia, Central America

## 2. Pro KR Translation

The Pro Player KR Legend (File 02) maps college KR to professional viability.

### College-to-Pro KR Projection

Pro projection uses the same component KRs as college evaluation, with reweighted emphasis:

| Component | College Weight (avg) | Pro Weight | Change | Why |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AKR (Athletic) | 25% | 35% | +10% | Pro level demands elite speed/agility. Physical tools are the differentiator. |
| SKR (Skill) | 25% | 25% | Same | Skill level requirements stay consistent. |
| QKR (QB only) | 35% | 30% | -5% | Pro systems are more structured. Less improvisation needed. |
| DKR (Defensive) | 10% | 20% | +10% | Pro-level defense is much more sophisticated. Two-way demands increase. |
| IQKR (IQ) | 30% | 25% | -5% | Pro coaching and play design reduce individual IQ burden slightly. |

### Entry KR vs Peak KR

- **Entry KR:** Day 1 professional reality. What this player is RIGHT NOW if placed on a pro roster.
- **Peak KR:** Development ceiling over 2-3 years of professional coaching, training, and competition.
- Maximum Peak KR = Entry KR + 12 (ceiling for pro-level development)

### Pro Viability Thresholds

| Pro Pathway | Minimum College KR | Ideal College KR | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| NFL/TMRW Pro League | 82+ | 88+ | Elite talent. Top of college game. |
| Team USA / Olympics | 85+ | 90+ | Best in the country. National team caliber. |
| WFA FLAG National | 75+ | 80+ | Strong college player. Competitive at club/semi-pro. |
| SCWPFFL / Regional Pro | 72+ | 78+ | Good college player. Regional professional level. |
| International (non-USA) | 70+ | 78+ | Competitive internationally. |

### Pro Projection Output Format

Every pro projection MUST include:
1. Entry KR (current pro-ready level)
2. Peak KR (projected ceiling with pro development)
3. Component KR reweighting (show the shift from college to pro weights)
4. Pathway recommendation (which pro pathway is the best fit)
5. Development priorities for pro preparation
6. Timeline: when is this player pro-ready? (Now, 1 year, 2 years)
7. Comparison context: what tier of pro player does this map to?

# FLAG-TO-TACKLE CROSSOVER ASSESSMENT

## 0. Purpose
Some women's flag football players have physical tools and skills that could translate to tackle football (should professional women's tackle football expand) or to coaching/training roles in tackle football programs.

This engine assesses which flag football skills transfer and which are flag-specific.

## 1. Skills That Transfer

| Flag Football Skill | Tackle Football Application | Transfer Rating |
|---|---|---|
| Route running | WR route running (identical) | 95% |
| Catching / Hands | WR/TE receiving (identical) | 95% |
| QB accuracy | QB accuracy (identical) | 90% |
| QB decision-making | QB decision-making (similar, more complex in tackle) | 80% |
| Coverage ability | DB coverage (similar, no tackling required) | 85% |
| Speed / Agility | Universal athletic trait | 95% |
| Pre-snap reads | Football IQ (similar, more complex in tackle) | 75% |
| Ball skills / INTs | DB ball skills (identical) | 95% |

## 2. Skills That Do NOT Transfer

| Flag Football Context | Tackle Football Reality | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| No blocking | OL/DL blocking is an entire skill set | No transfer |
| No tackling | Tackling is a fundamental defensive skill | No transfer |
| Flag pulling | Replaced by tackling | No transfer |
| 7v7 field awareness | 11v11 field awareness is more complex | Partial transfer |
| No kicking game | Kicking/punting is an entire specialty | No transfer |
| Screen blocking only | Full contact blocking technique required | Minimal transfer |

## 3. Crossover Archetypes

**High-Transfer Archetypes:**
- Speed Burner WR: Speed and route running transfer directly to tackle WR
- Dual-Threat QB: Accuracy and scrambling transfer. Must learn pocket presence and pass protection reads.
- Ball Hawk Safety: Coverage skills and ball skills transfer directly to tackle safety/CB
- Complete Receiver: Full skill set transfers to tackle WR

**Low-Transfer Archetypes:**
- Receiving Center: No blocking ability means no position in tackle football beyond WR
- QB Spy Defender: Flag pulling is irrelevant. Must learn tackling from scratch.
- Screen Blocking Specialist: No contact blocking ability

## 4. Coaching / Training Crossover

Flag football knowledge transfers to tackle football coaching in several areas:
- Route design and teaching
- Coverage concepts (man, zone, pattern matching)
- QB development (accuracy, decision-making, reads)
- Offensive play design (passing concepts, formations)
- Speed/agility training methodology

Many flag football coaches and players may find careers in tackle football programs as passing game coordinators, WR coaches, DB coaches, or skill development trainers.

# COACHING IMPACT MODIFIER

## 0. Purpose
Measures how much a coaching staff accelerates or decelerates player development.

## 1. Inputs
- Coach tenure at current program (years)
- Player KR progression under this coach (year-over-year improvement)
- Team KR progression under this coach
- Recruiting quality (incoming player KR average)
- Player placement after program (transfer destinations, pro pathways)
- System stability (same system year-over-year, or constant change)

## 2. Coaching Impact Score (CIS)

CIS = weighted combination of:
- Player Development Rate: Average KR improvement per player per year (40% weight)
- System Execution: How close team performs to expected Team KR (25% weight)
- Recruiting Quality: Average incoming recruit KR relative to level (20% weight)
- Retention: % of players who stay through eligibility (15% weight)

### CIS Bands

| CIS | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 90-100 | Elite Developer | Players dramatically outperform expectations. KR gains of 8+ per year. Program produces pro-pathway players. |
| 80-89 | Strong Developer | Players consistently improve. KR gains of 5-7 per year. Competitive program. |
| 70-79 | Average Developer | Normal improvement curve. KR gains of 3-4 per year. |
| 60-69 | Below Average | Limited improvement. Players plateau early. KR gains of 1-2 per year. |
| Below 60 | Detrimental | Players stagnate or regress. Negative development environment. |

## 3. Flag Football Coaching Context

Flag football coaching is in its infancy. Most coaches are:
- New to flag football (coming from tackle football coaching backgrounds)
- Learning the sport alongside their players
- Building programs from scratch (first-year programs)
- Operating with minimal staff (HC only or HC + 1 assistant)
- Working with limited budgets and facilities

This means:
- CIS data is extremely limited. Most coaches have 1-3 years of flag football coaching data.
- Tackle football coaching experience is a positive signal but not a direct indicator of flag football coaching ability.
- Players developing faster than expected may reflect the coach OR may reflect natural athletic progression from multi-sport athletes entering a new sport.
- CIS should be interpreted with low confidence (30-50%) until a coach has 3+ years of flag football data.

## 4. New-Program Coaching Adjustment

First-year programs get a +10 CIS buffer for Year 1. Building something from nothing is inherently harder than maintaining an established program. A first-year CIS of 70 is actually impressive (equivalent to 80 at an established program).
