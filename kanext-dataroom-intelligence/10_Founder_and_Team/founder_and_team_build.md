One founder. Operating as an assistant basketball coach at Lincoln University in Oakland, California, one of the most resource-constrained programs in college athletics. No salary. No scholarships. The architecture was not designed in a lab. It was designed by an operator who needed it. Every engine exists because a real institutional problem demanded it. The simulation engine exists because game preparation at resource-constrained programs is based on intuition. The settlement layer exists because institutional finances are fragmented across disconnected tools with no governance. Every layer of KaNeXT was built to solve a problem the founder experienced firsthand, inside institutions that had no operating system.

## Current State

KaNeXT today is founder-led. The architecture, product specification, intelligence system, and iOS application were created by the founder operating alone. The core intelligence system is built and already operating in live basketball workflows: player evaluation, team intelligence, recruiting search, and Dipson-powered conversational access to the full intelligence corpus. What exists today is the core IP, the working system logic, and a proven decision architecture. What does not yet exist is a production-scale engineering organization or an institutional operations team.

The Year 1 $33M capital raise supports the Partner Institution Athletics-Only Agreement deployment, platform operations, and initial team buildout across KaNeXT and institutional operations.
