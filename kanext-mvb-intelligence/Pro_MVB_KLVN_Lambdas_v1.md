## SCOPE

Pro lambdas normalize production across professional men's volleyball leagues worldwide. The reference league (lambda = 1.000) is the Italian SuperLega, widely regarded as the strongest men's club volleyball league in the world.

## MEN'S PRO KLVN NOTES

### Why Italian SuperLega is Reference (Not Polish or Brazilian)
Italy has been the most consistent top-level men's league for decades. While Poland and Brazil are close competitors:
- Italy attracts the most diverse international talent
- The SuperLega has the deepest competitive balance (8-10 genuine title contenders)
- Champions League results consistently favor Italian clubs
- Historical depth is unmatched

### Russian League Note
The Russian Superliga (Zenit Kazan, Belogorie, Dynamo Moscow) was historically among the top 2-3 leagues in the world. Due to geopolitical situations since 2022, Russian clubs are excluded from CEV competitions and the league's international standing is in flux. Lambda reflects historical strength but may need adjustment based on current accessibility and competition quality.

### League Accessibility for American Players
American players face different barriers by league:
- **Italy/Poland/Turkey:** Most competitive import markets. 2-3 import spots per team. KR 88+ typically required.
- **France/Germany:** More accessible entry point. 3-4 import spots in some leagues. KR 82+ viable.
- **Japan/Korea:** Limited import spots (2-3). Cultural adjustment significant. KR 84+ for Japan, 82+ for Korea.
- **Brazil/Argentina:** Portuguese/Spanish language. Very limited import spots (Brazilian league favors domestic talent). KR 85+ for Brazil.
