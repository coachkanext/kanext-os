## NAIA-SPECIFIC CALIBRATION NOTES

1. **NAIA is the data anchor for all flag football evaluation.** With 4+ years of competitive data (since 2021), NAIA provides the deepest and most reliable production baselines.

2. **Top-vs-standard program gap is significant.** Ottawa, Keiser, and St. Thomas have been dominant since inception. Their rosters feature recruited athletes with flag football experience. Newer programs are often competing with first-year athletes learning the sport. The lambda split (0.920 top / 0.870 standard) captures this gap.

3. **Multi-sport athlete distortion.** Many NAIA flag football players are track stars, soccer players, or basketball players who picked up flag football. Their athletic testing (AKR) may be elite while their flag-specific skills (SKR) lag behind. Production stats may understate athletic potential or overstate football competency.

4. **Two-way player prevalence.** At NAIA roster sizes (typically 15-25 players), most starters play both offense and defense. Per-snap production is a better indicator than total volume because snap counts are shared across both sides.

5. **Seasonal timing.** NAIA flag football is a spring sport. This means overlap with track season, softball, and other spring sports. Multi-sport athletes may have divided attention or limited availability.

6. **Scholarship context.** The $15,000 team stipend means individual athletic aid is small (often $500-1,500 per player). Most financial aid comes from academic scholarships and need-based grants. Recruiting leverage is limited compared to fully-funded sports.
