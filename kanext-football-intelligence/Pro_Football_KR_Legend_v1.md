# PRO FOOTBALL PLAYER KR LEGEND -- v1

## Key Concept: Entry vs Median vs Peak

- **Entry KR** = Day 1 as a rookie. Almost every rookie is 80-87 regardless of draft position. Entry KR is nearly useless for top-10 evaluation.
- **Median Outcome** = Most likely version by Year 3. Midpoint of best and worst scenarios. What late-first teams draft for.
- **Peak Ceiling** = Best realistic outcome by Year 3-5. What top-10 teams draft for.

Entry KR does NOT correlate with draft order. A safe late-first pick can have a HIGHER entry than the #1 overall pick. The #1 pick is drafted for ceiling, not Day 1. Two players with identical entry KR can have completely different draft values because their ceilings differ.

## Draft-Range Output Priority

| Draft Range | Primary KR | What Team Is Buying |
|------------|-----------|-------------------|
| #1-5 | Peak Ceiling | Best player available. Franchise QB or generational talent. Fit/overlap less important — team is bad, roster will change around this pick. |
| #6-15 | 3-Year Projection | Complementary star next to existing talent. Fit starts to matter. |
| #16-32 | Median Outcome | Contributors NOW. High floor, narrow range, role-ready. System fit critical. |
| 2nd-3rd round | Entry KR + Role | Earn a roster spot. What can you do Day 1? Specialists and depth. |
| 4th-7th round | Entry KR + Upside | Lottery tickets. One elite trait that could develop. |
| UDFA | Entry KR only | Make the 53. Special teams first. |

## NFL PLAYER KR TIERS

### 98-100 -- All-Time Great / MVP Caliber
**Role:** Hall of Fame trajectory. Perennial All-Pro. MVP candidate every year. Top-5 player in the league. Franchise-defining force.
**NFL Cap %:** 18-23% ($50M-$65M+ AAV)
**Draft context:** PEAK tier only. Almost no rookies enter here. Appears only in Peak Ceiling projections for generational QBs or transcendent defenders. If a prospect's peak projects here, they go #1 regardless of entry KR.
- QB: 4,500+ pass yds, 100+ passer rating, 35+ TD, MVP votes. Patrick Mahomes, Josh Allen tier.
- Non-QB: All-Pro. DPOY candidate. Best at position by wide margin. Myles Garrett, TJ Watt tier.

### 95-97 -- Perennial All-Pro / Elite Starter
**Role:** Consistent All-Pro. Top-10 at position. Game-plan centerpiece. Drives playoff runs. First-round value.
**NFL Cap %:** 12-20% ($35M-$55M AAV)
**Draft context:** PEAK tier for top-5 picks who fully develop. The ceiling #1-3 picks are drafted for.
- QB: 4,000+ pass yds, 95+ passer rating, 30+ TD. Franchise QB. Top-5 at position.
- Non-QB: All-Pro caliber. Top-5 at position. Drives unit performance.

### 92-94 -- High-Quality Starter / Pro Bowl Caliber
**Role:** Pro Bowl level. Consistent positive starter. Drives unit performance. Top-15 at position.
**NFL Cap %:** 7-14% ($20M-$40M AAV)
**Draft context:** PEAK for picks #3-10. 3-YEAR for top picks developing well. A median outcome here makes a player worth a top-5 pick.
- QB: 3,500+ pass yds, 90+ passer rating, 25+ TD. Franchise starter. Wins playoff games.
- Non-QB: Pro Bowl level. Consistent high-level starter. Anchor of a unit.

### 89-91 -- Solid NFL Starter
**Role:** Reliable starter. Not elite but clearly starting-caliber. Fills a role effectively. Wins his matchups more often than not.
**NFL Cap %:** 4-8% ($10M-$22M AAV)
**Draft context:** ENTRY tier for most first-round picks (this is what rookies actually are Day 1). MEDIAN for mid-first. PEAK for late first.
- QB: 3,000+ pass yds, 85+ passer rating, 20+ TD. Capable starter. Can win with a good roster around him.
- Non-QB: Starting-caliber. Does his job. Not a liability. Above-average at position.

### 86-88 -- Low-End Starter / High-End Backup
**Role:** Fringe starter. Capable of starting if needed but ideally a high-end backup or rotational piece. Bridge starter in a rebuild.
**NFL Cap %:** 2-5% ($5M-$14M AAV)
**Draft context:** ENTRY for late-first and early second-round. FLOOR for mid-first picks who stall.
- QB: 2,500+ pass yds, 80+ passer rating. Can start but limits the offense. Bridge QB.
- Non-QB: Fringe starter. Starts on a weak unit but is ideally a backup or package player on a good team.

### 83-85 -- Quality Backup / Rotational Piece
**Role:** Reliable backup. Plays in packages. Not a liability when on the field but not driving outcomes.
**NFL Cap %:** 1-3% ($2M-$7M AAV)
**Draft context:** ENTRY for second-round. MEDIAN for late-first who become career backups.
- Across positions: Solid backup. Steps in without disaster. Package specialist. 25-45% snap share.

### 80-82 -- Roster Depth / Special Teams Core
**Role:** Makes a 53-man roster on special teams value or depth. Limited defensive/offensive role.
**NFL Cap %:** 0.5-1.5% ($1M-$3.5M AAV, often veteran minimum)
**Draft context:** ENTRY for 3rd-4th round. FLOOR for 2nd-round picks who don't develop.
- Across positions: Makes the roster. Special teams ace. Developmental with one NFL-caliber trait.

### 77-79 -- Practice Squad / Fringe 53
**Role:** Bubble player. Practice squad candidate. Occasional game-day elevation.
**NFL Cap %:** Practice squad salary ($12,500-$20,000/week)
**Draft context:** ENTRY for 5th-7th round. FLOOR for 3rd-4th round who don't develop.

### 74-76 -- Camp Body
**Role:** Unlikely to make a 53-man roster. Training camp depth. May catch on with a practice squad.

### Below 74 -- Below NFL Viability
Not sustainable at NFL level. CFL/UFL candidate.

## CFL PLAYER KR TIERS

Reference: CFL salary cap approximately $6.75M CAD per team (2025).

### 92-100 -- CFL All-Star / MOP Caliber
**Role:** Most Outstanding Player candidate. Dominant in the CFL. Grey Cup MVP contender. Could compete for an NFL roster spot.
**CFL Salary:** $300K-$750K+ CAD
- QB: 5,000+ pass yds (CFL), 30+ TD, MOP caliber.
- Non-QB: CFL All-Star. Dominant at position.

### 86-91 -- CFL Starter / Impact Player
**Role:** Reliable CFL starter. Positive contributor. Anchors a unit. Could earn an NFL tryout.
**CFL Salary:** $100K-$300K CAD

### 80-85 -- CFL Rotation / Backup
**Role:** Depth in the CFL. Backup or package player. Special teams contributor.
**CFL Salary:** $65K-$120K CAD

### Below 80 -- Below CFL Viability
