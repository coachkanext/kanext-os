# KaNeXT Revenue Model: Institutional

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

## Overview

This document presents the institutional revenue projection for KaNeXT LLC through the SFBC partnership across a five-year horizon. Year 1 is the Athletics-Only Agreement under which KaNeXT operates athletic programs autonomously and the partner institution retains academic authority. Year 2 forward is the expansion to a Full Institutional Operating Agreement under which KaNeXT operates institutional infrastructure, enrollment, platform deployment, and, beginning with the Miami Lakes campus acquisition, physical campus operations.

The model shows both sides of the partnership economics. KaNeXT captures operator economics from athletic revenue, tuition share, platform revenue, and campus operations revenue. The partner institution captures direct partnership payments, retained non-athletic revenue, enrollment growth, and institutional transformation. Both parties grow the pie together. The partnership only succeeds if both sides win.

The model scopes institutional revenue through the SFBC partnership. Platform revenue at scale across mandate institutions, intelligence licensing, bank revenue, and other KaNeXT revenue streams are documented in separate revenue model files and are not quantified here. This document is the institutional revenue story.

## Comparable Institutions

Three Christian universities demonstrate three positioning strategies that inform the SFBC trajectory. Each achieved scale through a different mix of athletic visibility, campus investment, tuition positioning, and online expansion. None had the combination of advantages SFBC has.

### High Point University

Premium positioning. United Methodist-affiliated institution describing itself as Christian by tradition and interfaith in vision. High Point grew from approximately 1,450 students in 2005 to 6,335 on-campus today. Tuition rose from roughly $16,000 to $45,312 for 2024-25, moving to $46,444 for 2025-26, with total cost of attendance reaching $67,986 and rising to $71,429 for Tier 1 housing in 2026-27. Over $3B in campus investment over 20 years. 88% of students receive institutional aid averaging $21,416. Membership in Big South Conference at Division 1 level. The model is prestige pricing backed by campus experience and athletic visibility.

### Liberty University

Scale positioning. Evangelical Christian institution founded by Jerry Falwell, theologically conservative. Liberty reached approximately 16,000 residential and over 124,000 online students. Tuition at $24,650 for 2025-26 with room and board of $14,604. Total residential cost of attendance around $40,924. Annual revenue exceeded $1.2B. The model is mass-scale residential and dominant online market position at middle-tier tuition. Liberty is the proof that evangelical Christian higher education can scale past 100,000 students through disciplined online operations.

### Grand Canyon University

Affordability and online scale positioning. Non-denominational Christian institution, originally Southern Baptist, operating as a nonprofit since 2018. GCU froze on-campus tuition at $16,500 for 18 consecutive years while growing from under 1,000 on-campus students in 2008 to approximately 25,000 on-campus plus approximately 98,000 online. Room and board at $10,658. Over $1.8B in campus infrastructure investment. The model is disciplined affordability, aggressive marketing, and massive online scale.

SFBC is launching an MBA in Fall 2025 and a BSN in development. Academic program growth is already underway institutionally before the KaNeXT partnership begins.

SFBC has a 40-year Christian institutional brand in Broward County, inside the Miami-Fort Lauderdale-West Palm Beach DMA, one of the largest metropolitan markets in the country.

KaNeXT adds what the partner institution did not have on its own: a live institutional operating system, governed intelligence across admissions and retention, nationally-visible athletic programming, NIL infrastructure, platform financial operations, a path to Division 1 athletic membership, and capital to fund the transformation.

SFBC's evangelical Christian identity occupies a distinct position from any of the comparables. HPU operates from a Methodist-interfaith tradition. Liberty is evangelical but at mass scale and middle-tier tuition. GCU is non-denominational at affordability pricing. The combination of evangelical Christian identity, premium-tier positioning trajectory, and AI-native intelligence platform is a category that does not currently exist in the market. SFBC occupies uncontested ground.

The partner institution does not need to build what HPU, Liberty, or GCU had to build. SFBC needs to execute the partnership, let the athletic engine drive visibility, let the enrollment engine convert visibility to enrollment, let the tuition trajectory follow the prestige build, and let the Miami Lakes campus anchor the growth. The comparables took 20 years. The partner institution reaches meaningful scale faster because the foundation is already there.

## Assumptions Grounding The Model

The model rests on five operational assumptions. Each is grounded in observable conditions at the partner institution and in KaNeXT's current capabilities.

**Athletic visibility is immediate, scheduled, and national.** Year 1 men's basketball schedules 12 to 16 Division I guarantee games, targeting essentially the entire non-conference slate. Every game broadcast on ESPN+ through host D1 school media agreements. The schedule alone puts SFBC on national television a dozen or more times during basketball season at an institution with eleven hundred students.

KaNeXT plans to build a mid-major quality team that regularly beats NAIA opponents by 30 to 40 points. The intelligence system selects D1 opponents where SFBC has measurable personnel and system advantages, scheduling D1 games KaNeXT is competitive in or favored to win. The objective is not to play D1 schedules as sacrifices. The objective is to use the intelligence architecture to identify favorable matchups and schedule accordingly, converting the guarantee game slot into a real competitive opportunity.

One or two D1 wins generates national media attention. NAIA programs have beaten D1 teams before, and when it happens, the story gets real coverage. An NAIA Christian bible college beating D1 programs using an AI intelligence system is the specific story arc that drives coverage to SportsCenter, national sports media, and beyond the sports category into general interest media. The founder's coaching track record at Lincoln University Oakland, where a roster built with no scholarships and no infrastructure won back-to-back conference regular season titles and the GAAC tournament championship while scheduling six D1 guarantee games, demonstrates the program-building capacity at maximum constraint. SFBC removes the constraints.

**The enrollment engine operates from Day 1.** KaNeXT's enrollment intelligence architecture, covering retention, persistence, pipeline, and recovery, deploys in parallel with the admissions intelligence and NIL intelligence already live. Enrollment operates against the full lifecycle from admissions inquiry through graduation, not just front-end recruiting.

**The online program infrastructure scales what exists.** SFBC's Populi platform and online operations are scaled rather than rebuilt. KaNeXT adds Dipson AI advising, national marketing capacity, and enrollment engine optimization to infrastructure SFBC already runs. This is acceleration, not construction.

**The Miami Lakes campus anchors Year 3 onward.** Miami Lakes campus acquisition begins Year 2. Initial operations begin Year 3. Majority of operations move Year 4. Full operations Year 5. The campus is the physical manifestation of institutional transformation and the economic center of institutional revenue from Year 4 forward.

**The tuition trajectory follows the HPU pattern.** Athletic visibility plus campus investment plus brand build supports rising tuition. SFBC does not lead with tuition increases. Tuition rises as the institutional product rises. Scholarship aid scales proportionally so net tuition remains accessible even as sticker tuition rises.

**The Year 1 athletic scholarship structure contributes to direct tuition revenue.** Because NAIA Varsity scholarship equivalencies do not activate until Year 2 NAIA membership, Year 1 deploys strategic scholarships only where recruiting requires. Most Year 1 athletes receive Title IV federal financial aid plus standard institutional aid and pay tuition as enrolled students. Twelve teams at approximately 15-18 athletes per team produces approximately 180-220 athletes in Year 1 as direct tuition-paying enrollment. Year 2 onward the Varsity teams activate NAIA scholarship equivalencies while Development teams continue as tuition-paying rosters. The athletic enrollment contribution compounds over Year 1 through Year 5 as the sport portfolio expands.

## Campus Trajectory And Revenue Ownership

Where students live determines which party captures housing, dining, and campus commerce revenue.

**Year 1 through Year 3: Deerfield Primary, Miami Lakes Build**

The partner institution operates from its owned 5.4-acre Deerfield campus. Academic operations, administrative functions, and most student housing run through existing partner institution infrastructure. KaNeXT operates athletic-specific cluster housing through master lease. The partner institution retains all non-athletic housing, dining, and campus commerce revenue.

Miami Lakes campus acquisition proceeds through Year 2 with Graham Companies. Phase 1 buildout during Year 2 and Year 3 includes athletic facilities, initial residential housing, initial academic buildings, and initial student life infrastructure. First students begin relocating to Miami Lakes in Year 3 as facilities come online.

**Year 4: Miami Lakes Majority, Deerfield Retained**

Majority of partner institution students operate from Miami Lakes. KaNeXT owns the Miami Lakes campus and therefore captures housing, dining, facility fees, parking, campus retail, and event revenue from all students at Miami Lakes. Deerfield operates as a satellite campus honoring the founding location, with the partner institution retaining Deerfield-scoped revenue there.

**Year 5: Miami Lakes Full Operation, Christian HPU State**

Miami Lakes is the primary campus operating at Christian HPU scale. KaNeXT operates the campus and captures the full campus economics. Miami Lakes hosts athletic programs at a level approaching or achieving Division 1 membership, with associated licensing, media rights, and prestige revenue.

**The campus ownership question is the single largest revenue lever.** When KaNeXT owns the campus, KaNeXT captures the housing and dining revenue at the scale HPU and Liberty demonstrate. HPU's 6,335 residential students generate approximately $134M annually in room and board alone. Liberty's 16,000 residential students generate approximately $233M annually in room and board alone. SFBC at 12,800 to 14,200 residential students on Miami Lakes generates room and board revenue in the $190M to $285M range annually by Year 5.

### The $1/Year Lease Structure

When KaNeXT acquires the Miami Lakes campus, KaNeXT enters into a long-term ground lease with the partner institution at $1 per year. The partner institution operates academically on a KaNeXT-owned campus without paying market rent, preserving SFBC's academic authority and institutional continuity while transferring the physical asset burden to KaNeXT.

Under this structure, KaNeXT captures housing, dining, facility fees, parking, campus commerce, and event revenue as campus operator. The partner institution captures academic tuition per the Full IOA split, preserves its Deerfield founding campus, and operates from a flagship facility at no real estate cost.

The $1/year lease is the mechanism that makes the partnership work cleanly. SFBC gets a real flagship campus without the capital cost. KaNeXT captures the operational economics that justify the infrastructure investment. Both parties align economically on the same flywheel: KaNeXT wins when SFBC wins, because KaNeXT's campus revenue depends entirely on the partner institution's enrollment success.

The structure mirrors the GCU-GCE precedent, with terms structured substantially more favorably to the partner institution than the GCU historical arrangement.

## Investment In Institutional Quality

The tuition trajectory from the partner institution's current $7,740 to the Year 5 target range is not a function of sticker pricing alone. It is a function of institutional quality that supports the premium. KaNeXT's capital deployment includes substantial ongoing investment in the academic institution itself, not only in athletic programs and campus infrastructure. Students and families pay premium Christian university tuition because they experience premium Christian university quality.

**Faculty investment.** Christian higher education historically pays faculty below market rates, which caps academic quality and program reputation. KaNeXT invests in faculty compensation above the Christian university market to attract and retain top academic talent. This includes named faculty chairs, research capacity funding, program director positions, and competitive salary ranges that exceed peer institutions. Breaking the Christian university faculty compensation ceiling is how the partner institution builds the academic product that earns the tuition premium.

**Academic program development.** New degree programs, expanded existing programs, online program scaling, specialized certifications, and interdisciplinary programs aligned with market demand. The MBA launched Fall 2025 and the BSN in development are the beginning, not the end, of academic program growth. KaNeXT supports the development and launch of programs that expand the partner institution's academic footprint within ABHE's accreditation scope and, over time, potentially through additional accreditation pathways.

**Student services excellence.** Dipson-powered academic advising, tutoring, career services, mental health and wellness, student life programming, and the high-touch student experience that HPU and similar premium institutions deliver. Dipson extends student support infrastructure without the per-student staffing cost that limits smaller institutions.

**Academic infrastructure.** Libraries, laboratories, research centers, technology, classrooms, academic buildings. The physical and digital academic environment that matches the tuition tier. Initial investment at Deerfield, substantially expanded at Miami Lakes as the campus comes online.

**Accreditation expansion pathway.** Supporting the partner institution's ABHE accreditation continuity and the 2028 reaffirmation cycle. Over time, supporting potential expansion of accreditation scope or pursuit of additional accreditations that support program growth beyond ABHE's current undergraduate and master's scope.

This investment is what earns the tuition premium. Athletic visibility draws attention to the institution. Academic quality retains and compounds enrollment once students experience the education firsthand. HPU spent $3B+ over 20 years building the academic product alongside the campus. The partner institution does not need 20 years because the platform, the intelligence, and the operator capacity accelerate the build.

A meaningful portion of the Core Operations budget in Year 1, and expanded allocations in Year 2 forward under Full IOA, is directed to institutional quality investment. ## Year 1 Revenue Model: Athletics-Only Agreement

### KaNeXT Revenue (Year 1)

**Athletic revenue (100% KaNeXT).** Game tickets, merchandise through fulfillment, sponsorships, D1 guarantee game fees, NIL marketplace processing fees, athletic broadcast revenue. Year 1 projection: $500,000 to $1.5M.

**Housing operation.** Athletes pay room and board through financial aid, KPay, or family payment. Master-leased cluster housing with team operations. Year 1 housing operates approximately at breakeven to slight loss, stabilizing in Year 2.

**KaNeXT total operational revenue Year 1: approximately $1M to $2.5M.** This is modest against the $30M deployment because Year 1 is the launch investment, not the return year. The athletic engine is building visibility. Enrollment is compounding. The platform is generating data. Year 1 revenue is a launch data point, not the model return.

### Partner Institution Revenue (Year 1)

**Direct payments from KaNeXT.** Operational Partnership Fee of $500,000. Philanthropic Contribution of $500,000 to $1,000,000. Combined direct flow: $1,000,000 to $1,500,000.

At partner institution current tuition of $7,740 per student, new non-athlete tuition revenue ranges from approximately $2.3M to $4.6M annually by end of Year 1.

**All existing partner institution revenue.** Academic tuition for existing non-athlete students, grant revenue, existing contributions, all baseline institutional revenue. No change to existing streams.

**Partner institution net new revenue Year 1 from the partnership: approximately $3.6M to $6.6M.** This is on top of the partner institution's current institutional baseline. The partnership in Year 1 alone creates new revenue approaching or exceeding SFBC's entire current institutional revenue base, without SFBC investing a dollar.

## Division 1 Forward Trajectory

Partner institution athletic programs operate at the NAIA Varsity level through Year 4. Division 1 transition planning activates in Year 4 once the Miami Lakes campus provides the infrastructure footprint D1 institutions require.

D1 membership requires NCAA application, compliance buildout, scholarship scale at NCAA minimums, facility requirements, and a multi-year transition period. Institutional resource commitments at D1 exceed NAIA by a substantial margin. KaNeXT's athletic infrastructure buildout at the partner institution is designed to scale into D1 requirements, not require rebuilding.

The strategic case for D1: institutional prestige rises materially at D1 status. Tuition pricing power rises. Enrollment inquiry rises. Donor capacity rises. NCAA media rights and tournament revenue become available. D1 is the athletic state that matches the Christian HPU institutional state. The partner institution at Miami Lakes with D1 athletic programs is operating in the same competitive category as Baylor, TCU, BYU, and other faith-based Division 1 institutions.

D1 transition is forward optionality. Revenue impact is noted as Tier 3 upside and not quantified in the five-year base case.
