# Mandate Economics Deep Dive

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

## Purpose

The Governing Body Mandate Strategy doc explains the distribution model. The Go-to-Market doc explains how the mandate compounds. This document explains the unit economics: what it costs to deploy each mandate institution, what each school generates in revenue, and how the conversion from viewer to wallet holder to transactor works at a per-school level.

---

## Deployment Cost

### Hardware

Two free Cam Sport units per school. The mandate covers every sport the school offers, not a single sport. A school with basketball, volleyball, soccer, baseball, and track uses the same cameras across all sports.

Cost per unit: approximately $130 COGS. Two units per school: approximately $260 per school. Total hardware for 1,000+ member institutions and select HS programs: approximately 2,000+ units at ~$130 = $260,000-$300,000. With packaging, shipping, and logistics: under $350,000 total.

This is one of the lowest-cost elements of the entire deployment. Under $350K to create the physical capture network across 1,000+ member institutions and select HS programs.

### Platform and Operations

Per-school platform deployment cost is near-zero at the marginal level. The KaNeXT OS, KTV, KPay, Dipson, and intelligence infrastructure are built once and serve every school. Adding a mandate institution to the platform is a provisioning event (creating the school's brand, activating the storefront, enabling the camera feed), not a new infrastructure build.

Estimated per-school platform onboarding cost: $500-$1,000 (provisioning, initial configuration, brief training on camera setup and upload workflow).

### Support

Ongoing support per mandate institution is lightweight. Camera troubleshooting, upload assistance, and basic platform questions are handled through Dipson (AI-assisted) with escalation to a small support team. Estimated support burden: 1 support staff member per 200-300 schools. Across the 1,000+ institution footprint: 3-5 support staff at approximately $40,000-$50,000 each = $120,000-$250,000 annually.

### Total Deployment Cost

| Item | Cost |
|---|---|
| Camera hardware (2,000+ units) | ~$290K |
| Packaging, shipping, logistics | ~$60K |
| Platform onboarding (1,000+ member institutions and select HS programs) | ~$500K-$950K |
| Annual support staff (3-5 people) | ~$120K-$250K/year |

The mandate strategy doc estimates total deployment cost at $2.6-4.5M including marketing, event presence (NAIA convention), and governing body relationship management. The hardware and platform costs above represent the operational core of that budget.

## What Each Mandate School Receives (Free)

Every mandate institution receives the full platform at no cost. This is not a limited trial or a feature-gated free tier. This is the operating infrastructure.

**Two cameras.** Professional-grade Cam Sport units covering one indoor and one outdoor venue. All sports use the same cameras. The only requirement: games must be uploaded to the platform within 48 hours.

**A front-facing brand page.** The school gets its own branded presence on the platform: roster, schedule, stats, scores, game archives, program information. This replaces whatever basic website or social media presence the school currently uses for athletics. Parents, fans, recruits, and media can find the school, see the schedule, watch archived games, and follow results. This is NOT the intelligence layer. It is the public-facing school page with standard stats and game information.

**A merch store.** Through the fulfillment center, every school gets a managed merchandise storefront at zero cost. School-branded apparel, spirit wear, and accessories produced on demand. The school earns revenue on every sale. KaNeXT handles production, shipping, and customer service.

**KPay for everything.** Tickets, concessions, donations, fundraising, event fees, booster giving, team dues, equipment orders - all processed through KPay. The school pays 5% processing (compared to 1.5-3.5% legacy rates across multiple intermediaries, but with the addition of a complete storefront, ticketing, and donation infrastructure the school never had before). Schools keep 95% of revenue that in many cases did not exist in any organized form before KaNeXT arrived.

**KTV streaming.** Every game uploaded is available on KTV. Parents watch from anywhere. Recruits see film. The school has a broadcast presence it never had.

**Live broadcasting with school-set pricing.** Schools can charge for live game access and set their own prices. A school can charge $5-$15 per game for live streaming, with KaNeXT processing the transaction through KPay. This is new revenue that most sub-D1 schools have never been able to generate because they lacked the streaming infrastructure.

**Stats and game management.** StatKeeper provides game-day stat tracking with advanced statistics. Scores, box scores, season statistics, and advanced metrics populate automatically. Schools get a level of statistical depth that most sub-D1 programs have never had access to.

### What Schools Do NOT Get for Free

**Intelligence.** The full evaluation system (KR ratings, system fit, scouting reports, simulation, development projections, recruiting intelligence) is NOT included in the free mandate package. During the partner institution's Year 1-3 validation phase, the deep intelligence is not commercialized at scale - it remains exclusive to the partner institution as the competitive moat. Mandate schools see that intelligence exists through the platform. They see KR ratings referenced. They see intelligence-backed features previewed. Commercials during KTV game streams show how the intelligence works across every sport. Small-scale ancillary intelligence products (player valuation lookups, recruiting match previews, basic scouting briefs) may be available during Year 1-2 at commodity analytics-tool tier pricing. Full commercial intelligence licensing opens in Year 3+ at institutional-IP tier pricing after the partner institution demonstrates publicly attributable competitive outcomes.

This is the upsell path. The mandate gives schools everything they need to operate and generate revenue for free. Intelligence is what they want once they see what it can do. And they see it constantly because it is embedded in the platform they use every day and advertised during the games they watch every week.

## What KaNeXT Monetizes

### 1. Commerce Processing (5%)

Every transaction through KPay: tickets, merch, concessions, donations, fundraising, event fees, live broadcast purchases, team dues. 5% flat. This is the foundation of mandate economics.

Estimated annual commerce volume per mandate institution varies significantly by size, sport count, and fan engagement. A conservative estimate:

| School Tier | Annual Commerce Volume | KaNeXT 5% Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| Small (1-3 sports, low attendance) | $50,000-$100,000 | $2,500-$5,000 |
| Medium (4-8 sports, moderate attendance) | $100,000-$300,000 | $5,000-$15,000 |
| Large (8+ sports, strong attendance) | $300,000-$750,000 | $15,000-$37,500 |
| **Blended average (1,000+ member institutions and select HS programs)** | **~$150,000** | **~$7,500** |

Across the 1,000+ institution footprint with a blended average of $150K commerce per school: total mandate commerce volume = approximately $150M annually. KaNeXT 5% = approximately $7.5M annually.

This estimate is conservative. Commerce volume per school grows as the platform becomes more integrated into daily institutional operations and as wallet adoption increases (wallet transactions have higher margin because card network fees are eliminated).

### 2. Advertising and Commercials on KTV

Every game streamed on KTV is an ad-supported media surface that KaNeXT owns. Across the 1,000+ institution footprint generating tens of thousands of games per year, the total ad inventory is massive. Pre-roll ads, mid-game commercial breaks, sponsored overlays, and partner advertising all generate revenue at the network level.

Critically, KaNeXT uses this ad inventory to promote its own products. Commercials during mandate institution games show the intelligence system in action - how KR ratings work, how recruiting intelligence identifies players, how scouting reports are generated, how the evaluation system operates across every sport. Every game broadcast is both a revenue-generating ad surface and a demonstration of the premium intelligence products that schools, coaches, and families can purchase.

### 3. Intelligence (Held In-House Year 1-3, Licensed Year 3+)

Intelligence is the highest-margin revenue stream across the platform, but it is NOT sold broadly during the partner institution's Year 1-3 validation phase. Mandate institutions receive the full platform for free: KaNeXT OS, KTV broadcast, KPay payments, KVision cameras, and KStat data capture. What they do NOT receive is the deep evaluation intelligence (KR scoring, trait libraries, system fit computation, simulation engine, scouting intelligence, development engines). The deep intelligence stays in the partner institution's hands as the competitive moat.

The reason: commercializing the intelligence before it is validated dilutes the moat before the moat is proven. The intelligence IP appreciates while it is held, not while it is sold. By keeping the intelligence in-house during Year 1-3, the partner institution's competitive outcomes - NAIA championships, D1 guarantee game upsets, visible pro placements, enrollment growth attributed to the platform - become the market proof that establishes pricing power later.

Ancillary intelligence services may be offered at small scale during Year 1-2 without compromising the core moat: player valuation lookups (NIL-adjacent), recruiting match previews (rival-style data products), and basic scouting intelligence. These are tier-1 analytics products comparable to existing vendors (NCSA, 3 Star Athletics, Rivals-style platforms). They generate modest recurring revenue without giving away the core evaluation engine.

Full commercial intelligence licensing begins in Year 3+ once the partner institution has publicly demonstrated the architecture works at scale. At that point, the intelligence licenses at institutional-IP tier across seven commercial markets (coaching, pro front office, agent, media, institutional operator, corporate enterprise, data licensing) at pricing that reflects a validated institutional asset rather than an unproven analytics tool.

### 4. Live Broadcast Revenue

Schools set their own prices for live game access. A school charges fans $5-$15 per game for live streaming through KTV. KaNeXT processes the transaction through KPay (5% processing). The school keeps 95%. This is new revenue that most sub-D1 schools have never been able to generate.

### Intelligence Data

Every game filmed by KVision produces structured data that feeds the intelligence system. Across the 1,000+ institution footprint generating 20,000+ games per year of labeled data across competitive levels no existing provider covers, the data asset compounds annually. ### 6. Democratized Recruiting and Visibility

The mandate makes recruiting democratic. Every game at every mandate institution is filmed and available on KTV. Every player has a profile with stats and film. A college coach anywhere can see a player at a small school in rural Oklahoma that they would never have traveled to scout. A parent in another country can watch their child's game live. A recruit at a school that never had film or stats now has both, visible to every coach on the platform.

This visibility benefits the athletes (more exposure), the schools (more recruiting interest means more institutional credibility), and the partner institution's intelligence system (the national player pool is now populated with data from mandate institutions that no other recruiting service covers). The mandate creates the dataset that makes the intelligence valuable. When commercial licensing opens in Year 3+, the intelligence licenses at institutional-IP tier precisely because the mandate-generated dataset is something no competing vendor can replicate. The mandate and the intelligence are symbiotic across the full strategic horizon.

### App Installs and Wallet Adoption

This is the highest-value conversion chain per mandate institution.

**Step 1: Game broadcast.** A parent, fan, or recruit watches a game on KTV. To watch, they download the app. This is an app install.

**Step 2: Wallet activation.** Inside the app, one tap creates a KPay wallet. The wallet is presented naturally alongside the viewing experience (buy a ticket, purchase merch, donate to the program). The conversion from app install to wallet activation depends on how immediately useful the wallet is.

**Step 3: Transaction.** The wallet holder makes a purchase (merch, ticket, donation, concession). This generates commerce revenue (5%) and creates a deposit in the banking system.

**Step 4: Retention.** The user returns for the next game. They receive a phone number (VoIP). They add to their wallet balance. They send money to a family member (remittance). They bring their church or business onto the platform. The user becomes a multi-function platform participant.

The moderate scenario (1.0M installs, 25% wallet activation, 40% transaction rate) produces approximately 250K wallet holders and 100K active transactors in Year 1. These numbers grow in subsequent years as the platform becomes more integrated, more features activate (VoIP, remittance, merch stores), and network effects compound.

## Camera Upgrade Revenue

The mandate deploys two free cameras per school. Schools wanting additional coverage or enhanced capabilities purchase through the upgrade ladder:

| Product | Price | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| Cam Sport+ | $500 | Additional venue coverage, enhanced quality |
| Cam Broadcast | $1,500 | Multi-camera broadcast production |
| Cam Pro | $3,000+ | Professional broadcast, conference-level production |

No competitor can match this pricing because KaNeXT does not depend on camera margin or subscription revenue. Competitors charge $949-$5,000+ for hardware plus $100-$225/month in mandatory subscriptions. KaNeXT charges lower hardware prices with zero subscription because the 5% commerce processing across the ecosystem funds the platform.

Estimated upgrade adoption: 10-20% of mandate institutions purchase at least one upgrade in Year 1. Across the 1,000+ institution footprint, that is 100-200+ additional camera sales generating $50,000-$300,000 in hardware revenue depending on product mix.

## Why the Mandate Economics Work

The mandate is economically viable because the cost per school is extremely low (under $2,000 all-in for hardware, onboarding, and first-year support) while each school generates recurring commerce revenue, advertising inventory, intelligence data, and wallet adoption that compounds over time.

The pitch to governing bodies is structurally compelling: every member school receives free cameras, free streaming, free merch stores, free player profiles and visibility tools, free advanced stats, free scheduling, and a free institutional operating platform. The governing body pays nothing. The schools pay nothing. KaNeXT monetizes through 5% flat commerce processing. Schools keep 95% of revenue that in many cases did not exist in any organized form before KaNeXT arrived. The deep evaluation intelligence is not commercialized to mandate institutions during the partner institution's Year 1-3 validation phase; full intelligence licensing opens to the mandate network in Year 3+ after visible competitive outcomes establish institutional-IP tier pricing power.

Common objections have structural answers. Cost: free. Hardware: provided. Broadcast rights: the 48-hour replay standard respects existing agreements. Vendor lock-in: uploads are camera-agnostic. Competitive imbalance: every school gets the same platform with no feature gating.
