# PRO SOFTBALL PLAYER KR LEGEND -- v1

## Scope
Universal KR for professional softball players worldwide on a 0-100 scale. Covers Athletes Unlimited, WPF, Japan Softball League, Australian Softball League, Italian Softball League, Mexican Softball League, and all professional circuits including Olympic/national team competition.

## KEY CONCEPT: ENTRY vs PEAK (Softball-Specific)

Softball's college-to-pro pipeline is fundamentally different from baseball:

- **Entry KR** = Day 1 as a professional. This is the player's IMMEDIATE professional starting point. There is no minor league system in softball. Players go directly from college to professional competition.
- **Peak KR** = Best realistic professional outcome after 2-3 years of pro experience. What the player becomes when fully developed at the professional level.
- **Current KR** = Present-day evaluation of an active professional. Used for roster/selection/trade decisions.

Entry KR correlates more closely with college KR than in baseball because:
1. Same field dimensions (60-foot bases, 43-foot pitching distance)
2. Same ball size (12 inch)
3. Same pitching style (underhand)
4. No equipment transition (composite bats at all levels)
5. Adjustment is primarily COMPETITION QUALITY, not physical/equipment changes

Typical college-to-pro adjustment: -3 to -8 KR points from college Final KR (D1 Power). Smaller adjustment than baseball because fewer physical variables change.

## PROFESSIONAL SOFTBALL MARKET CONTEXT

The professional softball market is dramatically smaller than MLB, NBA, or NFL:
- Total domestic professional roster spots: approximately 100-150
- Average professional salary: significantly below livable wage for most players
- Athletes Unlimited top earners: $30K-$60K for a 5-week season
- Japan Softball League: highest international pay, $50K-$100K+ for top imports
- Career length: most professional careers are 2-5 years
- Many elite college players never play professionally because the financial model does not support it
- The Olympic pathway is the highest-profile professional opportunity

This means KR tiers below do NOT map to salary ranges the way baseball/basketball pro legends do. Professional softball value is measured in selection priority, playing time, team impact, and national team consideration - not contract dollars.

## PRO PLAYER KR TIERS -- HITTER TRACK

### 96-100 -- Generational / Olympic-Caliber Hitter
**Role:** Best hitter in professional softball. USA Softball national team lock. Olympic starter. Changes every game she plays in.
**Production:** .350+/.450+/.650+, 15+ HR in a pro season, elite in every offensive dimension. Dominates Athletes Unlimited leaderboards or JSL MVP.
**Selection:** First pick in any format. National team automatic selection.
**Historical context:** 2-3 players per generation reach this tier. Think all-time NCAA record-holders who sustain production at the pro level.

### 92-95 -- Elite Professional / National Team Core
**Role:** Perennial All-Star equivalent. USA Softball national team roster. Top-5 professional hitter in any league she plays in.
**Production:** .320+/.420+/.580+, 10-15 HR, elite in 3+ component KRs.
**Selection:** Top-10 pick in Athletes Unlimited. Starting lineup in any international competition.
**Olympic context:** National team roster player. Likely starter.

### 88-91 -- All-Pro Caliber Hitter
**Role:** Core hitter on any professional roster. National team pool player (top 30-40 consideration).
**Production:** .290+/.380+/.500+, 8-12 HR, above-average in most components.
**Selection:** Top-20 pick in Athletes Unlimited. Lineup regular in any league.

### 84-87 -- Quality Professional Regular
**Role:** Everyday starter in any professional league. Solid contributor.
**Production:** .270+/.350+/.430+, 5-8 HR, reliable across components.
**Selection:** Mid-round in Athletes Unlimited. Roster lock in WPF or international leagues.

### 80-83 -- Professional Starter / Role Player
**Role:** Starting lineup player or strong platoon. Contributes value but not a centerpiece.
**Production:** .250+/.320+/.380+, 2-5 HR.
**Selection:** Late-round in Athletes Unlimited. Roster player in WPF. Starter in international leagues.

### 76-79 -- Professional Fringe / Roster Bubble
**Role:** Last few roster spots. May cycle in and out of professional rosters. Specialist value (elite defense, speed weapon, pinch-hit role).
**Production:** .230-.250/.300-.320/.340-.380.
**Selection:** Replacement-level in Athletes Unlimited. Roster bubble in WPF. Regular in lower-tier international leagues.

### 72-75 -- Replacement Level
**Role:** Available for roster fill. Not a first-choice professional. May play in lower-tier international leagues (Australia, Italy, Mexico).
**Production:** Below professional average but functional.

### Below 72 -- Below Professional Level
Not viable at the professional level. College-level production only.

## PRO PLAYER KR TIERS -- PITCHER TRACK

### 96-100 -- Generational / Olympic-Caliber Arm
**Role:** Best pitcher in professional softball. USA Softball national team ace. Olympic Game 1 starter. The most valuable player in the sport.
**Production:** sub-1.00 ERA, 10.0+ K/7, sub-0.60 WHIP, 150+ IP in a pro season with dominance throughout. Can pitch every game of an international tournament and still be the best pitcher on the field.
**Selection:** #1 overall pick in any format. National team automatic.
**Historical context:** Once-a-decade arm. The ace who carries a national team to an Olympic medal.

### 92-95 -- Elite Professional / National Team Arm
**Role:** Top-3 pitcher in any professional league. National team roster. Can carry a team through a tournament weekend.
**Production:** 1.00-1.50 ERA, 9.0-10.0 K/7, sub-0.80 WHIP.
**Selection:** Top-5 pick in Athletes Unlimited. National team co-ace.

### 88-91 -- All-Pro Caliber Arm
**Role:** Front-of-rotation professional. National team pool (top 30-40 consideration). Can ace any Athletes Unlimited or international team.
**Production:** 1.50-2.20 ERA, 8.0-9.0 K/7, 0.80-1.00 WHIP.
**Selection:** Top-15 pick in Athletes Unlimited. Go-to arm for any professional roster.

### 84-87 -- Quality Professional Pitcher
**Role:** Reliable professional arm. Consistent starter. Can be the primary pitcher for a mid-tier professional team.
**Production:** 2.20-3.00 ERA, 7.0-8.0 K/7, 1.00-1.15 WHIP.
**Selection:** Mid-round in Athletes Unlimited. Rotation lock in WPF and international.

### 80-83 -- Professional Starter / Secondary Arm
**Role:** Solid professional contributor. Starter or strong relief. Complements an ace on a good team.
**Production:** 3.00-3.80 ERA, 6.0-7.0 K/7, 1.15-1.30 WHIP.
**Selection:** Roster player in Athletes Unlimited. Starter in WPF and international leagues.

### 76-79 -- Professional Fringe / Specialist
**Role:** Roster bubble. Provides contrasting look from primary pitcher. Spot-start and relief role.
**Production:** 3.80-4.50 ERA. Functional but not dominant.
**Selection:** Replacement-level in domestic leagues. Starter in lower-tier international leagues.

### 72-75 -- Replacement Level
**Role:** Available for roster fill in international leagues. Not a first-choice domestic professional.

### Below 72 -- Below Professional Level
Not viable at the professional level.

## OLYMPIC / NATIONAL TEAM KR THRESHOLDS

The Olympic pathway is the highest-profile opportunity in professional softball. These are approximate KR thresholds for USA Softball national team consideration:

| Selection Level | Approximate KR | Context |
|----------------|---------------|---------|
| Olympic roster lock (top 15) | >= 92 | Automatic selection barring injury |
| Olympic roster contender (top 25) | 88-91 | Competing for final spots |
| National team pool (top 40) | 84-87 | Invited to camps and selection events |
| National team radar | 80-83 | Monitored but not actively selected |

Other national teams (Japan, Australia, Canada, Mexico, etc.) have lower KR thresholds due to smaller talent pools, but the competition structure is similar.
