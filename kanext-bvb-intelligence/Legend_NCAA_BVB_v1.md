# Legend: NCAA Women's Beach Volleyball (National Collegiate) v1

---

## SCOPE

This legend covers all NCAA women's beach volleyball competition. Beach volleyball uses a National Collegiate Championship format - there is no separate D1/D2/D3 championship. All divisions compete together under one championship umbrella. However, program tiers vary significantly in recruiting investment, coaching infrastructure, and competitive depth.

This legend is organized by program tier (Top, Mid-Tier, Developing) because the competitive context differs even within the same championship structure.

All production benchmarks are per-match rates unless noted. Beach volleyball matches are best of 3 sets (sets to 21/15).

---

## CROSS-TIER INTERPRETATION NOTES

- A KR 88 player at a developing program is NOT the same as a KR 88 player at a top program. The KR is the same universal number, but the Level Tier Map shows the interpretation difference.
- KLVN lambda normalization accounts for competitive level: top program lambda (0.820) vs mid-tier (0.720) vs developing (0.620). The same raw stats produce different trait scores after normalization.
- Transfer portal evaluation: when a player transfers between tiers, evaluate her using the new program's legend for fit assessment, but her KR does not change (KR is universal).
- Indoor overlap: many players at developing and mid-tier programs are primarily indoor players who also compete in beach. Their beach-specific development may lag behind dedicated beach recruits.
