# Enrollment Engine Brief

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

---

## Overview

The enrollment engine is the conversion layer between platform activity, athletic visibility, and tuition revenue. It is the function that takes the awareness KaNeXT creates (athletics, media, diaspora, intelligence-identified prospects) and converts it into enrolled students paying tuition.

Year 1 operates under the Athletics-Only Agreement. Enrollment engine scope is athletic recruitment (finding and converting the athletes who fill the 12-team Varsity and Development roster structure) plus the institutional enrollment channels directly activated by athletic visibility. Year 2 activates Full IOA and expands the enrollment engine to institution-wide operations covering residential and online enrollment across the partner institution's program portfolio.

Year 1 enrollment infrastructure is built within KaNeXT Core Operations. Professional enrollment staff, CRM platform, digital marketing infrastructure, and intelligence-driven lead scoring are deployed at Year 1 scale. An enrollment company acquisition is a Year 2+ strategic capital event to accelerate capacity, team scale, and call center operations for the institution-wide enrollment expansion described in the revenue model.

---

## Why This Matters: Comparable Institutional Growth

Grand Canyon University grew from fewer than 1,000 on-campus students in 2008 to approximately 25,000 on-campus and 98,000 online by 2024. Total enrollment exceeds 125,000 after $1.8B in campus investment over 16 years.

Liberty University grew to over 140,000 students by 2024, including approximately 16,000 residential and 124,000 online. Annual revenue reached $1.2B with $3.5B in net assets.

High Point University grew from 1,450 students in 2005 to 6,335 on-campus by 2024 with over $3B in total campus investment.

These institutions achieved this growth with none of the structural advantages KaNeXT provides. No intelligence system identifying prospects. No KTV generating millions of views. No VoIP funneling diaspora families into the app. No mandate creating platform awareness across hundreds of schools. No owned media surface. No integrated enrollment-to-tuition-to-wallet pipeline. No multi-sport athletic system producing hundreds of games per year on a nationally visible platform.

They grew through enrollment marketing, campus investment, and institutional brand building. The partner institution will have all of those traditional growth drivers plus an integrated institutional operating system that may meaningfully improve conversion economics and reduce customer acquisition costs relative to traditional enrollment marketing.

## Why an Enrollment Engine Is Required

The revenue model projects total enrollment growing from 2,000-2,200 in Year 1 to 24,600-27,700 by Year 5. Online enrollment grows from 400-500 in Year 1 to 11,800-13,500 by Year 5. Residential enrollment grows from 1,500-1,700 in Year 1 to 12,800-14,200 by Year 5. Neither trajectory is achievable through passive admissions. Both require a professional enrollment operation converting the awareness and demand that the rest of the system generates.

The partner institution currently operates at approximately 1,100 enrollment. The institution has existing enrollment operations tied to its ABHE-accredited programs but lacks the marketing infrastructure, call center scale, digital lead generation, and conversion pipeline required to support multi-year enrollment scaling at the trajectory the partnership projects. The enrollment engine creates this capability within the partnership while respecting the partner institution's institutional identity and mission.

## The Structural Advantages

**The awareness already exists.** The enrollment engine does not build awareness from scratch. It converts awareness that the rest of the ecosystem is already generating. The partner institution's 12 Varsity and Development teams produce 250-350+ games per year on KTV visible to every user on the platform. D1 guarantee games against Division I opponents generate national media exposure. The philanthropic commitment and campus transformation generate national press. Mandate institution games (tens of thousands annually across GAAC, NAIA, USCAA, NCCAA member schools) create platform-wide content that surfaces the partner institution brand. Diaspora families connected through VoIP and remittance are already inside the app. The enrollment engine's primary job is not advertising. It is converting the demand that every other part of the system produces.

**Intelligence-driven targeting.** The enrollment engine has access to data no typical enrollment company has. The intelligence system identifies athletes in the national player pool across every mandate institution, each one a potential recruit for the partner institution. The platform tracks which families are on KTV, which diaspora communities are connected through VoIP, which prospects have engaged with the partner institution content, and which users have wallets and financial activity indicating institutional affinity. The CRM does not start with a purchased lead list. It starts with a growing ecosystem audience. The Enrollment Intelligence architecture (RKR Retention, PKR Persistence, PLKR Pipeline, RCKR Recovery) governs lead scoring, persistence modeling, and pipeline management across the funnel.

**Institutional integration.** A prospect who fills out an interest form can immediately be contacted through KaNeXT messaging, scheduled through Dipson, shown a campus tour on KTV, connected with a current student through the app, and processed for financial aid through KPay, all without leaving the ecosystem. The enrollment funnel is not a separate website feeding a separate CRM. It is a native function of the platform the prospect is already using.

**Dipson assists at every stage.** Prospects can ask Dipson questions about programs, campus life, financial aid, and application requirements at any hour. Dipson responds within the authority scope appropriate for prospective students. Admissions counselors use Dipson to pull prospect data, generate personalized outreach, and manage pipeline workflows.

**The evangelical Christian identity.** the partner institution's identity as an ABHE-accredited evangelical Christian institution is marketable to distinct audiences: Christian-identifying families seeking faith-integrated higher education, international students from Christian communities worldwide (the partner institution already serves 424 international students from 43 countries), working professionals seeking values-aligned online education, and pastors and ministry leaders pursuing theological and leadership credentials. These are large, underserved audiences with strong institutional loyalty when the right school reaches them.

## How the Enrollment Engine Works

**Digital marketing** drives awareness where the ecosystem has not already created it: targeted advertising, search engine optimization, content marketing, and social media campaigns positioning the partner institution to prospective students nationally and internationally. The digital marketing function scales significantly under Full IOA activation in Year 2.

**The call center** converts leads. Admissions counselors contact prospects, answer questions, guide applications, and close enrollments. A prospective student who engages with the platform, whether through a game on KTV, a social media ad, a referral from a mandate institution coach, or a family connection through VoIP, receives follow-up measured in hours rather than traditional institutional timelines.

**The CRM** manages the pipeline. Every prospect is tracked from first touch through enrollment. Lead source attribution shows which channels produce enrolled students, not just clicks. The intelligence system scores prospects on institutional fit, academic readiness, and engagement level, prioritizing counselor time on the highest-probability conversions.

**Year 1 scope:** athletic enrollment (180-220 athletes across 12 teams), general institutional enrollment driven by platform visibility, and pipeline buildout for Year 2 scale expansion. Year 2 scope: full institution-wide enrollment operations across residential and online programs under the Full IOA structure.

## The Scale Opportunity

GCU, Liberty, and High Point demonstrated that aggressive enrollment marketing combined with campus investment and institutional brand building can produce massive growth at private universities. Each of those schools started with less capital, less technology, and less structural advantage than the partner institution will have under the partnership.

The partner institution's Year 5 projection of 24,600-27,700 total enrollment (12,800-14,200 residential plus 11,800-13,500 online) represents a fraction of the scale GCU and Liberty achieved over longer time horizons with less initial capital and fewer integrated growth engines. The projection is aggressive but grounded in demonstrated institutional growth precedents and KaNeXT's integrated operating model. Actual results depend on execution, program approvals, faculty scaling, conversion rates, ABHE compliance through the Full IOA substantive change process, and market conditions.

## Enrollment Company Acquisition (Year 2+ Capital Event)

The enrollment company acquisition is a Year 2+ capital event raised separately from the Year 1 partnership funding. The acquisition target is a higher education enrollment marketing firm providing 20-40 employees, an established call center operation, CRM and digital marketing infrastructure, admissions counselor teams, and lead generation and conversion pipelines.

Post-acquisition, the operation is dedicated to the partner institution and KaNeXT exclusively. Every counselor hour, every CRM workflow, every marketing dollar is directed at the partner institution enrollment and KaNeXT institutional growth. No competing clients. No split attention. Acquisition compresses years of enrollment infrastructure buildout, team recruitment, and operational refinement into one move while providing an immediately functional operation for the Year 2-5 enrollment scale expansion.

The acquisition is sized and funded against Year 1 the partner institution execution and the scale expansion triggered by Full IOA activation. Budget and structure are determined at the time of the capital event.

## Strategic Value

The enrollment engine is the conversion layer between platform activity and tuition revenue. KTV creates visibility. VoIP creates diaspora connections. Athletics creates brand awareness. The intelligence system identifies prospects. The enrollment engine converts all of it into enrolled students paying tuition through KPay.

Without the enrollment engine, the awareness generated by the rest of the system dissipates. With it, every KTV viewer, every VoIP user, every mandate institution parent, and every athlete in the national database is a prospect in a governed pipeline.
