# KaNeXT Revenue Model: OS Platform

**KaNeXT LLC | Confidential**

---

## Purpose

This document presents the platform revenue model for KaNeXT LLC. Platform revenue is generated by the infrastructure layers of the KaNeXT OS across all three institutional tiers: school network, governing body mandate, and the broader platform user base.

Platform revenue is excluded from the institutional base case (covered in the Institutional Revenue Model) and from intelligence licensing (covered in the Intelligence Revenue Model). This model captures the financial activity generated by the operating system itself: payments, commerce, media, communications, fulfillment, and institutional licensing.

All projections are directional estimates. Actual results depend on execution, user adoption, regulatory approvals, and market conditions.

---

## Revenue Streams

### 1. KPay Commerce Processing (5%)

Every transaction across every mandate institution and network institution flows through KPay's governed settlement chain. KaNeXT charges 5% flat processing on all commerce: tickets, merchandise, concessions, donations, event fees, and ancillary transactions.

At mandate institutions, this revenue is created from nothing. These schools had no digital ticketing, no online merch stores, and no governed commerce before KaNeXT. The 5% processing fee is on revenue that did not previously exist.

At network schools (IOA institutions), non-tuition commerce also flows through KPay. Athletic revenue, campus commerce, dining, parking, and events all settle through the governed chain.

**Drivers:** Number of institutions on the platform, average commerce volume per institution, wallet adoption rate (closed-loop wallet transactions eliminate card network fees and increase margin).

### 2. Wallet Float Income

Every KPay wallet balance is a deposit. The bank earns the spread between what it pays wallet users (target competitive APY, dependent on banking partner and charter status) and what it earns on aggregate deposits.

Deposit sources: student accounts, institutional operating accounts, remittance float, fund capital under management, philanthropic capital in institutional accounts, CRA-motivated corporate deposits from major banks seeking MDI credit.

**Drivers:** Total wallet users, average balance per user, deposit growth from mandate and network school adoption, spread between deposit yield and APY paid.

### 3. Remittance

International money transfer through KPay at competitive rates targeting a fraction of the global industry average (over 6%). The diaspora play through VoIP creates the international funnel. A student calls home for free. The parent downloads the app. The parent sends money home through KPay instead of Western Union.

**Drivers:** International user base, remittance volume per user, fee per transaction, corridor-specific pricing.

### 4. Interchange Income

Every KPay card transaction outside the KaNeXT ecosystem (Apple Pay, Google Pay, physical card) generates 1-2% interchange revenue for the bank. This scales with wallet adoption across all institutional layers.

**Drivers:** Card-active wallet users, average external spend per user.

### 5. KTV Advertising

Every person watching content on KTV is watching on a KaNeXT-owned media surface. KaNeXT controls the ad inventory. Ad opportunities: pre-roll on game replays, commercial insertions at natural breaks, sponsored team and school pages, in-app promotion, and partner advertising targeted by sport, region, level, and interest.

**Drivers:** Total viewing hours, ad fill rate, CPM rates, advertiser demand (growing with mandate footprint).

### 6. Bank Lending

The bank originates loans to borrowers already operating inside the KaNeXT ecosystem with underwriting data unavailable to traditional lenders. Lending categories: private student loans at network institutions, SBA-guaranteed loans to small businesses in the ecosystem, commercial lending to campus vendors and service providers, real estate lending for cluster housing near campus, and personal lending to platform users with established histories.

**Drivers:** Deposit base (determines lending capacity), loan demand, credit quality of borrowers, regulatory capital requirements.

### 7. Bank Treasury and Institutional Services

Network institutions require cash management, payroll processing, accounts payable, vendor payment systems, and financial reporting. The bank provides these as fee-based services.

**Drivers:** Number of network institutions, service fees per institution.

### 8. VoIP Recurring Revenue

The acquired VoIP company has an existing customer base producing $3.2M-$4.4M in annual recurring revenue. This revenue continues post-acquisition while KaNeXT scales the user base through mandate and network school deployment.

**Drivers:** Existing ARR, retention rate, new institutional deployments.

### 9. Merch Fulfillment Margin

The fulfillment center produces merchandise for every mandate institution, network institution, athlete NIL store, brand store across all modes, and KaNeXT-owned events. KaNeXT earns production margin on every unit plus 5% commerce processing on every transaction.

Illustrative unit economics: a $45 hoodie produces approximately $6 production margin plus $2.25 commerce processing. Tier 3 premium products carry higher margins.

**Drivers:** Total storefronts on the platform, average order volume per storefront, product mix (Tier 1/2/3), per-unit cost improvements at scale.

### 10. OS Licensing to Non-Mandate Institutions

KaNeXT OS licensed as a standalone institutional operating system to schools, K-12 systems, community and faith institutions, and businesses that are not mandate institutions and not under IOAs. These customers get the OS (identity, communication, financial settlement, media, scheduling, operations) without the intelligence layer or the athletic system.

**Pricing model:** Annual license per institution. Tiered by size and feature access. Estimated range: $10,000-$100,000 per institution per year depending on scale and mode.

**Addressable market:** Thousands of churches, private schools, community organizations, and small businesses seeking an integrated institutional operating system.

### 11. Camera Sales

Camera hardware sold to programs and institutions outside the mandate at retail pricing (Cam Sport $200, Sport+ $500, Broadcast $1,500, Pro $3,000+). KaNeXT earns hardware margin on every unit sold.

**Drivers:** Demand from non-mandate programs wanting KVision processing or KTV streaming capability.

## Margin Profile

Platform revenue has a mixed margin profile. Commerce processing and advertising are high-margin (70-90%). Bank revenue depends on deposit cost, credit quality, and regulatory capital. VoIP is moderate-margin with existing infrastructure. Fulfillment has lower margins on Tier 1 products but improves with scale and Tier 3 mix. Camera sales carry hardware margin dependent on COGS management.

Blended estimated gross margin on platform revenue: 50-70%, improving as wallet adoption shifts transactions from card-based (higher cost) to closed-loop (lower cost).

---

## Strategic Notes

Platform revenue is excluded from the institutional base case. It represents additional upside beyond the institutional base case and accelerates the path to operating profitability.

The platform revenue model is driven by institutional deployment, not consumer marketing. Every mandate institution that deploys creates commerce volume, viewing hours, wallet adoption, and deposit growth without KaNeXT spending consumer acquisition dollars. The mandate is the distribution engine for platform revenue.

The bank charter is the inflection point for several streams (wallet float, lending, interchange, treasury services). Pre-charter, these streams operate through white-label partnerships at lower margins. Post-charter, margins expand as KaNeXT captures the full economics on owned rails.
