## D2 EVALUATION CONTEXT

**D2 women's golf has 5.4 equivalency scholarships.** This is slightly fewer than D1's 6 but still allows meaningful scholarship support for most lineup players.

**D2 field strength is more variable than D1.** Some D2 conferences (RMAC, Peach Belt, Sunshine State) are quite strong, while others are significantly weaker. Strength of schedule matters more for D2 evaluation than D1 because the variance is larger.

**D2 scoring averages are typically 2-4 strokes higher than D1 Power 4.** A 75.0 scoring average at D2 represents a stronger relative performance than a 75.0 at D1 Power 4 because course setups, field quality, and coaching resources differ.

**Transfer portal movement from D2 to D1 is common in women's golf.** Players who dominate at D2 (scoring average under 74.0, 70%+ GIR) are strong candidates to transfer up to D1 mid-major or low-major programs.

**D2 National Championship is stroke play (72 holes).** No match play component. This favors consistency (high MKR, low scoring variance) over explosiveness.
