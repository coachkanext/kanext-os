# SCOUTING CONFIDENCE GATES

## Data Tier Reference

| Tier | Definition |
|---|---|
| V1 - Stats-Only | Public box scores, roster, season stats. No play-type data. |
| V1+ - Enhanced | V1 + additional context (coach quotes, highlight film, athletic testing). |
| V2 - Film (1 Season) | Owned film data + full play-type tagging + clip library. |
| V3 - Film Deep | Multi-season film + full archive + trend analysis. |

Note: Most flag football scouting will operate at V1 or V1+ for the foreseeable future. Film infrastructure is minimal across women's flag football programs. V2 and V3 are aspirational tiers.

## Scout Confidence Gate (Pregame)

| Data Available (Opponent) | Scout Confidence % |
|---|---|
| V1 stats-only: season stats + roster | 40-55% |
| V1 stats-only + multi-game (returning core) | 48-62% |
| V1+ enhanced: stats + coach intel + highlight clips | 55-70% |
| V1+ multi-year | 60-75% |
| V2 film (1 season): 3+ games processed | 70-82% |
| V2 film high coverage: 6+ games processed + stable personnel | 75-88% |
| V3 film deep: multi-season + full archive | 80-92% |

Note: All flag football confidence ranges are 8-12 points lower than tackle football equivalents due to the emerging sport's data limitations, smaller sample sizes, and less structured statistical infrastructure.

## Scout Confidence Adjusters

- Sample size: fewer than 4 current-season games: bottom of range
- First-year program: -10 pts (no historical data, coaching patterns unknown)
- Recency: last 2 games show clear shift (lineup change, system change, key player injury/return): downshift 5-8 pts
- Roster volatility: significant roster additions mid-season: downshift 5-8 pts
- System ambiguity: OSIE/DSIE still provisional: downshift 5-8 pts
- Prior matchup: played this opponent earlier this season: upshift 5-8 pts
- NAIA established program (3+ years): upshift 3-5 pts (deeper pattern data)
- High stability: locked system + stable roster: upshift to top of range

# GAME OPS - 4-PHASE FLOW

## PHASE 1: PREGAME SCOUT PACKET

### Purpose
Deliver a complete opponent scouting report to coaching staff before game week begins. This is the foundation for game planning.

### MUST OUTPUT (every Pregame Scout Packet):

**1.1 Opponent Offensive Tendencies**
- Offensive system identification (from OSIE) with confidence %
- Pass/Run ratio (season, last 3 games)
- Formation frequency breakdown (spread, trips, bunch, motion, etc.)
- Motion frequency and type (pre-snap motion %)
- Tempo (plays per minute, hurry-up frequency)
- QB rushing frequency (designed runs vs scrambles)
- Red zone tendencies (passing concepts, conversion rate)
- Third/Fourth-down tendencies by distance
- Two-minute offense approach
- Favorite plays / concepts identified (top-3 called plays by frequency if available)
- First-play-of-drive tendencies
- Conversion attempt tendencies (1-point vs 2-point, by game situation)

**1.2 Opponent Defensive Tendencies**
- Defensive system identification (from DSIE) with confidence %
- Coverage shell tendencies (man%, zone%, hybrid%)
- Rush frequency (% of plays with a designated rusher)
- Double rush frequency and situations
- Spy usage (% of plays, against which QB types)
- Coverage preferences by formation (how does defense align vs spread, trips, bunch?)
- Blitz/rush success rate
- Turnover creation patterns (INTs from which coverage? Sacks from which rush look?)
- Flag pull reliability (team-level flag pull %)

**1.3 Key Personnel**
- Opponent QB profile: archetype, KR (if evaluated), key tendencies, scramble threat level, hot routes
- Opponent WR1 profile: speed, route tree, favorite routes, YAC ability
- Opponent primary defensive playmaker: coverage skills, ball hawk tendency, rush ability
- Opponent two-way players: which players play both sides? When do they rotate?
- Injury/availability notes

**1.4 Scheme Matchup Analysis**
- Our offensive system vs their defensive system (from Interaction Library)
- Their offensive system vs our defensive system (from Interaction Library)
- Key archetype matchups (WR vs CB speed, QB vs spy/rusher, etc.)
- Speed mismatch assessment (AKR differentials at key positions)
- Predicted advantage: offense or defense, and why

**1.5 Game Plan Recommendations**
- Top-3 offensive concepts to exploit matchup advantages
- Top-3 defensive adjustments to counter opponent's strengths
- Key player to stop (opponent's highest-impact player and how to limit her)
- Risk assessment: what can go wrong, and what's the contingency

## PHASE 2: IN-GAME LIVE OPS

### Purpose
Real-time intelligence during the game. Quick-reference adjustments based on what's actually happening.

### Live Tracking (Staff Records During Game):
- Offensive play log: formation, play type (pass/run), result, yards gained, who touched the ball
- Defensive play log: coverage type, rush/no rush, result, flag pull success/miss, turnovers
- QB tracking: completions/attempts, rushing attempts, sacks taken, INTs
- Conversion tracking: 1-pt vs 2-pt attempts and results
- Timeout usage
- Momentum indicators: consecutive scoring drives, turnover streaks, flag pull misses

### In-Game Alerts (Nexus generates when patterns emerge):

**Offensive Alerts:**
- "Opponent is in [man/zone] coverage on [X]% of plays. Our [play concept] should exploit this."
- "Opponent's CB on our WR1 has been beaten on [X] of [Y] routes. Attack that matchup."
- "Opponent's rush is arriving in [X] seconds. Our QB release time is [Y]. Adjust to quicker routes."
- "Opponent has not used double rush. Consider taking deeper shots."
- "Opponent shifts to [coverage] when we motion. Use motion to identify and exploit."

**Defensive Alerts:**
- "Opponent QB is [X] for [Y] passing. Completion % is [Z]. She's [hot/cold]."
- "Opponent's favorite route is [curl/out/slant]. Jump it."
- "Opponent QB has rushed [X] times for [Y] yards. Deploy spy."
- "Our flag pull rate this game is [X]%. Focus on technique."
- "Opponent is converting [X]% on 4th down. They're aggressive. Anticipate go-for-it."

## PHASE 3: HALFTIME STAFF PACKET

### Purpose
Structured halftime analysis for coaching staff. 5-minute delivery window.

### MUST OUTPUT:

**3.1 First-Half Statistical Summary**
- Score
- Total plays / Total yards (both teams)
- Pass/Run breakdown
- Turnovers
- Flag pull rate (both teams)
- Conversion rate (4th downs, 1-pt/2-pt attempts)
- Time of possession

**3.2 What's Working**
- Our top-2 offensive concepts by success rate
- Our defensive coverage that's been most effective
- Key matchup we're winning and should continue to exploit

**3.3 What's Not Working**
- Our bottom-2 offensive concepts by success rate
- Defensive coverage/alignment that's being exploited
- Key matchup we're losing and how to adjust

**3.4 Halftime Adjustments (Prioritized)**
1. Highest-leverage adjustment: The single change most likely to swing the game
2. Secondary adjustment: Supporting change
3. Personnel adjustment: Any substitution or role change recommended
4. Conversion strategy update: Should we change 1-pt vs 2-pt approach based on game flow?

**3.5 Second-Half Keys**
- 3 specific things we must do to win the second half
- Clock management reminders (if close game)
- Momentum assessment: who has it, and what changes it

## PHASE 4: POSTGAME STAFF PACKET

### Purpose
Comprehensive postgame analysis for coaching staff and player development.

### MUST OUTPUT:

**4.1 Final Statistical Summary**
- Final score
- Complete box score (both teams)
- Drive-by-drive log with outcomes
- Conversion summary (4th down, 1-pt/2-pt)
- Turnover summary with context
- Flag pull rate (both teams, individual if tracked)

**4.2 Game Grade: Offense**
- QB grade (comp%, TD:INT, rushing, decision-making)
- WR grades (catch rate, YPR, drops, YAC)
- Center grade (snap reliability, receiving production)
- RB grade (rushing efficiency, receiving)
- Offensive system execution grade (did we run our system effectively?)
- Conversion execution grade

**4.3 Game Grade: Defense**
- Coverage grade (completion % allowed, big plays allowed)
- Rush grade (sack rate, pressure on QB)
- Flag pull grade (pull %, misses, critical misses)
- Turnover creation grade
- Defensive system execution grade

**4.4 Key Moments Analysis**
- Identify the 3-5 plays that most determined the outcome
- For each: what happened, what should have happened, what's the teaching point

**4.5 Player Development Notes**
- Per player: 1-2 specific areas to improve based on this game
- Highlight standout performers
- Flag players who need increased reps in practice on specific skills

**4.6 Scouting Update**
- Update opponent profile with new data from this game
- Adjust OSIE/DSIE confidence if new tendencies emerged
- Flag any opponent tendencies that diverged from pregame scout

**4.7 Next Opponent Preview (if available)**
- Quick look at next opponent: system, key players, initial matchup thoughts
- What from this game's performance is relevant for next week's preparation

# FLAG FOOTBALL-SPECIFIC GAME OPS NOTES

## No Kicking Game
There is no punting, no field goals, no kickoffs in flag football. This eliminates entire sections of traditional game ops. All scoring is touchdowns and conversions. Fourth-down decisions are always go-for-it or turnover on downs.

## Conversion Strategy
The 1-point vs 2-point conversion decision is one of the most important strategic choices in flag football. From the 3-yard line (1 point) or 10-yard line (2 points), this is a pure matchup/risk decision.

General framework:
- When trailing by 7+: go for 2 to close the gap faster
- When leading by 1-6: go for 1 to maintain lead
- When tied: game situation dependent (time remaining, momentum, matchup confidence)
- When your offense is dominant: go for 2 more often (higher conversion rate)
- When defense is dominant: go for 1 (protect the lead, trust defense to hold)

## Two-Way Player Management
Managing two-way players is a critical game ops function. Players who play both sides need monitoring for:
- Fatigue indicators (declining speed, missed flag pulls, dropped passes late in games)
- Mental errors that increase with fatigue (wrong coverage, wrong route)
- Strategic rest: when to give two-way players a play off on one side

## Timeout Usage
Flag football timeouts are precious. Typical allotment is 2-3 per half. Use for:
- Stopping a momentum run by the opponent
- Setting up a critical 4th-down play call
- Rest for two-way players in late-game scenarios
- Clock management in final minutes (stop clock when trailing)
